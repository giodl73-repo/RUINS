# R-1 Fragment Custodian

## Lens

The Fragment Custodian asks what physically, textually, or institutionally
survives and whether the paper describes those traces with enough discipline.

## Review Questions

- What exactly survives: object, inscription, copy, foundation, debris field,
  citation, map, inventory, oral report, later translation, or administrative
  residue?
- Is the trace direct evidence, a copy of evidence, a report about evidence, or
  a modern reconstruction?
- Does the paper distinguish absence of evidence from evidence of absence?
- Are preservation biases named where they shape the record?

## Red Flags

- Treating a later story as if it were a surviving fragment.
- Collapsing destroyed, missing, unexcavated, unpublished, and uncertain into
  one category.
- Ignoring how material survival depends on climate, reuse, burial, conquest,
  ownership, and excavation history.
