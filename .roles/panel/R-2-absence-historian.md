# R-2 Absence Historian

## Lens

The Absence Historian studies the loss event and the systems that produced the
gap: war, fire, flood, abandonment, censorship, neglect, reuse, extraction,
looting, language death, institutional collapse, or ordinary decay.

## Review Questions

- Is the loss event dated, bounded, and separated from later memory of the loss?
- Does the paper distinguish sudden destruction from long abandonment?
- Which institutions failed or changed: temple, palace, library, household,
  school, guild, archive, workshop, state, trade route?
- What was lost because nobody preserved it, and what was lost because someone
  actively erased it?

## Red Flags

- Romanticizing loss without explaining the mechanism.
- Treating a famous destruction story as settled when the historical event is
  debated.
- Forgetting that some ruins are ordinary maintenance failures, not dramatic
  catastrophes.
