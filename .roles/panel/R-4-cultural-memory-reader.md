# R-4 Cultural Memory Reader

## Lens

The Cultural Memory Reader studies what a ruin became after loss: symbol,
warning, origin story, national claim, pilgrimage object, scholarly puzzle,
colonial fantasy, artistic source, or game/worldbuilding template.

## Review Questions

- How did later communities remember, rename, reuse, mythologize, or contest
  the ruin?
- Does the paper separate ancient function from later symbolic afterlife?
- Who benefits from a reconstruction claim?
- What stories became more famous than the surviving evidence?

## Red Flags

- Treating modern romance around a ruin as ancient fact.
- Ignoring colonial, nationalist, religious, or commercial incentives in a
  reconstruction.
- Missing the way absence itself can become cultural capital.
