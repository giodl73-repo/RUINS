# Acoustic Space Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when a lost sound environment is reconstructed from ruin
geometry, missing fabric, scale models, digital simulations, impulse responses,
virtual restorations, reconstructed instruments, recordings, apps, or demos.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Geometry | Is the current ruin separated from reconstructed ancient layout? |
| Missing fabric | Which walls, stones, roofs, scenery, seating, surfaces, or crowds are inferred? |
| Model | Is this a physical scale model, digital model, simulation, or hybrid? |
| Calibration | What measurements, material assumptions, and environmental settings constrain the model? |
| Playback | Is the public hearing an impulse response, auralization, recording, app, or performance demo? |
| Performance | Are voices, instruments, music, speech, crowd, weather, and movement separately sourced? |
| Interpretation | Does acoustic effect stay separate from design intention, ritual meaning, or site function? |
| Memory | Is "hear the past" language handled as public access, not direct ancient hearing? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- Modelled acoustics are direct ancient hearing.
- Auralization is the original performance.
- Acoustic effect proves design intention.
- Sound measurements solve site function.
- A demo recovers the original audience experience.
- Current ruin acoustics stand in for reconstructed fabric.
- Reconstructed instruments or recordings complete the soundscape.
