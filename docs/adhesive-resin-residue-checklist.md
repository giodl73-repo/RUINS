# Adhesive/Resin Residue Checklist

Use this checklist when bitumen, asphalt, birch bark tar, pitch, pine tar,
resin, gum, wax, beeswax, plant exudate, protein glue, starch paste, mortar,
ochre, filler, compound adhesive, hafting residue, sealing material,
waterproofing, inlay putty, coating, varnish, paint binder, or black spot is
used to reconstruct tool function, hafting, repair, waterproofing, craft,
recipe, medicine, exchange, route, source, or technological behavior.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Artefact/context | Which tool, handle, vessel, sherd, basket, boat, ornament, wall, burial, layer, or collection preserves the residue? |
| Residue location | Is the residue on edge, haft, fracture, interior, exterior, seam, repair, surface coating, lump, or sediment contact? |
| Preservation | Could heat, burial, oxidation, leaching, microbial action, conservation, handling, or contamination alter the residue? |
| Analytical method | Which GC-MS, Py-GC/MS, FTIR, Raman, microscopy, SEM-EDS, biomarker, isotope, or thermal method produced the identification? |
| Substance class | Is the claim bitumen, tar, resin, wax, gum, protein, starch, mortar, pigment, filler, or mixed/unknown adhesive? |
| Mixture | Are binders, fillers, plasticizers, pigments, ochre, plant fibers, minerals, and contaminants separated? |
| Reference set | Which modern, experimental, geological, botanical, or archaeological reference materials support assignment? |
| Use-wear link | Does edge damage, polish, handle trace, fracture, residue position, or experiment support adhesive use? |
| Function inference | Is the claim hafting, repair, sealing, waterproofing, decoration, medicine, storage, paint, or accidental residue? |
| Production inference | Is the claim raw material collection, heating, distillation, mixing, recipe, skill, workshop, or craft system? |
| Provenance inference | Is the claim local source, geological deposit, plant species, trade route, exchange, migration, or identity? |
| Public memory | Are "oldest glue," "Neanderthal technology," "secret recipe," and trade-network stories contained? |

## Mandatory Refusals

A RUINS paper must refuse:

1. chemical hit as adhesive function without residue-position and use-wear custody;
2. substance class as exact recipe;
3. biomarker match as certain source without reference-set and alteration controls;
4. preservation bias as absence of adhesives;
5. experimental replica as direct ancient process;
6. compound adhesive as cognitive, social, or species superiority proof;
7. bitumen/resin source as direct trade route;
8. residue on one object as complete craft system, medicine, toolkit, or
   technological tradition.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Bitumen and biomarker analysis | Compound ochre adhesives | Preservation and identification bias |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source condition | Thermal and chromatographic methods identify bitumen and compare geological references. | Ochre, bitumen, fillers, and tool contexts support compound adhesive claims. | Different adhesive materials preserve and identify unevenly. |
| Evidence pressure | Biomarker becomes source, route, or function. | Compound recipe becomes cognition or culture proof. | Preserved tar becomes universal adhesive history. |
| Core rule | Substance, mixture, position, use-wear, source, and behavior claims must remain separate. | Recipe claims need mixture and experiment gates. | Absence and frequency claims need preservation-bias controls. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats bitumen, asphalt, birch bark tar,
pitch, pine tar, resin, gum, wax, beeswax, plant exudate, protein glue, starch,
mortar, ochre, filler, black residue, GC-MS hit, Py-GC/MS result, FTIR spectrum,
Raman spectrum, biomarker, isotope value, experimental replica, or compound
adhesive as exact hafting, direct tool function, complete recipe, certain
source, trade route, medicine, cognitive proof, species superiority, craft
specialization, complete toolkit, or technological tradition without this
checklist or an equivalent adhesive/resin residue gate.

## Source Pressure

- Bitumen method studies foreground reference materials, biomarkers,
  analytical choices, and archaeological object contexts as separate layers.
- Compound adhesive papers show that mixture, filler, residue position, and
  experimental replication are needed before behavioral claims.
- Preservation-bias studies show that different adhesives survive and identify
  unevenly, so frequency and absence claims need controls.
