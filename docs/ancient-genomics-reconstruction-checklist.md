# Ancient Genomics Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when ancient DNA is used to reconstruct ancestry, phenotype,
population history, kinship, identity, affiliation, or public memory.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Sample | Which bone, tooth, extract, library, or genome dataset anchors the claim? |
| Authentication | What damage pattern, contamination test, coverage, and quality controls constrain use? |
| Reference panel | Which ancient and modern populations are included or absent from comparison? |
| Model | Is the claim PCA, ADMIXTURE, qpAdm, f-statistic, kinship, phenotype prediction, or another model? |
| Phenotype | Are trait markers probabilistic and separated from facial/artistic reconstruction? |
| Population | Is the inference individual affinity, kinship, replacement, admixture, continuity, or affiliation? |
| Governance | Are living-community relation, consent, repatriation, and legal authority separately handled? |
| Memory | Is public race, origin, ancestor, or identity language separated from genomic evidence? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- Genome affinity is modern race.
- Phenotype prediction is exact appearance.
- Facial reconstruction is recovered identity.
- Population affinity settles cultural continuity by itself.
- Affiliation analysis replaces living-community authority.
