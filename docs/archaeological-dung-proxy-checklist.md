# Archaeological Dung Proxy Checklist

Use this checklist when animal tending, herding, penning, fuel use, plaster
preparation, foddering, grazing, vegetation, land use, domestication, or
human-animal proximity is reconstructed from dung spherulites, fecal biomarkers,
dung fungi, coprolites, phytoliths, micromorphology, FTIR, flotation residues,
or sediment chemistry.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Deposit | Is the evidence dung accumulation, fuel ash, plaster preparation, floor sediment, midden, flotation residue, or mixed deposit? |
| Proxy | Is the signal spherulite, fecal lipid, dung fungus, phytolith, micromorphology, FTIR, or chemistry? |
| Preservation | Did burning, dissolution, trampling, alkalinity, water, curation, or sampling alter the proxy? |
| Host/source | Is the dung human, herbivore, omnivore, livestock, wild animal, mixed, or unidentified? |
| Formation | Did animals deposit it in place, did humans collect it, or was it moved as fuel, plaster, or manure? |
| Reuse | Was dung used as fuel, building material, floor preparation, fertilizer, or waste? |
| Method | Which counting, microscopy, lipid, FTIR, phytolith, or multi-proxy workflow supports the claim? |
| Activity | Is the inference tending, penning, herding, fuel use, foddering, grazing, or land use? |
| Scale | Is the claim feature-level, household-level, site-level, regional, or historical transition? |
| Memory | Are earliest-herding, domestication, and ancient-dung public stories kept separate? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- A dung spherulite is direct domestication.
- A proxy concentration is exact herd location.
- Dung fuel is a full fuel economy.
- Dung in plaster is complete construction practice.
- Fecal biomarkers are final species identity without source controls.
- Dung fungi are whole vegetation history.
- A site sequence is the origin of herding.
