# Access Substitution Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:access-substitution-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** digital surrogates, replica significance, and 3D/digital
authenticity debates
**Rubric:** v1.75

## Problem

RUINS often works with cases where original access is impossible, restricted, or
ethically inappropriate. Substitute access is valuable, but it is also a common
overclaim surface. Digital surrogates, scans, models, replicas, casts,
facsimiles, 3D prints, VR/AR scenes, haptic devices, tactile copies, remote
cameras, digital twins, virtual tours, apps, viewing windows, visitor routes,
teaching copies, and restitution substitutes can collapse into the original
object, original context, object-equivalent fabric, full site access, original
movement, original experience, direct inspection, complete preservation,
physical return, governance settlement, community consent, research closure, or
public access equity.

## Core Rule

Access-substitution claims must keep original access, substitute form, capture
basis, transformation, metadata/paradata, use purpose, missing affordance,
governance, evaluation, and public memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Access-substitution gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Digital surrogate preservation | Digital cultural objects require context, provenance, rights, and preservation metadata before they can support access claims. |
| Replica significance | Replicas can have heritage value and afterlives without becoming object-equivalent originals. |
| 3D/digital authenticity | Digital reproduction shifts authenticity, materiality, institutional purpose, and curatorial transparency. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.75. Access substitution custody fills a
cross-family gap by requiring surrogate, replica, digital, remote, tactile, and
route-based access to remain explicitly labelled as mediated access rather than
direct original custody or complete public access.
