# Adhesive/Resin Residue Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:adhesive-resin-residue-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** bitumen biomarker analysis, compound ochre adhesives, and
adhesive preservation-bias studies
**Rubric:** v1.73

## Problem

Adhesive and resin residues can preserve rare evidence of hafting, repair,
waterproofing, sealing, coating, inlay, craft, and material knowledge. They can
also overpromote chemistry into behavior. Bitumen, asphalt, birch bark tar,
pitch, pine tar, resins, gums, waxes, beeswax, plant exudates, protein glues,
starches, mortars, ochre, fillers, black residues, GC-MS hits, Py-GC/MS results,
FTIR spectra, Raman spectra, biomarkers, isotope values, experimental replicas,
and compound adhesive identifications can collapse into exact hafting, direct
tool function, complete recipe, certain source, trade route, medicine,
cognitive proof, species superiority, craft specialization, complete toolkit,
or technological tradition.

## Core Rule

Adhesive/resin residue claims must keep artefact/context, residue location,
preservation, analytical method, substance class, mixture, reference set,
use-wear link, function inference, production inference, provenance inference,
and public memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Adhesive/resin residue gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Bitumen analytical methods | Biomarkers and thermal/chromatographic signals require reference materials, alteration controls, and object-context custody. |
| Compound ochre adhesives | Mixtures of bitumen, ochre, fillers, and residues require experiment, residue-position, and behavioral-inference gates. |
| Preservation-bias studies | Different adhesive materials preserve and identify unevenly, so absence and frequency claims need bias controls. |
