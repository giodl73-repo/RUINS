# Archaeobotanical Macroremain Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:archaeobotanical-macroremain-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger paper:** `research/publications/panel-archaeobotanical-macroremain-custody/`
**Rubric:** v1.62

## Problem

Plant macroremains can make ancient food and farming visible through charred,
waterlogged, desiccated, or mineralized remains. Those remains are easily
overpromoted. Charred seeds, cereal grains, chaff fragments, rachis segments,
spikelet forks, weed seeds, fruits, nutshells, pulses, flotation counts, heavy
fractions, crop ratios, ubiquity values, density values, immature grain ratios,
and public first-farming images can become exact crop inventories, complete
diets, direct domestication proof, final crop-processing stages, full storage
systems, harvest calendars, field ecology, household functions, market supply,
famine proof, migration routes, ritual meanings, or complete agricultural
economies.

## Core Rule

Macroremain claims must keep sample, recovery method, preservation pathway,
context, taxon assignment, crop/weed status, taphonomy, processing stage,
quantification, spatial distribution, seasonality, activity inference, economy
synthesis, and public plant-food memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Macroremain gate.
- Source lock.

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.62. Archaeobotanical macroremain
custody creates a reusable control for seed, grain, chaff, flotation,
crop-processing, storage, diet, agriculture, and first-farming claims.
