## Overclaim Pressure

Vivid catastrophe images can outrun source custody: one fire, river of ink,
total loss, final blame, destroyed inventory, or civilizational closure.

## Control Artifacts

| Artifact | Path | What it controls |
|---|---|---|
| Alexandria refusal register | `research/publications/panel-library-alexandria-loss-memory/refusal-register.md` | Blocks one-event burning, Caesar-total-destruction, Arab-conquest closure, and universal-library literalism. |
| House primary-source custody gate | `research/publications/panel-house-of-wisdom-institution-memory/primary-source-custody-gate.md` | Keeps river-of-ink and exact destruction ledgers as memory/future-custody claims. |
| House scorecard | `research/publications/panel-house-of-wisdom-institution-memory/reviews/SCORECARD.md` | Caps literal catastrophe/destruction-ledger claims without dedicated custody. |
| Cross-case checklist | `docs/catastrophe-image-audit-checklist.md` | Separates event, source tradition, material loss, inventory, blame/closure, and later memory. |
| Rubric v1.10 cap | `QUALITY_RUBRIC.md` | Caps papers that treat vivid catastrophe images as complete loss, inventory, final blame, or civilizational closure without a gate. |

## ASHLAR Decision

Record `catastrophe-image-audit` as Core. Alexandria and House of Wisdom already
test the method across different famous-loss images; the dedicated checklist and
rubric cap make it reusable. The Core method is not a claim that every
catastrophe image is false. It is a scoring and refusal rule: a vivid image may
be used only at the custody level it has earned, and it cannot become a complete
inventory, final blame account, or civilizational closure by narrative force.
