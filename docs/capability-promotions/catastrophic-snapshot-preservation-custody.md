## Overclaim Pressure

Excellent disaster preservation makes a site look like a frozen complete moment.
Akrotiri tempts readers to turn ash-buried architecture, wall paintings, vases,
everyday objects, absent bodies, and "Pompeii of the Aegean" language into a
complete final day, certain evacuation story, whole society, ritual system, or
civilizational collapse account.
Joya de Ceren adds a domestic/agricultural version of the same pressure:
preserved households, fields, food, storage, and activity surfaces can look like
complete daily life or a complete Maya village record.

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:
- Allowed: Akrotiri as an ash-preserved Bronze Age settlement with preserved
  architecture, wall paintings, pottery, everyday objects, and volcanic
  destruction context.
- Refused: exact final day, certain evacuation narrative, complete household
  inventory, total ritual system, Atlantis frame, and civilizational-collapse
  closure.
- Future P3: exact household inventories, crop economy, activity timing,
  eruption microsequence, ritual interpretation, and Maya social-history
  synthesis.
- Sibling handoff: PORTO owns Thera geography; CERES owns volcanic geology,
  ash, conservation, and materials; CANON owns site/object identity; MAXIM/LUCIA
  own Bronze Age history; RITE owns ritual interpretation; script/text systems
  own Linear A claims.
