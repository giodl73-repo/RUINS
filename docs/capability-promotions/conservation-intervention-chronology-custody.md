# Conservation Intervention Chronology Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:conservation-intervention-chronology-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** conservation documentation, integrated heritage
documentation protocols, and World Heritage conservation archives
**Rubric:** v1.69

## Problem

Conservation records are essential evidence for ruins because modern treatment
often changes what survives, what is visible, and what the public can access.
But treatment reports, condition maps, cleaning records, consolidants, coatings,
mortars, fills, replacement fabric, stabilization projects, shelters,
monitoring records, digital documentation, management plans, conservation
archives, and rescue narratives can collapse into original condition, original
appearance, ancient fabric, complete conservation history, original date,
permanent preservation, solved authenticity, final access status, community
meaning, or total site history.

## Core Rule

Conservation intervention claims must keep surviving fabric, condition record,
intervention event, materials used, documentation archive, chronology,
reversibility, monitoring, attribution, public access, and public memory
separate.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Conservation documentation | Treatment records and images describe care and treatment but do not automatically provide complete object or site history. |
| Integrated documentation protocols | Sustainable conservation needs history, attributes, preservation state, and alterations separated across the monument's lifetime. |
| Conservation archives | Archives document conservation history, but record survival, selection, scope, and archival gaps remain evidence conditions. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.69. Conservation intervention
chronology custody fills the Site Damage, Access, and Conservation Afterlife
gap by treating modern conservation as a dated evidence layer rather than as
original-state proof, final preservation status, or complete site history.
