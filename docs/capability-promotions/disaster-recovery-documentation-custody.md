# Disaster Recovery Documentation Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:disaster-recovery-documentation-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** post-disaster damage assessment, first aid to cultural
heritage, and World Heritage disaster-risk management
**Rubric:** v1.71

## Problem

Disaster recovery records are often the first surviving evidence after damage,
loss, conflict, flood, fire, earthquake, or emergency stabilization. They are
also produced under pressure. Emergency plans, rapid damage assessments,
first-aid forms, incident logs, drone images, satellite images, emergency
photographs, no-access reports, triage lists, salvage priorities, stabilization
records, recovery plans, reconstruction proposals, funding documents, and public
before/after images can collapse into complete loss inventories, total condition
maps, absence proof, cultural value ranking, completed conservation,
reconstruction authority, community consensus, complete destruction, complete
recovery, or solved disaster history.

## Core Rule

Disaster recovery documentation claims must keep pre-disaster baseline, hazard
event, access condition, rapid assessment, damage category, triage decision,
salvage record, recovery plan, funding/governance, update cycle, and public
memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Disaster recovery documentation gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Post-disaster damage assessment | First assessments are time-sensitive and often access-limited, so they cannot stand as complete inventories. |
| First aid to cultural heritage | Emergency workflows separate security, assessment, salvage, stabilization, documentation, and recovery. |
| World Heritage disaster-risk management | Preparedness, response, damage assessment, treatment, recovery, and rehabilitation are distinct stages. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.71. Disaster recovery documentation
custody fills the Site Damage, Access, and Conservation Afterlife gap by
treating emergency and recovery records as staged, access-limited evidence
rather than final loss, conservation, reconstruction, or recovery authority.
