## Overclaim Pressure

When an original site is too fragile for public access, replicas and digital
visits become the public experience. Lascaux tempts readers to treat Lascaux IV,
virtual tours, public photographs, conservation summaries, animal imagery, and
prehistoric-art language as if they were direct access to the original cave,
complete conservation knowledge, final chronology, or settled meaning.

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:
- Allowed: Lascaux as a fragile original decorated cave whose public access is
  mediated through replicas, virtual tours, and institutional interpretation.
- Refused: replica-as-original, complete conservation status, final chronology,
  ritual certainty, whole-culture meaning, and direct public access claims.
- Future P3: compare with Chauvet, Altamira, or another unlike restricted cave
  before Core promotion.
- Sibling handoff: PORTO owns Vézère geography; CERES owns cave environment,
  conservation, pigments, and replica materials; CANON owns cave/replica
  identity; RITE owns ritual meaning; MAXIM/LUCIA own broader prehistory;
  digital/experience systems own visitor-media implementation.
