# Geophysical Subsurface Survey Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:geophysical-subsurface-survey-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger paper:** `research/publications/panel-geophysical-subsurface-survey-custody/`
**Rubric:** v1.60

## Problem

Geophysical prospection can make buried plans visible without excavation. GPR,
magnetometry, magnetic susceptibility, resistivity, EMI, conductivity,
gradiometry, radar time slices, amplitude maps, depth slices, anomaly clusters,
interpolated grids, motorized-array outputs, and integrated datasets can be
overpromoted into confirmed buildings, complete street grids, exact wall lines,
certain tombs or ritual features, complete urban plans, excavation-equivalent
proof, final chronologies, preservation conditions, sacred-landscape designs,
solved site functions, or whole cities.

## Core Rule

Geophysical survey claims must keep instrument, grid, sampling interval,
processing, georeferencing, depth model, soil contrast, anomaly class,
natural/modern interference, feature candidate, ground truth, chronology,
function, plan synthesis, and public nondestructive-discovery memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Geophysical-survey gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.60. Geophysical subsurface survey
custody creates a reusable control for GPR, magnetometry, EMI, resistivity,
buried-plan, hidden-monument, and city-without-digging claims.
