# Glass/Faience Compositional Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:glass-faience-compositional-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** ancient glass element/isotope interpretation, plant-ash
glass provenance, and Egyptian faience compositional studies
**Rubric:** v1.72

## Problem

Glass and faience composition can identify raw-material families, production
zones, colorant choices, opacifier recipes, recycling, and technological
traditions. It can also overpromote chemistry into history. Glass, faience,
frit, glaze, beads, vessels, ingots, tesserae, amulets, colorants, opacifiers,
natron, plant ash, sand, Sr/Nd/Pb isotopes, trace elements, LA-ICP-MS, SEM-EDS,
EPMA, Raman, XRF/pXRF, compositional groups, source matches, cullet signals,
and recycling markers can collapse into exact workshop, direct trade route,
complete recipe, final production method, vessel maker, owner, ethnicity,
status, migration, political control, complete industry, solved provenance, or
lost technology.

## Core Rule

Glass/faience compositional claims must keep object/sample, context, material
class, analytical method, component signal, weathering, grouping, reference set,
recycling/mixing, production inference, provenance inference, and public memory
separate.

## Required Controls

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Element/isotope interpretation | Elements and isotopes may reflect raw materials, colorants, opacifiers, contamination, recycling, and alteration rather than a single provenance signal. |
| Plant-ash glass provenance | Isotopic and trace-element groups can suggest probable production zones while route, workshop, and exchange claims remain probabilistic. |
| Egyptian faience composition | Body, glaze, colorant, technology, and uncertain origin require separate analytical and interpretive custody. |
