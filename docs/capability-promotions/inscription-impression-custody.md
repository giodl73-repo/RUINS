# Inscription Impression Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:inscription-impression-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** epigraphic squeezes, RTI/photogrammetry documentation, and
digital rubbing workflows
**Rubric:** v1.76

## Problem

Epigraphic copies can preserve access to fragile, distant, worn, inaccessible,
or later-damaged inscriptions. They can also overpromote copies into source
closure. Squeezes, rubbings, estampages, casts, molds, latex impressions,
photographs, RTI images, photogrammetry models, micro-photogrammetry outputs,
3D scans, digital rubbings, tracings, line drawings, facsimiles, catalogue
images, digital corpus records, and enhanced images can collapse into the
original inscription, original context, final reading, complete text, edition,
translation, authorship, date, provenance, law, ritual, social history, corpus
completeness, or solved inscription.

## Core Rule

Inscription impression claims must keep original surface, surface condition,
impression method, capture distortion, orientation, archive custody, reading
layer, edition boundary, context boundary, and public memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Inscription impression gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Paper squeezes | Physical impressions can preserve relief and support readings while remaining copies with their own archive custody. |
| RTI and photogrammetry | Digital relighting and modelling can improve visibility, but capture and processing layers must not become final editions. |
| Digital rubbing | Simulated rubbing can be non-invasive and useful, but it is a transformed digital output, not direct access to the original surface. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.76. Inscription impression custody
fills the Text, Corpus, and Mediated Recovery gap around squeezes, rubbings,
estampages, RTI, photogrammetry, and digital rubbings by separating copy,
capture, processing, reading, edition, context, and public recovery claims.
