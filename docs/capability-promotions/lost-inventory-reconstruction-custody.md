# Lost Inventory Reconstruction Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:lost-inventory-reconstruction-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** reconstructed libraries, dispersed manuscript records,
and former-library databases
**Rubric:** v1.77

## Problem

Lost inventories are often reconstructed from witnesses made before, during, or
after dispersal. Those witnesses are valuable and incomplete. Catalogues, shelf
lists, accession books, sale catalogues, dealer records, shipping lists,
donation lists, estate inventories, owner marks, fragments, quotations,
citations, library stamps, marginalia, bindings, digital databases, provenance
indexes, and later summaries can collapse into complete original inventory,
exact copy identity, uninterrupted custody, whole work, whole collection, proof
of absence, digitally reunited collection, restitution map, total loss,
complete intellectual world, or solved collection biography.

## Core Rule

Lost-inventory reconstruction claims must keep original collection, inventory
witness, witness date, item identity, ownership signal, transfer event, survival
status, matching rule, gap model, reconstruction claim, and public memory
separate.

## Required Controls

- Lost-inventory reconstruction gate.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Reconstructed personal libraries | Lists, annotations, and later decisions can identify holdings and dispositions while leaving copy identity and completeness uncertain. |
| Dispersed manuscript records | Sale catalogues and dealer archives preserve uneven provenance visibility and broken ownership chains. |
| Former-library databases | Digital tools can reconnect partial witnesses without restoring original order or complete collection closure. |
