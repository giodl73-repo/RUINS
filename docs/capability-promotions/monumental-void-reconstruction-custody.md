# Monumental Void Reconstruction Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:monumental-void-reconstruction-custody`
**State:** Core
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger papers:** `panel-bamiyan-empty-niche-reconstruction-custody`; `panel-mostar-bridge-reconstructed-void-custody`
**ASHLAR decision date:** 2026-07-06

## Pressure

Destroyed monuments can be known through iconic absence, fragments, exposed
remnants, rebuilt fabric, replica-like reconstruction, recovery campaigns, and
peace or memory narratives. Public knowledge can then treat a void or
reconstruction as the original monument, complete recovery, community consensus,
or final symbolic meaning.

## Gate

The Bamiyan paper separates empty niches, blast scars, fragments, surviving
artefacts, stabilization, demining, caves and murals, reconstruction debate,
local/community context, and global intentional-destruction memory.

The Mostar paper adds missing-span memory, recovered remnants, rebuilt bridge
fabric, authentic materials and techniques, exposed original material,
international scientific reconstruction, restored surroundings, and
reconciliation symbolism.

## Promotion Basis

The capability is Core because two unlike publication-ready papers exercise the
same overclaim surface and because rubric v1.24 adds a case-derived check and
automatic cap.

Required artifacts:

- `docs/monumental-void-reconstruction-checklist.md`
- Bamiyan monumental-void gate and refusal register
- Mostar reconstructed-void gate and refusal register
- source locks and page-anchor audits in both papers
- role reviews and scorecards naming the capability

## Core Rule

An empty niche, missing span, fragment, exposed remnant, reconstructed replica,
restored setting, memorial image, peace symbol, or recovery narrative must not
become the original monument, complete original fabric, finished conservation,
community consensus, final symbolic meaning, or total heritage recovery without
separate custody.
