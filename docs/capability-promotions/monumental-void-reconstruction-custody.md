# Monumental Void Reconstruction Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:monumental-void-reconstruction-custody`
**State:** Active
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active
**Trigger paper:** `panel-bamiyan-empty-niche-reconstruction-custody`
**ASHLAR decision date:** 2026-07-06

## Pressure

When a destroyed monument leaves an iconic void, the absence can be mistaken for
the original monument, a reconstruction mandate, a solved conservation plan, or
a complete memory of the destroyed heritage.

## Gate

The Bamiyan paper separates:

- empty niches,
- blast scars,
- fragments and surviving artefacts,
- cliff and niche stabilization,
- demining and safety,
- caves and mural remains,
- reconstruction debate,
- local/community context,
- and global intentional-destruction memory.

## Promotion Basis

The paper is publication-ready under rubric v1.23 and contains:

- a monumental-void gate,
- a source lock,
- a page-anchor audit,
- a refusal register,
- a cultural-memory register,
- a sibling-boundary register,
- and a scorecard naming this capability as the promotion decision.

## Core Trigger

Promote to Core after a second unlike paper where a destroyed monument is known
through an empty setting, fragments, reconstruction debate, memorialization, or
recovery law without becoming the original monument.

## Current Rule

Until this capability is Core, use it as an active review lens but do not add an
automatic score cap. Papers should refuse original-monument equivalence,
finished-conservation, reconstruction-consensus, final iconographic meaning, and
total-recovery claims unless those custody surfaces are separately locked.
