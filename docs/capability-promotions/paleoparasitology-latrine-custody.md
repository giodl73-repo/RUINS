## Problem

Ancient latrines, coprolites, and related sediments can preserve parasite eggs,
sedimentary ancient DNA, plant DNA, animal DNA, and pathogen markers. These
signals can expose diet, sanitation, animal proximity, health, and movement, but
they can also be overread as disease diagnosis, complete diet, migration route,
trade proof, sanitation system, or public-health history.

## Core Rule

Paleoparasitology claims must keep deposit, context, sampling, parasite stage,
host specificity, molecular or immunological assignment, infection versus
exposure, diet, sanitation, and memory separate.
