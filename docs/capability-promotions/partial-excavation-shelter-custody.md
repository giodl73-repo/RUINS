## Overclaim Pressure

A partially excavated site under shelter creates a persuasive public view. The
visitor sees exposed buildings, pillars, conservation work, and walkways, while
large parts of the site remain unexcavated or interpreted through survey,
ongoing fieldwork, and changing scholarly models. Gobekli Tepe tempts readers to
turn that view into complete site exposure, final ritual function, complete
settlement model, solved social hierarchy, or finished conservation status.
Catalhoyuk adds a mudbrick settlement case where shelters protect exposed areas
but conservation continues, visitor signage interprets selected areas, and many
objects and records are held off-site.

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:
- Allowed: Gobekli Tepe as a partially excavated, sheltered, conserved, and
  publicly visited Neolithic site whose interpretation is still changing.
- Refused: complete exposure, final ritual or funerary interpretation, complete
  settlement model, solved hierarchy, finished conservation, oldest temple as
  total explanation, and visitor view as total site.
- Core rule: no paper may treat sheltered excavation, visitor route, exposed
  building, reconstructed house, conservation treatment, off-site object display,
  archive, or current interpretation as complete exposure, final function,
  complete settlement model, solved hierarchy, finished conservation, or whole
  origin story without the checklist.
- Sibling handoff: PORTO owns geography; CERES owns materials, weathering,
  excavation, and conservation; CANON owns named buildings/pillars; RITE owns
  ritual/funerary interpretation; MAXIM/LUCIA own Neolithic transition; visitor
  experience systems own route/shelter implementation.
