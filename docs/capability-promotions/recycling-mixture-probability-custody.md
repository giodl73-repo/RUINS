# Recycling/Mixture Probability Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:recycling-mixture-probability-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** metal provenance and circulation, glass recycling, and
scientific provenance-method critiques
**Rubric:** v1.74

## Problem

Many RUINS source claims depend on materials that have more than one history.
Recycling, remelting, alloying, blending, reuse, repair, refiring, reworking,
contamination, batching, compositing, cullet, scrap, old metal, secondary raw
materials, conservation materials, and mixed residues can preserve useful
evidence, but they can also erase or combine signals. Mixed composition,
recycled material, remelted glass, alloyed metal, contaminated residue,
cluster membership, source matches, outliers, and mixture scenarios can collapse
into single source, direct workshop, exact ore, direct trade route, complete
recipe, intentional technology, foreign import, market map, scarcity proof,
collapse evidence, solved circulation system, or complete industry history.

## Core Rule

Recycling/mixture claims must keep object/sample, primary signal, secondary
signal, mixing mechanism, endmember model, proportion estimate, analytical
method, statistical model, temporal layer, provenance claim, and public memory
separate.

## Required Controls

- Recycling/mixture probability gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| Metal provenance and circulation | New metal provenance, old metal circulation, mixing, alloying, and compositional change overlap. |
| Glass recycling | Primary glassmaking source, recycled cullet, colorant contamination, and mixed antimony/manganese systems can coexist. |
| Provenance-method critiques | Scientific source matching depends on assumptions that fail when artefacts mix sources, recipes, repairs, and histories. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.74. Recycling/mixture probability
custody fills the Material Trace and Production Systems gap by requiring
mixture mechanisms, endmember assumptions, proportions, temporal layers, and
uncertainty before material signals become clean provenance or production
history.
