## Overclaim Pressure

A replica, facsimile, restoration, or digital model can be treated as if it were
the damaged original, as if public display authorized reconstruction, or as if
project-public specifications were engineering or conservation evidence.

## Control Artifacts

| Artifact | Path | What it controls |
|---|---|---|
| Replica specification gate | `research/publications/panel-palmyra-replica-reconstruction/replica-specification-gate.md` | Separates project-public replica description from engineering, structural, conservation, and original-monument claims. |
| Restoration methodology gate | `research/publications/panel-palmyra-replica-reconstruction/restoration-methodology-gate.md` | Refuses on-site restoration methods, conservation advice, engineering recommendations, and claims that the replica authorizes reconstruction. |
| RUINS rubric v1.6 | `QUALITY_RUBRIC.md` | Adds replica-status custody checks and caps for treating replicas, restorations, facsimiles, or digital models as object-equivalent. |

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:

- Allowed: source-scoped public replica/facsimile status and UNESCO governance
  boundaries.
- Refused: object-equivalence, engineering dimensions, conservation materials,
  intervention sequencing, and "Palmyra has been rebuilt" claims.
- Future P3: restoration-methodology custody, feature-specific expansion beyond
  the current Arch of Triumph claim, and direct technical project-document
  custody.
- Sibling handoff: PORTO for place/site geography; CANON for named feature
  identity; FONTES for image/model custody.
