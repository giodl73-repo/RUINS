## Overclaim Pressure

Rock-cut sites are often known through a single facade image. Petra tempts
readers to treat the Treasury, Royal Tombs, or Palace Tomb as the whole city,
and to treat conservation of one facade or water-diversion system as complete
knowledge of the city's water engineering, erosion state, chronology, and
Nabataean history.

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:
- Allowed: Petra as a rock-cut and partly built city where facade images,
  water-engineering remains, sandstone decay, tourism pressure, and conservation
  projects require separate custody.
- Refused: facade-as-whole-city, Palace Tomb conservation as finished
  conservation, water channels as complete water-system reconstruction, tourism
  image as archaeology, and lost-city memory as total history.
- Future P3: compare with Lalibela, Ellora/Ajanta, Abu Simbel, or another unlike
  carved-rock site before Core promotion.
