# Rock-Surface Exposure Dating Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:rock-surface-exposure-dating-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger paper:** `research/publications/panel-rock-surface-exposure-dating-custody/`
**Rubric:** v1.64

## Problem

Surface-age methods can constrain when rocks, carvings, walls, cairns, quarries,
or landforms were exposed. They can also be overpromoted. Cosmogenic nuclide
concentrations, 10Be ages, 26Al/10Be burial dates, 36Cl exposure ages, lichen
thallus diameters, lichen size-frequency curves, rock-varnish layers, varnish
microlamination, microerosion rates, weathering rinds, calibration curves,
shielding corrections, erosion corrections, inherited exposure, and public
oldest-surface headlines can become exact monument construction dates, direct
carving dates, certain petroglyph ages, final quarry dates, abandonment events,
complete surface stability, path-use chronologies, landform-event proof,
cultural phase dates, or solved site chronologies.

## Core Rule

Rock-surface dating claims must keep sample surface, exposure event,
pre-exposure inheritance, shielding, erosion, calibration, species/substrate,
varnish formation, disturbance, date model, archaeological association, event
inference, and public age memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Surface-exposure gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.64. Rock-surface exposure dating
custody creates a reusable control for cosmogenic nuclide, lichenometry, rock
varnish, microerosion, quarry, monument, petroglyph, and oldest-surface claims.
