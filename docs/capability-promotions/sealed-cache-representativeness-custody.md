## Overclaim Pressure

A sealed cache can look like a complete library, institutional archive,
community memory, or canonical corpus because it survived in bulk. Dunhuang
Cave 17 adds further pressure because the cache was discovered, partially
removed, dispersed, catalogued, digitized, and remembered as a "Library Cave."

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:
- Allowed: Cave 17 as a sealed and later dispersed cache containing tens of
  thousands of manuscripts, paintings, printed documents, textiles, and other
  objects.
- Refused: complete library function, known sealing motive, original order,
  whole Silk Road history, canonical corpus, or complete digital reunion.
- Future P3: compare with Cairo Geniza, Nag Hammadi, or another unlike cache
  before Core promotion.
- Sibling handoff: FONTES/MUNDUS own editions, languages, translations, and
  exact readings; CANON owns named cave/collection identity; MAXIM/LUCIA own
  Silk Road history; legal/policy systems own acquisition and repatriation.
