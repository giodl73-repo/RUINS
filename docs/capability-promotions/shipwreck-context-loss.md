## Overclaim Pressure

A shipwreck can preserve a dense cargo assemblage while destroying or
displacing the operating context that made the cargo meaningful. Readers may
turn cargo diversity into a route map, settled destination, owner identity,
crew identity, political-sponsor claim, complete trade model, or museum-display
equivalent of original context.

## Scope Decision

Current paper permission:

- Allowed: source-scoped wreck identity, excavation chronology, broad cargo
  classes, context-loss method claims, uncertainty/refusal claims, and museum
  afterlife framing.
- Refused: exact cargo counts, object-level identities, route closure,
  destination closure, owner/crew/political-sponsor closure, campaign-specific
  finds, stowage reconstruction, whole-economy model, display-as-original
  context, and site-location/diving/recovery/collecting guidance.
- Future P3: direct campaign report, catalogue, specialist-study, or sibling
  custody before exact cargo, route, hull, stowage, object, or identity claims.
- Sibling handoff: FONTES / MUNDUS own exact publication and source custody;
  CANON owns object/entity identity; PORTO owns route and maritime geography;
  MAXIM / LUCIA own broader Late Bronze Age history; CERES owns material and
  craft-economy claims.
