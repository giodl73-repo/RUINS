# Structural Simulation Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:structural-simulation-custody`
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active -> Core
**Trigger pressure:** scan-to-FEM, finite-element heritage analysis, structural
digital twins, and archaeological simulation workflows
**Rubric:** v1.67

## Problem

Structural simulation helps conservation and interpretation, especially where
direct destructive testing is impossible. But finite element analysis, finite
element method, discrete element method, limit analysis, scan-to-FEM workflows,
laser-scan meshes, structural digital twins, modal testing, material models,
damage maps, load cases, seismic runs, deformation predictions, collapse
simulations, shipwreck simulations, and intervention scenarios can collapse into
claims that a ruin's original geometry, past behavior, stability, collapse
cause, construction recipe, restoration plan, design intention, builder
identity, technological superiority, or whole history has been solved.

## Core Rule

Structural simulation claims must keep surviving fabric, survey data, geometry
simplification, material model, boundary conditions, damage state, calibration,
scenario, sensitivity, conservation claim, and public memory separate.

## Required Controls

- Structural simulation gate.
- Source lock.
- Page-anchor audit.
- Refusal register.
- Cultural-memory register.
- Sibling-boundary register.
- Cross-case checklist.

## Source Pressure

| Source family | Pressure exposed |
|---|---|
| FEM and laser-scanning reviews | Scan acquisition, mesh generation, model validation, diagnostics, deformation, and digital twins must not collapse into one authoritative reconstruction. |
| Scan-to-FEM heritage workflows | Point clouds and BIM/FE models require simplification, retopology, material assumptions, and dynamic-analysis custody. |
| Archaeological simulation cases | Scaled shipwreck, artefact, or structural scenarios can support interpretation while remaining scenario-bound and parameter-sensitive. |

## Decision

Promote directly to Core under rubric v1.67. Structural simulation custody fills
the Reconstruction, Replica, and Sensory Access gap for computational behavior
models and supports the Site Damage, Access, and Conservation Afterlife family
without letting engineering outputs become original-state certainty or solved
history.
