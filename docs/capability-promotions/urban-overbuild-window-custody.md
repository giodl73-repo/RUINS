# Urban Overbuild Window Custody

**Capability id:** `ruins-capability:urban-overbuild-window-custody`
**State:** Active
**Promotion:** Candidate -> Active
**Trigger paper:** `panel-templo-mayor-urban-overbuild-window-custody`
**ASHLAR decision date:** 2026-07-06

## Pressure

Overbuilt urban sites are often known through excavation windows, rescue
excavations, museum routes, under-building displays, and removed objects. Public
knowledge then tends to treat the visible window as the buried city or sacred
precinct itself.

## Gate

The Templo Mayor / Huei Tzompantli paper separates:

- buried city remains,
- excavation window,
- under-building fragment,
- museum object,
- colonial and modern overbuild,
- conservation monitoring,
- public rediscovery memory,
- and whole-city or whole-precinct interpretation.

## Promotion Basis

The paper is publication-ready under rubric v1.22 and contains:

- an urban-overbuild window gate,
- a source lock,
- a page-anchor audit,
- a refusal register,
- a cultural-memory register,
- a sibling-boundary register,
- and a scorecard naming this capability as the promotion decision.

## Core Trigger

Promote to Core after a second unlike case, preferably London Mithraeum, Rome
Metro C archaeology, or another case where construction, relocation, display, or
excavation windows mediate public access to buried urban fabric.

## Current Rule

Until this capability is Core, use it as an active review lens but do not add an
automatic score cap. Papers should refuse whole-city, complete-plan,
direct-access, finished-conservation, and solved-ritual claims unless the
relevant custody surfaces are separately locked.
