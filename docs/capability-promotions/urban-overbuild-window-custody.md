## Pressure

Overbuilt urban sites are often known through excavation windows, rescue
excavations, museum routes, under-building displays, relocated remains,
reconstructed fabric, immersive exhibits, removed objects, and digital guides.
Public knowledge can then treat the visible or reconstructed window as the
buried city, complete structure, original context, or final ritual meaning.

## Gate

The Templo Mayor / Huei Tzompantli paper separates buried city remains,
excavation windows, under-building fragments, museum objects, colonial and
modern overbuild, conservation monitoring, public rediscovery memory, and
whole-precinct claims.

The London Mithraeum paper adds relocation, listed reconstruction,
reconstruction accuracy, original fabric, new mortar/render/wood, immersive
display, recent excavation objects, headquarters context, and public-discovery
memory.

## Core Rule

An excavation window, under-building display, relocated ruin, reconstructed
fabric, immersive exhibit, museum route, removed object, digital guide, or public
rediscovery image must not become the whole buried city, complete precinct,
complete building plan, direct original context, finished conservation, final
ritual meaning, or total urban history without separate custody.
