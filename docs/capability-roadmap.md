# Capability Roadmap

Status: active ASHLAR roadmap
Date: 2026-07-05

This roadmap turns the capability registry into next-paper work. Core
capabilities are already reusable RUINS method. Active capabilities need one
more test, a cross-case checklist, or an automatic cap before they become core.
Candidate capabilities need a trigger paper.

## Active To Core

| Capability | Current state | Core promotion trigger | Good next paper pressure |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ruins-capability:palimpsest-overwrite-custody` | Active from Archimedes Palimpsest. | A second overwritten/erased-text paper tests whether undertext/overtext/imaging/reading controls work beyond Archimedes. | Sinai palimpsests, Codex Ephraemi Rescriptus, erased Qur'an palimpsests, recovered charters, or reused parchment fragments. |

## Candidate To Active

| Capability | Candidate pressure | Activation trigger | Good next paper pressure |
|---|---|---|---|
| _None currently queued_ | Add the next candidate when a paper plan names a new pressure surface. | A reviewed paper creates a gate, register, cap, or refusal pattern. | Prefer cases that stress a new evidence condition. |

## Paper Selection Heuristic

Prefer the next case that exercises the weakest active capability while adding a
new evidence condition. A good RUINS next paper should:

1. Require at least one new gate or register.
2. Stress a sibling boundary that is not already routine.
3. Avoid becoming a general history article.
4. Produce one capability promotion decision: candidate to active, active to
   core, or no promotion with reasons.
5. Leave exact source, route, restoration, recipe, or textual claims gated if
   they are not directly audited.
