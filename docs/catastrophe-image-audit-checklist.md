# Catastrophe Image Audit Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:catastrophe-image-audit`
Owner: ASHLAR / ERRATA / Cultural Memory Reader

Use this checklist when a vivid destruction image becomes more memorable than
the source custody behind it: a single fire, river of ink, total sack, final
burning, destroyed inventory, or civilization-ending scene.

## Required Separation

| Layer | Required question | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Event | What happened in the source-scoped event: fire, sack, siege, looting, dispersal, neglect, or institutional change? | Context only; not automatically complete loss. |
| Source tradition | Which source reports the image, when, through what transmission, and with what genre limits? | Reported tradition, not direct proof unless audited. |
| Material loss | What collection, building, object, text, archive, or institution is specifically shown to be lost? | Claim only to the audited object/scope. |
| Inventory | Does the source provide itemized holdings, counts, titles, rooms, catalogues, or collections? | Refuse destroyed ledgers without direct custody. |
| Blame / closure | Does the image assign final blame or a single endpoint? | Treat as narrative pressure unless corroborated. |
| Later memory | How did the image become cultural shorthand, warning story, schoolbook scene, or public icon? | Afterlife evidence only. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A catastrophe-image audit must refuse:

1. one vivid event as total loss without a source-scoped bridge;
2. one source tradition as the final fate of an institution or corpus;
3. dramatic imagery as an inventory, catalogue, or destroyed-book count;
4. final blame assignments without source custody;
5. civilizational closure from a single destruction scene;
6. public memory as ancient, medieval, or original-state proof;
7. publication-ready status for literal catastrophe claims when the paper only
   locks memory or context.

|---|---|---|---|
| Vivid image | Single burning of the universal Library. | River of ink and total library destruction after 1258. | Iconic image is not full loss custody. |
| Source pressure | Caesar-fire traditions, Serapeum debate, later Arab-conquest story, modern myth. | Bayt al-Hikma references, Abbasid scholarly ecology, 1258 context, later catastrophe memory. | Separate event, institution, source tradition, and afterlife. |
| Refused compression | One-event burning, total Caesar destruction, Arab-conquest closure, universal-library literalism. | Literal river-of-ink, exact destroyed ledger, all collection-loss details from Baghdad sack. | Refuse totalizing catastrophe unless directly audited. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats a vivid catastrophe image as a
complete loss event, destroyed inventory, final blame account, or civilizational
closure without this checklist or an equivalent catastrophe-image gate.
