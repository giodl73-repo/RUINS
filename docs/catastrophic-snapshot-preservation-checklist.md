# Catastrophic Snapshot Preservation Checklist

Status: Core RUINS checklist
Trigger cases: Akrotiri; Joya de Ceren
Capability: `ruins-capability:catastrophic-snapshot-preservation-custody`

Use this checklist when sudden destruction preserves architecture, households,
fields, objects, paintings, food, bodies, voids, or absence surfaces so well
that a site appears to capture a complete frozen moment.

## Evidence Layers

| Layer | Ask | Blocks |
|---|---|---|
| Disaster process | Was burial, ashfall, collapse, surge, flood, or fire directly evidenced? | Single cinematic final moment. |
| Preservation bias | What survived unusually well, and what did not? | Total recovery or total daily life. |
| Architecture | Is the feature excavated, restored, displayed, or reconstructed? | Complete social organization. |
| Household/object context | Are objects in primary, secondary, stored, abandoned, or displayed context? | Complete household inventory. |
| Food/fields/plants | Is the claim crop, meal, storage, field, or economy? | Complete diet or agricultural system. |
| Bodies/absences | Are bodies present, absent, displaced, cast, or inferred? | Certain death, escape, or evacuation story. |
| Activity timing | Does the claim require exact final action or sequence? | Frozen-last-moment narrative. |
| Public comparison | Is the phrase Pompeii, frozen city, vanished people, or lost world? | Evidence equivalence or civilizational closure. |

## Required Refusals

- Refuse complete final-day or last-moment narratives without sequence custody.
- Refuse whole household, whole village, or whole society from preserved
  contexts alone.
- Refuse certain escape, death, or evacuation claims from body presence or
  absence alone.
- Refuse ritual, economy, and collapse claims without sibling-system source
  custody.
- Refuse Pompeii-style comparisons as method unless the evidence layers match.
