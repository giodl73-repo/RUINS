# Colonial Denial Register Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:colonial-denial-register`
Owner: ASHLAR / ERRATA / Cultural Memory Reader

Use this checklist when a ruin or monument has been interpreted through
colonial, racialized, settler, or ownerless-history narratives that deny local
or Indigenous authorship.

## Required Separation

| Layer | Required question | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Material site | What architecture, earthwork, artifact, inscription, or context survives? | Direct evidence layer. |
| Local or Indigenous authorship | What current archaeology or heritage custody supports local/Indigenous construction or use? | Source-scoped consensus layer. |
| Colonial denial narrative | What non-local, non-Indigenous, lost-race, foreign-builder, or ownerless framing was imposed? | Interpretive violence / memory layer, not a live alternative. |
| Trade/contact evidence | Are imported goods, wide exchange, or distant raw materials present? | Contact layer only; not authorship proof. |
| Public mystery framing | Does popular language revive denial through "mystery," "lost race," "giants," or ancient visitors? | Refusal target. |
| Living memory and heritage | What modern community, sacred, national, or heritage meanings exist? | Afterlife/custody layer; not a shortcut to every original-state claim. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A colonial-denial paper must refuse:

1. non-local or non-Indigenous authorship claims that survive only as colonial
   or racialized speculation;
2. trade goods, long-distance materials, or impressive scale as proof of
   foreign builders;
3. "mystery" framing where the mystery is produced by refusing local agency;
4. ownerless lost-city or vanished-race language;
5. sacred/community memory as decorative folklore;
6. heritage recognition as total proof of every detailed archaeological claim;
7. modern political symbolism as direct medieval, ancient, or precontact
   identity evidence.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Great Zimbabwe | Mound Builder denial | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Denial form | Non-African, Biblical, Phoenician, Arab, or ownerless lost-city theories. | Lost race, giants, ancient visitors, Ten Lost Tribes, or non-Native builders. | Name denial as interpretation history, not archaeology. |
| Evidence compression | Imported goods become foreign authorship. | Earthwork scale and geometry become non-Native authorship. | Contact, scale, and complexity never prove outsider builders by themselves. |
| Corrective source layer | UNESCO, public scholarship, archaeology, local memory. | NPS, UNESCO/heritage pages, Ohio History Connection, Smithsonian/Thomas, archaeology public scholarship. | Current source-scoped archaeology controls authorship claims. |
| Living memory | Sacred landscape, local ritual, national afterlife. | Indigenous ancestral ties, sacred sites, modern heritage return/access. | Memory is custody and afterlife, not a full substitute for site archaeology. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats colonial, racialized, settler,
lost-race, or ownerless-history narratives as live archaeological alternatives
without a colonial-denial register or equivalent refusal gate.
