# Conservation Intervention Chronology Checklist

Use this checklist when a ruin, object, building, wall painting, sculpture,
inscription, site, archive, or landscape is interpreted through conservation
records, restoration archives, treatment reports, condition maps, monitoring
data, cleaning, consolidation, stabilization, replacement fabric, protective
shelters, visitor works, digital documentation, or management-plan records.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Surviving fabric | Which original, repaired, replaced, stabilized, exposed, buried, or inaccessible fabric is being described? |
| Condition record | What survey, condition map, photograph, scan, note, or inspection documents the state at a specific date? |
| Intervention event | What cleaning, consolidation, anastylosis, sheltering, drainage, replacement, removal, or stabilization occurred? |
| Materials used | Which consolidants, mortars, fills, adhesives, coatings, barriers, supports, or replacement materials were introduced? |
| Documentation archive | Which conservation file, lab report, drawing, database, image set, or management record anchors the claim? |
| Chronology | Are intervention dates, observation dates, damage dates, reuse dates, excavation dates, and original dates separated? |
| Reversibility | Is the intervention reversible, retreatable, compatible, experimental, emergency, partial, or undocumented? |
| Monitoring | What later inspection, sensor, image, condition map, or maintenance record tests the intervention effect? |
| Attribution | Is the claim original construction, later repair, modern restoration, conservation artifact, or display choice? |
| Public access | Are visitor routes, interpretation panels, replicas, closures, and safety works separated from original use? |
| Public memory | Are rescue, recovery, authenticity, neglect, and saved-for-the-future stories contained? |

## Mandatory Refusals

A RUINS paper must refuse:

1. current condition as original condition;
2. cleaned surface as original appearance;
3. restored fabric as ancient fabric;
4. treatment record as complete conservation history;
5. intervention date as original construction or damage date;
6. monitoring stability as permanent preservation;
7. replacement material as evidence of original material;
8. management-plan success as solved authenticity, access, community meaning,
   or final site history.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Conservation documentation | Integrated heritage documentation | World Heritage conservation archives | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Source condition | Textual and visual treatment records document care and treatment. | Systematic data collection records history, condition, alterations, and attributes. | Archives document the conservation history of a site. | Conservation records are time-bound evidence layers. |
| Evidence pressure | Treatment files become complete object history. | Documentation system becomes final condition or intervention truth. | Archive presence becomes solved conservation story. | Record, intervention, monitoring, and original-state claims must stay separate. |
| Overclaim pressure | Restoration becomes authenticity proof. | Digital documentation becomes preservation outcome. | Conservation archive becomes total site afterlife. | Conservation chronology needs gaps, dates, and intervention-material custody. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats a treatment report, conservation
file, condition map, cleaning record, consolidant, coating, mortar, fill,
replacement fabric, stabilization, shelter, monitoring record, management plan,
digital documentation, or conservation archive as original condition, original
appearance, ancient fabric, complete conservation history, original date,
permanent preservation, solved authenticity, final access status, community
meaning, or total site history without this checklist or an equivalent
conservation intervention chronology gate.

## Source Pressure

- Conservation documentation work defines treatment records as textual and
  visual records collected during care and treatment, not as full object
  histories.
- Integrated documentation protocols foreground history, attributes,
  preservation state, and lifetime alterations as separate data needed for
  sustainable conservation decisions.
- World Heritage conservation-archive guidance foregrounds records that
  document conservation history, which makes archive scope and survival part of
  the evidence problem.
