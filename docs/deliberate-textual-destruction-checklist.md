# Deliberate Textual Destruction Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:deliberate-textual-destruction`
Owner: ASHLAR / PALIMPSEST / ERRATA

Use this checklist when a text tradition, archive, book corpus, school, or
collection was reportedly targeted by deliberate destruction, suppression,
burning, proscription, or confiscation.

## Required Separation

| Layer | Required question | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Destruction order | Is there an edict, report, memorial, chronicle, or later account of deliberate action? | Source-tradition layer until passage custody is locked. |
| Target category | What exactly was targeted: private copies, official archives, rival histories, religious texts, images, teachers, or institutions? | Category-specific; no total corpus inference. |
| Exemptions and survivals | Which texts, archives, technical books, official copies, later copies, or witness sets survived? | Survival layer that limits total-loss claims. |
| Source mediation | Is the account contemporary, later, translated, hostile, apologetic, or polemical? | Mediation label required. |
| Corpus effect | What can be said about absent content, counts, genres, or destroyed titles? | Refuse unless directly inventoried. |
| Memory image | Did the event become a shorthand for tyranny, conquest, heresy, purification, or civilizational rupture? | Afterlife evidence only. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A deliberate-textual-destruction paper must refuse:

1. a destruction report as a complete inventory;
2. destroyed-book counts without direct ledger custody;
3. total-corpus content, genre, or intellectual-history reconstruction from the
   report alone;
4. one culprit, edict, or episode as the entire loss history;
5. exact quotation or translation without source and translation custody;
6. later moral memory as direct evidence of event details;
7. surviving witnesses as a representative sample of the destroyed whole.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Maya codices | Qin book burning | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Destruction form | Colonial religious destruction report and tiny surviving witness set. | Imperial policy report targeting politically dangerous private texts and rival histories. | Deliberate action is a source-scoped layer, not total corpus custody. |
| Survival pressure | Four extant codices can be mistaken for the whole corpus. | Exemptions, official copies, later transmission, and palace/archive fires complicate total-loss claims. | Always record exemptions and survivals. |
| Source mediation | Landa passage, translation/facsimile/object boundaries. | Li Si memorial as recorded by Sima Qian, later Han framing, modern critique. | Label report, recorder, translation, and later interpretation. |
| Refused compression | Destroyed-book counts, full Maya genre map, exact Landa quotation, codex readings. | Total Chinese intellectual rupture, exact destroyed inventory, scholar-execution image as settled fact, complete loss of classics. | Refuse inventory and closure without custody. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats a deliberate destruction report as
a complete inventory, corpus map, destroyed-book count, exact quotation, or
total intellectual-loss explanation without this checklist or an equivalent
destruction-custody gate.
