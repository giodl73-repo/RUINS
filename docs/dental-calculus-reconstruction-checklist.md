# Dental Calculus Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when diet, microbiome, health, behaviour, medicine, or
food-production history is reconstructed from dental calculus DNA, proteins,
microremains, isotopes, or chemistry.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Specimen | Which individual, tooth, site, date, and calculus deposit was sampled? |
| Matrix | Is the signal plaque mineral, DNA, protein, starch, phytolith, isotope, lipid, or mixed residue? |
| Sampling | How were sampling, extraction, controls, and contamination handled? |
| Method | Is the claim based on metagenomics, proteomics, microscopy, isotope, chemistry, or combined assays? |
| Assignment | How secure is the taxon, protein, peptide, species, pathogen, or food-source identification? |
| Diet/health | Is the claim occasional exposure, consumption, repeated practice, disease, or treatment? |
| Behaviour | Are medicine, cuisine, dairying, adaptation, ecology, and lifestyle separately sourced? |
| Memory | Are public ancient-menu, milk-origin, disease, or medicine stories kept separate? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- A taxonomic hit is an exact meal.
- Beta-lactoglobulin is a complete dairying system.
- Plant signal is deliberate medicine.
- Microbiome or pathogen signal is a full diagnosis.
- A few calculus samples define species-wide or culture-wide lifestyle.
