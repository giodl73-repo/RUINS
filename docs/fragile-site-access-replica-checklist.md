# Fragile Site Access Replica Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:fragile-site-access-replica-custody`
Derived from: Lascaux; Chauvet

Use this checklist when the original site is closed, restricted, or too fragile
for ordinary public access and replicas, facsimiles, scans, photographs, digital
tours, or visitor installations carry public knowledge.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Original site | Is the claim about the protected original, not a replica, visitor route, or digital surrogate? |
| Access status | Does the paper distinguish public access, research entry, conservation entry, and virtual access? |
| Conservation basis | Are closure, monitoring, intervention, non-intervention, and climate claims separately sourced? |
| Replica/facsimile | Is the replica identified by scale, selectivity, authority, method, and visitor purpose where the paper relies on it? |
| Digital mediation | Are 3D surveys, virtual visits, photographs, and interactive experiences treated as mediated access? |
| Image and dating | Are image inventory, species identification, chronology, and technique claims separately locked? |
| Interpretation | Are ritual, symbolic, cultural, and whole-prehistory meanings refused unless independently sourced? |
| Public memory | Is masterpiece, first-art, oldest-art, or discovery language treated as reception unless it supports a source-locked claim? |

## Automatic Cap

Cap the paper at `34/50` if it treats a closed or restricted fragile site,
research visit, replica, facsimile, digital tour, public image, conservation
summary, or visitor experience as direct public access to the original, complete
conservation status, final chronology, ritual meaning, or whole-culture
interpretation without this custody separation.

## Sibling Handoffs

| Surface | Owner |
|---|---|
| Geography, route, region, and tourism location | PORTO |
| Cave climate, pigments, geology, materials, and conservation science | CERES |
