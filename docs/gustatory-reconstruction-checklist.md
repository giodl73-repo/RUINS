# Gustatory Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when lost food or drink is reconstructed from residue,
charred remains, recipe tablets, ingredient lists, translation, experimental
cooking, commercial products, public recipes, or tastings.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Source witness | Is the claim based on residue, charred food, text, image, tool, vessel, or later recipe? |
| Ingredient | Are ingredient classes, substitutions, extinct terms, and modern equivalents separated? |
| Quantity | Are missing measures, proportions, temperatures, and timing future-locked? |
| Process | Which brewing, cooking, fermentation, soaking, grinding, or serving steps are inferred? |
| Experiment | Is the dish or drink a reconstruction, adaptation, classroom exercise, or commercial product? |
| Taste | Does the claim distinguish flavor, texture, mouthfeel, aroma, and modern palate? |
| Context | Is the food tied to banquet, ritual, household, palace, temple, or market context only when sourced? |
| Safety | Are modern food/alcohol instructions, edibility, and health claims excluded or owned elsewhere? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- A reconstruction is exact ancient taste.
- A recipe tablet is a complete recipe.
- A residue marker supplies quantities or process.
- A modern dish or drink is the original meal.
- A tasting event is direct sensory access.
- A named ruler/person consumed the reconstruction.
- A historical recipe is safe modern guidance.
