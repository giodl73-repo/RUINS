# Inscription Impression Checklist

Use this checklist when inscription, rock art, relief, seal, stamped mark,
graffito, painted text, carved sign, or damaged writing claims rely on squeezes,
rubbings, estampages, casts, molds, latex impressions, photographs, RTI,
photogrammetry, micro-photogrammetry, 3D scans, digital rubbings, line drawings,
facsimiles, tracings, hand copies, digital corpora, or catalogue images.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Original surface | Which stone, wall, object, seal, tablet, panel, fragment, or architectural context carries the inscription? |
| Surface condition | What erosion, breakage, staining, recutting, repainting, reuse, conservation, or modern damage affects legibility? |
| Impression method | Is the evidence squeeze, rubbing, estampage, cast, mold, latex impression, tracing, photograph, RTI, 3D scan, or digital rubbing? |
| Capture distortion | Could pressure, paper shrinkage, lighting, angle, scale, registration, smoothing, missing edges, or digital processing alter the result? |
| Orientation | Is the record positive, negative, reversed, mirrored, relit, enhanced, normalized, or reoriented for publication? |
| Archive custody | What collection, shelfmark, catalogue, file, image set, rights record, or chain of custody anchors the copy? |
| Reading layer | Who reads, restores, dots, brackets, expands, translates, dates, or attributes the text? |
| Edition boundary | Is the claim copy, facsimile, diplomatic transcription, edition, translation, commentary, or corpus metadata? |
| Context boundary | Does the copy preserve original location, scale, installation, viewing condition, reuse, or only surface text? |
| Public memory | Are "text recovered," "stone saved," "lost inscription," and final-reading stories contained? |

## Mandatory Refusals

A RUINS paper must refuse:

1. squeeze, rubbing, or estampage as the original inscription;
2. photograph, RTI, scan, or digital rubbing as final reading;
3. enhanced image as complete text;
4. catalogue image as edition;
5. impression copy as original context, scale, or installation;
6. archive survival as corpus completeness;
7. one lighting/capture mode as settled letter form;
8. copy-based reading as authorship, date, provenance, law, ritual, or social
   history without edition and context custody.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Paper squeezes | RTI and photogrammetry | Digital rubbing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source condition | Physical impressions can preserve relief from worn inscriptions and may outlive access to the stone. | Relighting and modelling can reveal faint strokes, tooling, and surface damage. | Simulation can produce non-invasive rubbing-like images from digital surface data. |
| Evidence pressure | Squeeze becomes stone or final text. | Enhanced visibility becomes final reading. | Digital rubbing becomes complete inscription recovery. |
| Core rule | Copy, capture, processing, reading, edition, and context claims must remain separate. | Visibility support is not philological closure. | Simulated impression is mediated evidence, not original access. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats a squeeze, rubbing, estampage,
cast, mold, latex impression, photograph, RTI image, photogrammetry model,
micro-photogrammetry output, 3D scan, digital rubbing, tracing, line drawing,
facsimile, catalogue image, digital corpus record, or enhanced image as the
original inscription, original context, final reading, complete text, edition,
translation, authorship, date, provenance, law, ritual, social history, corpus
completeness, or solved inscription without this checklist or an equivalent
inscription impression gate.

## Source Pressure

- Epigraphic squeeze discussions foreground the value of impressions as surface
  records while keeping the squeeze distinct from the stone and the scholarly
  reading.
- Digital epigraphy and RTI work foregrounds capture, lighting, modelling, and
  processing layers that improve visibility without closing edition questions.
- Digital rubbing work foregrounds simulation and non-invasive substitution,
  which require explicit mediation and transformation custody.
