# Light-Mediated Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when lost visual conditions are reconstructed from torches,
lamps, hearths, sunlight, shadow, reconstructed openings, simulation, VR,
reenactment, museum lighting, or digital firelight.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Trace | What material, textual, image, or architectural evidence anchors the light claim? |
| Light source | Is the source torch, lamp, hearth, sun, shadow, window, reflector, screen, or digital light? |
| Model | Is the reconstruction live experiment, physical model, simulation, VR, or display design? |
| Measurement | What intensity, duration, position, fuel, angle, scan, or software parameters are known? |
| Visibility | What becomes visible, hidden, distorted, animated, or navigable under the model? |
| Inference | Are art placement, route, activity, ritual, and intention kept separate? |
| Memory | Is "see the past" language handled as access, not direct ancient perception? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- Reconstructed light is direct ancient sight.
- Visibility proves intention or meaning.
- A VR scene is the original viewer experience.
- A light-and-shadow effect solves ritual function.
- A museum or reenactment lighting setup is object-equivalent.
