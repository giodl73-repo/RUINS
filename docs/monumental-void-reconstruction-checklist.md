# Monumental Void Reconstruction Checklist

Use this checklist when a destroyed monument is known through an empty setting,
missing span, fragments, exposed remnants, reconstruction, memorialization, or
recovery narrative.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Empty niche or missing span | Is the absence evidence of destruction, memorial testimony, public image, or a reconstruction target? |
| Surviving fragments | Are fragments inventoried, reused, exposed, conserved, or only generally reported? |
| Reconstructed fabric | Which parts are original, reused, newly made, replicated, or inferred? |
| Materials and techniques | Are materials and methods source-locked, analogous, experimental, or symbolic? |
| Stabilization and safety | Is conservation ongoing, complete, monitored, or still uncertain? |
| Restored setting | Is the surrounding landscape/city restored, rebuilt, managed, or narratively unified? |
| Memorial or peace symbolism | Is later meaning being handled as afterlife rather than original-state proof? |
| Recovery governance | Are community, expert, legal, donor, and World Heritage claims kept separately graded? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- The void or reconstruction is the original monument.
- The reconstructed monument contains complete original fabric.
- Fragments prove a complete original form.
- Rebuilding settles community consensus.
- Peace or recovery symbolism proves final meaning.
- Stabilization or reopening means conservation is finished.
- A successful reconstruction erases the destruction history.
