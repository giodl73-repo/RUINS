# Palimpsest Overwrite Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:palimpsest-overwrite-custody`
Owner: ASHLAR / PALIMPSEST / ERRATA

Use this checklist when erased, scraped, washed, overwritten, chemically
treated, imaged, processed, or catalogued text is used as evidence.

## Required Separation

| Layer | Required question | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Surviving object | What manuscript, folio, fragment, codex, or collection survives? | Object layer, not only hidden text. |
| Undertext | What erased or obscured writing is being recovered? | Source-scoped; exact readings require edition custody. |
| Overtext | What later writing or use changed the object? | Separate manuscript layer. |
| Material damage | Are leaves missing, moldy, chemically treated, glued, stained, overwritten, or forged? | Damage layer, not proof of absence or recovery. |
| Conservation / handling | What stabilization, disbinding, cradle, or handling shaped access? | Custody layer, not method advice. |
| Imaging modality | What spectral, XRF, UV, transmitted, raking, or other image was captured? | Evidence surface, not transcription. |
| Processing | What automated or manual processing made text legible? | Interpretation support layer. |
| Scholarly reading | Who identifies, reads, dates, or describes the text? | Scholarly claim layer; exact readings go to FONTES/MUNDUS. |
| Catalogue / digital access | Is the result a metadata record, image set, digital library, or edition? | Access layer, not complete closure. |
| Public memory | What lost-text, rediscovery, or technology-rescue story attached? | Reception layer. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A palimpsest paper must refuse:

1. imaging output as transcription;
2. processed image as edition;
3. catalogue record as complete identification;
4. public digital library as scholarly closure;
5. recovered undertext as complete lost work without edition custody;
6. overtext as merely an obstacle rather than part of the surviving object;
7. collection-scale discovery counts as exact text, date, authorship, or
   translation claims.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Archimedes Palimpsest | Sinai Palimpsests | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scale | Single famous codex. | Collection-scale project across many manuscripts. | Object and collection scope must be explicit. |
| Recovery layer | Conservation, multispectral imaging, XRF, scholarship, public data. | Spectral capture, automated/manual processing, codicology, catalogue. | Imaging and processing are evidence layers. |
| Text pressure | Unique Archimedes, Hyperides, commentary undertexts. | Multiple languages, preliminary discoveries, unidentified texts. | Discovery is not edition or translation. |
| Overclaim pressure | Complete recovery of the original book. | Digital library/corpus equals identified text history. | Refuse total recovery and catalogue closure. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats erased text, spectral image,
processed image, catalogue record, public digital library, or preliminary
identification as complete recovery, edition, translation, authorship, date, or
textual history without this checklist or an equivalent palimpsest overwrite
gate.
