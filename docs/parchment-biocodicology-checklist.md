# Parchment Biocodicology Checklist

Use this checklist when manuscript production, animal species, library history,
provenance, trade, herd management, microbiome, use, conservation, or textual
authority is reconstructed from parchment, vellum, eraser crumbs, ZooMS,
collagen peptides, DNA, mtDNA, microbiome, folio sampling, or codicological
metadata.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Object | Which manuscript, charter, folio, quire, binding, or fragment is sampled? |
| Folio | Is the claim leaf-level, quire-level, codex-level, archive-level, or library-level? |
| Sampling | Was the sample eraser crumb, conservation byproduct, destructive cut, swab, or imaging trace? |
| Conservation | What cleaning, handling, repair, storage, or contamination history affects the surface? |
| Material | Is the substrate parchment, vellum, leather, paper, binding material, or mixed object? |
| Molecular assignment | Is the signal collagen peptide, ZooMS mass fingerprint, DNA, mtDNA, microbiome, or protein? |
| Species | Is the animal source calf, sheep, goat, mixed, unresolved, or reference-limited? |
| Production | Are skin selection, quire construction, workshop, supply, and trade separately sourced? |
| Textual boundary | Are text, edition, authorship, date, and authority kept outside material species calls? |
| Memory | Are living-book, library-reconstruction, trade-route, and scriptorium stories separated? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- A species call is exact animal identity.
- A folio sample is complete manuscript provenance.
- ZooMS is scriptorium attribution.
- Parchment DNA is a trade route.
- Collagen peptides are herd history.
- Microbiome is complete use history.
- Material analysis is textual authority.
