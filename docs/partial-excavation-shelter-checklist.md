# Partial Excavation Shelter Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:partial-excavation-shelter-custody`
Derived from: Gobekli Tepe; Catalhoyuk

Use this checklist when public knowledge of a site comes through exposed
excavation areas, protective shelters, visitor routes, conserved remains,
reconstructed elements, off-site objects, archives, or interpretive exhibits.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Excavated exposure | Is the claim about exposed remains, not the whole site or mound? |
| Unexcavated reserve | Does the paper preserve what remains buried, unsurveyed, unpublished, or future-locked? |
| Shelter | Are roofs and covers treated as protection/visibility infrastructure, not final conservation? |
| Visitor route | Are walkways, signs, viewing terraces, replica houses, and exhibits treated as mediated public access? |
| Conservation | Are stabilization, monitoring, erosion, delamination, replacement, and reconstruction separated? |
| Object/archive custody | Are off-site objects, physical archives, digital records, and on-site replicas separately named? |
| Interpretation | Are ritual, domestic, urban, hierarchy, origin-story, and whole-culture claims separately locked? |
| Public memory | Is "oldest," "first city," "first temple," or "origin of civilization" language treated as reception unless source-locked? |

## Automatic Cap

Cap the paper at `34/50` if it treats a sheltered excavation, visitor route,
exposed building, reconstructed house, conservation treatment, off-site object
display, archive, or current interpretation as complete site exposure, final
site function, complete settlement model, solved hierarchy, finished
conservation, or whole origin story without this custody separation.
