# Partial Technical Transmission Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:partial-technical-transmission`
Owner: ASHLAR

Use this checklist when a technique survives through texts, material traces,
samples, experiments, public memory, or later reconstructions, but the complete
ancient practice does not.

## Required Layers

| Layer | Required question | Claim permission |
|---|---|---|
| Text | Is there a direct ancient passage, translation, quotation, or later report? | Textual witness only unless the passage is audited. |
| Material | What object, structure, residue, shell, pigment, tool, or facility survives? | Material survival only; not full process. |
| Sample | What exact sample was tested, by whom, and by what method? | Sample-specific identity or mechanism. |
| Context | What site, layer, workshop, deposit, or recovery context connects the trace to the technique? | Context-specific production/use claim. |
| Experiment | Is the experiment ancient, modern reconstruction, analytical test, or public demonstration? | Plausibility or mechanism only, with status label. |
| Memory | Is the claim a prestige story, lost-secret story, museum summary, or modern afterlife? | Reception evidence only. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A partial-technical-transmission paper must refuse:

1. one secret recipe unless source custody proves a bounded formulation;
2. universal technique claims from one site, text, sample, or experiment;
3. modern operational guidance unless the repo explicitly owns practical
   instructions;
4. social, civilizational, or superiority claims from technical survival alone;
5. modern reconstruction as ancient practice without a reconstruction-status
   gate;
6. exact quantities, timings, temperatures, routes, workshops, or labor systems
   without direct custody.

## Promotion Test

The capability is Core when two papers can use the same controls across
different technical domains.

| Test | Roman concrete | Tyrian purple | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Evidence survives through multiple surfaces | Texts, structures, marine samples, lime clasts, Pompeii workflow, public memory. | Shell deposits, pigment residues, textiles, HPLC, ancient descriptions, museum memory. | No single surface may become the whole technique. |
| Overclaim pressure | Secret ingredient, universal recipe, modern engineering copy, civilizational superiority. | Secret dye recipe, universal production sequence, shell-heap scale, royal status from color, modern dye guidance. | Name the strongest overclaims and refuse them directly. |
| Source-specific mechanism | Marine mineral evolution, hot-mixing hypothesis, Pompeii context. | Cape Kolonna production context, Timna textile identification, Kelsey public summary. | Mechanisms and identifications remain source/sample/site scoped. |
| Sibling handoff | Materials science, architecture, engineering, Roman history. | Chemistry, taxonomy, ancient-text custody, Phoenician/Roman history, site geography. | RUINS owns evidence discipline, not the sibling domain. |

## Automatic Cap

A technique paper cannot score above `34` if it treats a text, material sample,
experiment, public summary, or reconstruction as a complete lost technique
without this checklist or an equivalent custody gate.
