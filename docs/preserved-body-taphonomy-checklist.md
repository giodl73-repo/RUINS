# Preserved Body Taphonomy Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:preserved-body-taphonomy-custody`
Owner: ASHLAR / PALIMPSEST / ERRATA

Use this checklist when human remains preserve flesh, skin, hair, organs,
stomach contents, injury traces, facial appearance, or displayable body form.

## Required Separation

| Layer | Required question | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Find context | Where and how were the remains found? | Recovery layer, not identity or motive. |
| Taphonomy | What preserved or damaged the body? | Preservation layer, not biography. |
| Body completeness | Which parts survive and which are missing, altered, or catalogued separately? | Object layer. |
| Forensic finding | What injuries, ligatures, x-rays, scans, or autopsy findings are source-locked? | Cause layer, not motive closure. |
| Sampling | What gut, pollen, isotope, radiocarbon, DNA, or tissue sample is being used? | Sample-specific evidence. |
| Conservation | What treatment, dissection, reburial, freeze-drying, or display preparation occurred? | Modern custody layer. |
| Display | What museum state, case, loan, label, or public encounter exists? | Display layer, not original state. |
| Interpretation | What ritual, execution, crime, social-role, or biography claim is proposed? | Hypothesis layer requiring separate custody. |
| Public memory | What face, mystery, sacrifice, celebrity, or ethics narrative attached? | Reception layer. |

## Mandatory Refusals

A preserved-body paper must refuse:

1. preserved face as complete identity or biography;
2. injury as motive;
3. gut content as ritual proof;
4. display state as original body state;
5. conservation treatment as neutral access;
6. one body as population history;
7. public memory as forensic or ritual evidence.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Tollund Man | Lindow Man | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preservation | Danish peat-bog body with vivid face and partial body loss. | British peat-bog body with tissue, skin, bone, violent-death traces, and associated parts. | Taphonomy and completeness must be explicit. |
| Forensic pressure | Hanging by suffocation can become motive. | Multiple injuries can become ritual/crime certainty. | Cause and motive stay separate. |
| Conservation/display | Head preservation after autopsy/dismemberment. | PEG and freeze-drying support display. | Display is modern custody. |
| Overclaim pressure | Last meal and noose become sacrifice story. | violent death and armband/noose become social-role story. | Refuse biography, motive, and population closure. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats preserved human tissue, forensic
injury, gut content, conservation treatment, facial appearance, or display as
complete identity, biography, death motive, ritual meaning, social role, or
population history without this checklist or an equivalent preserved-body
custody gate.
