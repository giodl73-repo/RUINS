# Radiocarbon Reservoir Old-Wood Checklist

Use this checklist when event date, occupation phase, burial date, diet,
marine/freshwater use, pottery use, transition timing, demographic curve,
abandonment, migration, collapse, or public calibrated-date memory is
reconstructed from radiocarbon ages, charcoal, bone collagen, shell, fish bone,
aquatic residue, food crust, bulk sediment, calibration curves, reservoir
offsets, Delta-R, hardwater effect, old wood, Bayesian priors, outlier models,
summed probability distributions, or wiggle matches.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Sample | Which material, organism, artefact, context, collection, and lab record is dated? |
| Carbon source | Is the carbon terrestrial, marine, freshwater, mixed diet, old wood, or residual? |
| Context | Is the sample primary, intrusive, residual, curated, redeposited, or associated by inference? |
| Pretreatment | What cleaning, collagen quality, contaminant removal, and measurement uncertainty are locked? |
| Calibration | Which curve, calibration version, range, and probability structure is used? |
| Reservoir | What marine, freshwater, hardwater, Delta-R, local offset, and temporal variability apply? |
| Old wood | Could long-lived wood, inner rings, curated timber, or charcoal inheritance predate the event? |
| Diet | Does marine/freshwater diet affect human or faunal bone collagen date interpretation? |
| Model | What Bayesian prior, phase, boundary, outlier model, SPD, or wiggle match shapes output? |
| Synthesis | Are event, transition, population, migration, abandonment, and collapse handed to siblings? |
| Memory | Are exact calendar date, oldest-first, and solved-chronology stories contained? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- Calibrated range is an exact event date.
- Charcoal date is direct occupation date.
- Old wood date is construction or burning event.
- Reservoir correction is universal.
- Food crust date is pottery-use date.
- Bayesian boundary is historical transition.
- SPD peak is population curve.
- Outlier rejection removes contextual uncertainty.
