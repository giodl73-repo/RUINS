# Rock-Cut Facade Erosion Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:rock-cut-facade-erosion-custody`
Derived from: Petra; Lalibela

Use this checklist when a rock-cut or rock-hewn site is known through an iconic
facade, church, tomb, interior, tunnel, trench, route, shelter, conservation
project, or visitor image.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Iconic image | Is the public facade/church image kept separate from the whole site? |
| Carved surface | Are carved surface, freestanding structure, interior, roof, floor, and wall claims separated? |
| Water/drainage | Are channels, ditches, trenches, dams, cisterns, drainage, and runoff claims separately locked? |
| Erosion/damage | Are wind, rain, water damage, seismic disruption, mural loss, sculpture loss, and condition claims separately sourced? |
| Shelter/conservation | Are temporary shelters, stabilization, preventive conservation, monitoring, and visual-integrity impacts separated? |
| Living use | Are pilgrimage, worship, tourism, local community, and management layers kept distinct from original-state evidence? |
| Chronology | Are construction date, later use, restoration, and modern conservation history separately gated? |
| Whole history | Are city/civilization/religious-history claims handed to sibling systems unless directly source-locked? |

## Automatic Cap

Cap the paper at `34/50` if it treats an iconic rock-cut facade, rock-hewn
church, carved surface, tunnel/trench, drainage feature, shelter, conservation
project, visitor image, pilgrimage use, or tourism route as the whole site,
complete original form, complete hydraulic/drainage system, solved chronology,
finished conservation, final ritual meaning, or total cultural history without
this custody separation.
