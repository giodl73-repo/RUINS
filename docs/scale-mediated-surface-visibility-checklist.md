# Scale-Mediated Surface Visibility Checklist

Status: core RUINS method
Capability: `ruins-capability:scale-mediated-surface-visibility-custody`
Derived from: Nasca; Uffington White Horse

Use this checklist when a large surface mark, geoglyph, hill figure, landscape
trace, earthwork, or route-scale feature becomes legible only through a specific
scale of viewing, survey, maintenance, or image mediation.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Surface trace | Is the claim about the surviving ground/chalk/desert trace, not only an image or map? |
| Viewpoint | Does the paper distinguish ground, path, slope, distance, aerial, drone, satellite, and public-image viewpoints? |
| Maintenance | Are scouring, re-cutting, cleaning, restoration, erosion, turf growth, and modern consolidation separated from original form? |
| Survey image | Are aerial photographs, drone surveys, radar, LiDAR, and map outlines treated as documentation layers? |
| Candidate status | If remote sensing or AI finds a likely feature, is candidate status separated from field confirmation? |
| Dating | Are OSL, documentary record, stylistic comparison, and traditional dating claims separately locked? |
| Shape history | Are original shape, current outline, restored outline, and altered outline separated? |
| Interpretation | Are ritual, astronomical, emblematic, military, territorial, or whole-landscape meanings refused unless independently sourced? |

## Automatic Cap

Cap the paper at `34/50` if it treats a large surface mark, aerial/drone/satellite
image, survey outline, re-cut/restoration profile, remote-sensing candidate, or
public map as ancient intended viewpoint, complete original shape, complete
inventory, final date, final function, complete damage state, or whole-landscape
interpretation without this custody separation.

## Sibling Handoffs

| Surface | Owner |
|---|---|
| Geography, route, viewshed, and tourism location | PORTO |
| Geomorphology, soil, chalk, desert pavement, erosion, turf, and dating science | CERES |
| Named figure, geoglyph, monument, image, or feature identity | CANON |
| Ritual, astronomical, symbolic, or mythic interpretation | RITE |
| Broader culture history and national emblem history | MAXIM/LUCIA |
| AI, remote sensing, drone survey, map production, and image-processing implementation | AI/remote-sensing systems |
