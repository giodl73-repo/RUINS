# Soil Micromorphology Occupation-Surface Checklist

Use this checklist when activity area, floor, hearth, house use, room function,
construction episode, abandonment, cleaning, dung use, matting, craft area,
storage, penning, burning, plant processing, occupation intensity, daily life,
or public household memory is reconstructed from soil micromorphology, thin
sections, microstratigraphy, occupation surfaces, floor sediments, ash, dung
spherulites, phytoliths, charcoal, plaster, trampled deposits, laminated layers,
FTIR, soil chemistry, image analysis, or experimental hut floors.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Block sample | Which block, thin section, room, surface, layer, collection, and lab record is analyzed? |
| Context | Is the sample floor, fill, hearth, pit, wall, collapse, trample, or mixed deposit? |
| Microfacies | Which laminae, inclusions, voids, fabrics, and boundaries are separately described? |
| Sediment source | Are ash, dung, plaster, sediment, plant remains, charcoal, and anthropogenic input separated? |
| Deposition | Is the deposit in-use accumulation, dumping, sweep-out, construction, renewal, or abandonment? |
| Alteration | Are bioturbation, compaction, dissolution, burning, water movement, and later disturbance gated? |
| Activity residue | Does the residue indicate hearth, mat, animal, plant processing, craft, storage, or unknown activity? |
| Cleaning | Are absence, removal, sweeping, renewal, and preservation bias separated? |
| Spatial sampling | How many samples, rooms, surfaces, controls, and analogues support the pattern? |
| Synthesis | Are room function, household behavior, occupation intensity, and daily life handed to siblings? |
| Memory | Are domestic scene, tidy house, workshop, pen, ritual deposit, and abandonment stories contained? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- Thin section is exact activity area.
- Ash lens is complete hearth history.
- Dung spherulite is penning system.
- Floor lamina is daily-life routine.
- Soil chemistry spot is room function.
- Trampled deposit is occupation intensity.
- Absence of residue is cleaning practice.
- Experimental floor analogue is direct household behavior.
