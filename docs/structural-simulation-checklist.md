# Structural Simulation Checklist

Use this checklist when a ruin, monument, shipwreck, masonry structure,
sculpture, vault, wall, catacomb, bridge, tower, or artefact is interpreted
through finite element analysis, finite element method, discrete element method,
limit analysis, scan-to-FEM, structural digital twins, laser-scan meshes,
damage maps, modal testing, seismic simulation, load modelling, collapse
simulation, deformation modelling, or conservation-intervention simulation.

## Required Separations

| Layer | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Surviving fabric | Which stones, joints, timbers, metals, fragments, voids, cracks, repairs, or deposits survive? |
| Survey data | What laser scan, photogrammetry, CT, drawing, point cloud, or inspection record supplied geometry? |
| Geometry simplification | What was meshed, smoothed, idealized, mirrored, completed, omitted, or retopologized? |
| Material model | Which masonry, mortar, timber, metal, soil, ceramic, composite, or heterogeneous material assumptions were assigned? |
| Boundary conditions | What supports, constraints, contacts, loads, soil interactions, water forces, or environmental conditions were assumed? |
| Damage state | Are cracks, deformation, decay, previous repairs, missing parts, and active deterioration separately modelled? |
| Calibration | What monitoring, modal testing, lab data, historical survey, or field observation validates the model? |
| Scenario | Is the run static, dynamic, seismic, thermal, hygrothermal, impact, collapse, shipwreck, or intervention simulation? |
| Sensitivity | Which parameters, uncertainties, mesh resolutions, and alternative scenarios change the result? |
| Conservation claim | Is the claim diagnosis, risk screen, intervention comparison, restoration support, or proof of original behavior? |
| Public memory | Are collapse-cause, solved-engineering, safe-restoration, and genius-builder stories contained? |

## Mandatory Refusals

A RUINS paper must refuse:

1. finite element output as direct measurement of past behavior;
2. scan-derived mesh as complete original geometry;
3. digital twin as the monument;
4. material assumption as proven ancient construction recipe;
5. one load case as complete stability assessment;
6. modeled failure as actual collapse cause;
7. intervention simulation as conservation correctness;
8. structural adequacy as proof of design intention, builder identity,
   technological superiority, or solved history.

## Cross-Case Test

| Test | Scan-to-FEM heritage structure | Structural digital twin | Shipwreck or artefact simulation | Core rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Source condition | Point clouds, survey data, damage maps, and idealized meshes. | Geometry, monitoring, and model updating for risk assessment. | Scaled or reconstructed model with assumed vessel, cargo, seabed, or object properties. | Survey, mesh, material, boundary, scenario, and validation layers must be distinct. |
| Evidence pressure | Accurate-looking mesh becomes original fabric or complete condition. | Digital twin becomes the structure and future behavior. | Simulation scenario becomes actual event sequence. | Simulation is a scenario-bound inference, not recovered event or object history. |
| Overclaim pressure | FEM result becomes solved stability or restoration plan. | Vulnerability model becomes conservation certainty. | Dispersion or collapse model becomes exact wrecking, breakage, or loss story. | Structural outputs need sensitivity, calibration, and claim-grade gates. |

## Automatic Cap

A paper cannot score above `34` if it treats FEM, FEA, DEM, limit analysis,
scan-to-FEM, digital twin, laser-scan mesh, damage map, modal calibration,
seismic model, load case, collapse simulation, deformation model, or
intervention simulation as complete original geometry, direct past behavior,
actual collapse cause, certain stability, final restoration decision,
construction recipe, design intention, builder identity, technological
superiority, or solved site history without this checklist or an equivalent
structural simulation gate.

## Source Pressure

- FEM/LSD reviews foreground scan acquisition, mesh generation, model
  validation, structural assessment, diagnostics, deformation, and digital-twin
  workflows as distinct layers.
- Scan-to-FEM and heritage digital twin papers show that geometry capture,
  structural modelling, and dynamic or seismic simulation require explicit
  simplification and calibration.
- Archaeological simulation cases, including shipwreck and artefact scenarios,
  show how scaled or reconstructed models can be overread as exact event
  histories.
