# Urban Overbuild Window Checklist

Use this checklist when a buried urban site is known through modern construction,
excavation windows, relocated fabric, reconstructed displays, museum routes, or
digital access.

## Required Separations

| Surface | Required custody question |
|---|---|
| Buried city or structure | What is directly exposed, inferred, relocated, reconstructed, or absent? |
| Excavation or rescue context | Was the evidence recovered during planned excavation, construction rescue, foundation work, bomb-damage clearance, or redevelopment? |
| Window or under-building display | Does the visitor see original in-situ fabric, relocated fabric, reconstructed fabric, or an interpretive display? |
| Reconstruction materials | Which fabric is original, which is modern, and which placement/orientation is reconstructed? |
| Removed objects | Are objects tied to the excavated context, museum custody, later display, or broader urban fill? |
| Digital or immersive access | Is the guide, sound, haze, light, scan, or replica being treated as evidence or visitor interpretation? |
| Conservation status | Is the site monitored, stabilized, reconstructed, listed, or fully conserved? |
| Public rediscovery memory | Is public attention evidence of afterlife rather than original-state proof? |

## Refuse Unless Separately Locked

- The visible window is the whole buried city.
- The display is direct access to unmediated original context.
- The reconstructed plan is the complete original plan.
- Modern materials are equivalent to original fabric.
- Relocation restores original context.
- The museum route solves final function or ritual meaning.
- Public rediscovery proves total historical importance.
- Current conservation means finished conservation.
