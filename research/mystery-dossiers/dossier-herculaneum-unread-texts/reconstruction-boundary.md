# Reconstruction Boundary

## Responsibly Pieced Together

| Claim | Status | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| PHerc. 1667 was virtually unwrapped without physical opening. | Strong. | Applies to the surviving inner core, not the lost outer layers. |
| PHerc. 1667 now has readable Greek text across an extended reconstructed surface. | Strong. | Textual certainty varies by ink signal, surface condition, and papyrological review. |
| The method combines scan acquisition, geometric reconstruction, machine learning, and expert reading. | Strong. | No single layer should be credited as "AI read the scroll" without the others. |
| Better scan data can improve or validate earlier readings. | Strong. | Earlier public milestones should be preserved as method history, not final editions. |
| The approach may scale to more unopened scrolls. | Plausible. | Scalability remains scroll-specific because preservation, ink visibility, deformation, and scan quality vary. |

## Unresolved

| Question | Why unresolved |
|---|---|
| How many unopened Herculaneum scrolls are recoverable? | Each roll has different damage, deformation, ink visibility, and conservation history. |
| When does a recovered transcription become a critical edition? | Edition status requires papyrological review, apparatus, comparison, and version tracking. |
| Which authors and works remain hidden in the still-unopened corpus? | Author/title evidence must be recovered scroll by scroll. |
| Can all machine-detected ink signals be independently verified? | Detection must be linked to scan quality, surface reconstruction, and expert reading. |
| What does the recovered corpus change about ancient philosophy or literature? | Intellectual-history claims require stable text, attribution, context, and comparison. |

## Public-Memory Containment

| Public phrase | RUINS rewrite |
|---|---|
| "AI read the scrolls." | A scan-reconstruction-ML-papyrology pipeline recovered readable text from specified scrolls. |
| "The lost library is back." | One or more damaged scroll witnesses are becoming readable; the library remains partly sealed, partly lost, and partly mediated. |
| "Ancient secrets revealed." | Specific Greek text, title, author, or column evidence must be named. |
| "The scroll was fully read." | Name whether this means the surviving core, the reconstructed surface, reviewed transcription, or final edition. |

## Capability Pressure

`ruins-capability:edition-version-drift-custody`

transcription, release, revision, scholarly edition, translation, and public
summary, each version must keep its own date, authority, and claim boundary.
