## Source Hierarchy

1. Peer-reviewed technical and papyrological publications.
2. Preprints with open data and code.
3. Project announcements that point to data, code, or papers.
4. News or public summaries.
5. Social media and popular "lost library" framing.

## Required Citation Discipline

- Cite the object identifier: PHerc. 1667, PHerc. Paris 4, PHerc. 139, or other
  specific scroll.
- Separate scan acquisition, segmentation, unwrapping, ink detection,
  transcription, author/title attribution, and edition.
- Treat "read" as shorthand only after naming the exact recovery layer.
- Track dates, because June 2026 claims supersede earlier "first word" and
  partial-passage milestones but do not erase their methodological role.
