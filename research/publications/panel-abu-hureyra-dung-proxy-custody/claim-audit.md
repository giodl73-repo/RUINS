# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Abu Hureyra dung spherulites are a dung-proxy custody case. | Strong | The primary evidence is an archaeological dung proxy. |
| Spherulites can support animal-presence and activity-pattern inference. | Moderate | Useful with deposit and formation controls. |
| Spherulites alone prove domestication. | Refused | Domestication requires animal, biological, and historical custody. |
| Dung concentration gives exact herd location. | Refused | Proxy concentration is not a map of herd position. |
| Dung fuel proves a full fuel economy. | Refused | Fuel economy needs broader material and quantitative evidence. |
| Dung proxies reconstruct final foddering regime. | Refused | Foddering requires phytolith, plant, animal, and seasonal controls. |
| Abu Hureyra is the origin of herding. | Refused | Origin narratives belong to broader synthesis. |
