# Catastrophic Snapshot Custody Gate

## Gate Rule

Disaster preservation strengthens a material claim but does not make the site a
complete frozen moment. Each claim must state which preservation layer anchors
it and which missing layer remains absent.

## Evidence Layers

| Layer | Can support | Cannot support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Ash burial | Preservation and destruction context. | Complete final day or whole society. |
| Architecture | Building form and urban planning where documented. | Complete social organization. |
| Wall paintings | Imagery, technique, and display context. | Ritual certainty or belief system. |
| Pottery/vessels | Use surfaces and object categories. | Complete household inventory. |
| Everyday objects | Activity traces where context is documented. | Full daily life. |
| Absent bodies | A question about evacuation, recovery, or preservation bias. | Certain escape story. |
| Eruption sequence | Geological and archaeological process when source-locked. | Single cinematic catastrophe narrative. |
| Public comparison | Reception and memory. | Evidence equivalence to Pompeii or Atlantis. |

## Publication Test

Before publication, each Akrotiri claim must answer:

1. Is the claim about preservation, destruction, object context, evacuation,
   ritual, history, or public memory?
2. Does the claim depend on absence, such as no bodies or missing valuables?
3. Does the claim require CERES, RITE, PORTO, CANON, MAXIM/LUCIA, or script
   handoff?
4. Is the exact claim being made, refused, or demoted to P3?
