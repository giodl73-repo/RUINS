# Claim Audit: Akrotiri

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Akrotiri was preserved by volcanic ash after eruption-related destruction. | A | NAM source lock supports broad preservation and eruption context. |
| The site preserves architecture, wall paintings, pottery, vessels, and everyday objects in strong condition. | A | NAM source lock supports these evidence categories. |
| Akrotiri can be used as a catastrophic-preservation case for RUINS. | A | Evidence condition is direct and reusable. |
| Akrotiri records a complete final day. | Refused | Preservation does not equal full moment. |
| Absent bodies prove a certain evacuation story. | Refused | Absence is an inference surface, not certainty. |
| Wall paintings prove a full ritual or belief system. | Refused | Requires RITE and specialist source custody. |
| The eruption explains Minoan collapse or Atlantis. | Refused | These are cultural-memory or broader-history claims outside current scope. |
