# Plan: Akrotiri Catastrophic Snapshot Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a disaster-preserved settlement when excellent
preservation tempts readers to infer a complete frozen day, complete social
system, or certain evacuation story?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Akrotiri as a Bronze Age settlement on Thera/Santorini preserved by volcanic
  ash.
- Preserved architecture, multi-storeyed buildings, wall paintings, pottery,
  vessels, and everyday objects as evidence categories.
- The "Pompeii of the Aegean" comparison as cultural memory and overclaim
  pressure.

Refused:
- Exact final day.
- Certain evacuation story from absent bodies alone.
- Complete household inventories or whole daily-life reconstruction.
- Full ritual system from wall paintings.
- Atlantis or civilizational-collapse explanation.
- Exact eruption chronology or geological modeling beyond source lock.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Catastrophic-snapshot custody gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.

## Promotion Target

Fast-promote `ruins-capability:catastrophic-snapshot-preservation-custody` from
Candidate to Active if the package blocks frozen-moment and complete-life
overclaims while preserving the strength of ash-burial evidence.
