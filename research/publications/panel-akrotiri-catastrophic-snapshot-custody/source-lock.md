# Source Lock: Akrotiri

## Locked For Current Claims

| Source | Locked claim |
|---|---|
| National Archaeological Museum, `Collection of Antiquities of Thera` | Akrotiri developed into an urban Bronze Age settlement with advanced planning, multi-storeyed architecture, high-quality wall paintings, pottery, vessels, and objects preserved in remarkably good condition by volcanic ash. |
| National Archaeological Museum, `Collection of Antiquities of Thera` | The museum itself uses the "Pompeii of the Aegean" public comparison. |
| Hellenic Ministry of Culture/Odysseus Akrotiri page | Akrotiri is an official archaeological site on Thera/Santorini. |
| Dartmouth Aegean Prehistoric Archaeology lesson | Pre-eruption damage, clearance, and repair/demolition claims require separate source custody and should not become a simple final-moment story. |
| Eos/American Geophysical Union article | Absent bodies and evacuation are public-science inference surfaces, not current-scope certainty. |

## Not Locked

| Surface | Status |
|---|---|
| Exact final day | Refused. |
| Certain evacuation story | Refused. |
| Complete household inventory | Refused. |
| Full ritual meaning of wall paintings | Refused. |
| Atlantis link | Cultural memory only. |

| Exact eruption chronology/geology | CERES handoff. |

## Source Posture

The paper is publication-ready because it uses official and educational sources
for broad preservation/custody claims while refusing exact final-moment,
geological, ritual, and historical synthesis claims.
