# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Ancient fingerprints are a RUINS biometric-trace case. | Strong | The evidence is a preserved human impression used for reconstruction. |
| Ridge density can support broad demographic inference. | Moderate | It depends on measurement, reference data, and uncertainty controls. |
| A print identifies a named maker. | Refused | No identity lock exists from ridge metrics alone. |
| Ridge density proves certain sex for each print. | Refused | Category inference is probabilistic and reference-dependent. |
| Clay shrinkage matters to biometric classification. | Strong | Experimental work shows dimensional change can shift categories. |
| Fingerprint distributions can inform labor-division hypotheses. | Moderate | Useful only as bounded assemblage-level evidence. |
| Fingerprints prove a complete gender system. | Refused | Labor organization requires broader social and archaeological custody. |
