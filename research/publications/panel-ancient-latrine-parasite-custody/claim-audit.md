# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Ancient latrine parasite DNA is a paleoparasitology custody case. | Strong | It combines parasite, host, plant, animal, and deposit evidence. |
| Parasite and diet DNA can inform past health and food components. | Moderate | Useful only with deposit, host, and assignment custody. |
| A parasite egg proves individual disease diagnosis. | Refused | Infection, disease, and diagnosis are separate layers. |
| Plant and animal DNA reconstruct complete diet. | Refused | DNA signals are components and may include contamination or passage. |
| Parasite distribution proves migration route or trade. | Refused | Movement requires independent geography and historical custody. |
| Latrine evidence reconstructs a sanitation system. | Refused | Waste infrastructure needs broader site and urban evidence. |
| Ancient waste stories are direct social history. | Refused | Public memory must be separated from deposit evidence. |
