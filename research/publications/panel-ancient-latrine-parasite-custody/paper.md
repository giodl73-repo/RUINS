# Ancient Latrines and Paleoparasitology Custody

The 2018 PLOS ONE ancient-latrine DNA paper is a strong RUINS trigger because
it turns archaeological waste deposits into evidence for parasites, diet,
human-animal interaction, and sanitation. The study uses parasite egg
pre-concentration from ancient latrines and deposits, followed by shotgun
sequencing, to profile parasite, vertebrate, and plant DNA from Northern Europe
and the Middle East.

This evidence is unusually rich, but it is not a direct public-health ledger.
Latrine sediment and coprolites mix deposit formation, preservation, sampling,
parasite biology, host specificity, DNA assignment, diet passage, environmental
contamination, and site history. RUINS therefore treats every parasite,
pathogen, plant, animal, and sanitation claim as separately gated.

RUINS promotes paleoparasitology latrine custody to Core here. Ancient latrine
signals can support bounded parasite, diet-component, and human-animal
proximity claims, but they cannot become individual disease diagnosis, complete
diet, migration route, trade proof, sanitation system, animal-management system,
or public-health history without separate custody.
