# Ancient Pathogen Genome Custody

Ancient pathogen genomics can identify microbial DNA in human remains and place
old strains in evolutionary history. Dental pulp, bone, calculus, metagenomic
reads, capture-enriched sequences, SNPs, plasmids, and virulence markers can
make disease history visible where texts are absent or uncertain.

That visibility is not a full epidemiological history. A pathogen read, genome,
phylogenetic branch, virulence marker, or mass-burial association can support
bounded claims, but it cannot by itself become complete disease diagnosis,
certain cause of death, proven outbreak, pandemic origin, exact transmission
route, vector certainty, host reservoir, population collapse, migration proof,
immune-selection history, historical event attribution, or solved plague story.

The controlling RUINS move is to separate sample, skeletal context, screening
method, authentication, contamination, genome coverage, taxon assignment, strain
placement, virulence marker, host relation, chronology, epidemiological scale,
historical synthesis, and public disease memory. Plague genome papers show the
pressure clearly: the same sequence data can anchor pathogen presence while
leaving diagnosis, death, transmission, and social impact future-locked.

This paper promotes `ruins-capability:ancient-pathogen-genome-custody` to Core.
