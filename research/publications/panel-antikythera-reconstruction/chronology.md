# Chronology: Antikythera Reconstruction

**Owner:** ABSENCE HISTORIAN
**Status:** working chronology

This chronology separates ancient manufacture, shipwreck survival, modern
recovery, imaging, and reconstruction.

| Date / period | Layer | RUINS note |
|---|---|---|
| 2nd-1st century BCE | Manufacture / use horizon | Broad working period for the mechanism; exact construction date and calibration proposals require source lock. |
| 1st century BCE | Shipwreck context | The object enters a wreck assemblage; shipwreck cargo context is a separate claim surface. |
| 1900-1901 | Recovery | Divers recover the wreck material near Antikythera; the mechanism is initially a corroded fragmentary object. |
| 1902 | Recognition of gearwork | A gear-bearing fragment is recognized, shifting the object from lump of bronze to technological artifact. |
| 1950s-1970s | Early modern study and radiography | Price and collaborators make the mechanism a major history-of-technology case. |
| 2005-2006 | CT and surface imaging | New imaging reveals hidden gears and inscriptions, changing the reconstruction surface. |
| 2008 | Inscriptions and games-dial interpretation | Inscriptions expand functional interpretation beyond simple astronomical display. |
| 2021 | Cosmos/front-display model | A modern model proposes a fuller front display; RUINS treats it as reconstruction under assumptions. |
| Ongoing | Public memory and replicas | The mechanism becomes an emblem of lost ancient technology and "ancient computer" wonder. |

## Process Model

RUINS tracks:

1. surviving object,
2. corrosion and fragmentation,
3. imaging surfaces,
4. inscription reading,
5. gear-train reconstruction,
6. physical replicas,
7. public technological mythology.
