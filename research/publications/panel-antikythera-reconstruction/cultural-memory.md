# Cultural Memory Register: Antikythera Reconstruction

**Owner:** RELIQUARY
**Status:** first example register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This register records how the Antikythera mechanism circulates after modern
recovery and reconstruction. These entries are not evidence for gear layouts or
inscription readings. They are evidence for how a damaged mechanism became a
symbol of lost ancient technology.

## Examples

| Memory form | Example surface | What it does | RUINS use | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "World's first computer" framing | Public summaries, museum-facing writing, and media essays call the mechanism an ancient or first computer. | Makes the object legible through modern computing language. | Shows why RUINS must define "computer" as analog astronomical calculator shorthand. | Not authority for detailed mechanism claims. |
| Google Doodle / public spectacle | Google commemorated the discovery anniversary with a Doodle. | Converts a specialist artifact into a mass public memory object. | Evidence that the mechanism functions as public technological heritage. | Not evidence for operation or dating. |
| Lost-technology narrative | Popular writing stresses sophistication unmatched until much later. | Makes the object stand for technological discontinuity. | Useful as afterlife evidence and a claim-risk signal. | Does not prove a continuous lost industry. |
| Replica and model spectacle | Public videos, museum displays, and reconstructions make the mechanism visually complete. | Lets viewers see a working object rather than a fragmentary artifact. | Helps explain why reconstructions can feel more certain than the evidence. | Replica operation is not original identity. |
| Genius-object framing | The mechanism is often treated as a singular marvel. | Intensifies wonder and can erase workshop, tradition, and uncertainty. | Prompts RUINS to separate artifact, craft context, and inference. | Does not settle production ecology. |

## Paper Rules

1. Cultural-memory examples may explain why the artifact attracts strong
   claims.
2. They may not upgrade a gear, inscription, dating, or front-display claim.
3. When public writing says "computer," RUINS should specify analog
   astronomical calculator.
4. When public writing shows a complete mechanism, RUINS should identify
   whether it is a replica, model, animation, or surviving fragment.

## Review Result

For this paper, later memory is treated as afterlife evidence only. None of the
memory examples are used to strengthen a reconstruction, gear count, or
inscription claim.
