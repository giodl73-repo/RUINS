# Refusal Register: Antikythera Reconstruction

**Owner:** ERRATA / Safety Editor
**Status:** publication-scope refusal register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This register turns the paper's refusals into operational rules. A refusal is a
named boundary where artifact, scan, model, replica, or public memory does not
support a stronger claim.

## Refusal Rules

| Tempting claim | RUINS response | Reason | Allowed substitute |
|---|---|---|---|
| "The replica is the mechanism." | Refuse | A replica is a modern test object. | "This reconstruction models one proposed operation." |
| "The 2021 model solves the front display." | Refuse as closure | It is a model under constraints, not the ancient object. | "The 2021 paper proposes a front Cosmos model." |
| "All gears are known." | Refuse | Some components survive, some are scan-detected, and some are inferred. | "Current models distinguish preserved, imaged, and inferred components." |
| "CT data is direct bronze." | Refuse | Imaging is an analytic evidence surface. | "CT reveals hidden structure interpreted from scans." |
| "The mechanism proves a lost industrial tradition." | Refuse as current claim | Broader production ecology needs independent evidence. | "It suggests sophisticated ancient mechanical knowledge." |
| "Ancient computer means modern computer." | Refuse literally | The phrase is shorthand and can mislead. | "Analog astronomical calculator." |
| "NYU collection custody permits item-level claims." | Refuse without item audit | Collection-level custody is not item-level verification. | "The collection is a custody target." |

## Escalation Rule

Any sentence that treats a model, diagram, replica, animation, or dataset item
as direct ancient evidence must be downgraded, sourced as a reconstruction, or
removed.

## Current Scope Check

The current paper makes no detailed gear-layout claim, no inscription-reading
claim, no final-front-display claim, and no item-level dataset claim.
