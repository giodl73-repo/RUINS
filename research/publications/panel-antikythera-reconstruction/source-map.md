## Evidence Surfaces

| Surface | Evidence condition | RUINS use |
|---|---|---|
| Bronze fragments in the National Archaeological Museum, Athens | direct material evidence through conservation and museum custody | Establish what survives. |
| Visible gears, plates, dials, and inscriptions | direct material plus conservation observation | Separate observed mechanics from inferred systems. |
| X-ray, gamma-ray, CT, and surface imaging | analytic evidence surface | Reveal hidden gears and inscriptions without becoming direct ancient material. |
| Gear-train diagrams and mathematical models | reconstruction / analytic model | Explain plausible operation under stated assumptions. |
| Physical replicas and working models | modern reconstruction | Test feasibility; not identical to the ancient object. |
| Public "ancient computer" coverage | cultural memory | Track technological wonder and discontinuity narratives. |
