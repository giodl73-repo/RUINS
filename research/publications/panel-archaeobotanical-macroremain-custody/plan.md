# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS score papers that use archaeobotanical macroremains to
reconstruct diet, crop processing, storage, agriculture, or first-farming
stories?

## Scope

- Charred seeds, cereal grains, chaff, rachis segments, spikelet forks, weed
  seeds, fruits, nutshells, pulses, flotation samples, and heavy fractions.
- Waterlogged, desiccated, mineralized, charred, contaminated, and redeposited
  preservation pathways.
- Taxon assignment, crop/weed status, crop-processing stage, quantification,
  spatial pattern, seasonality, and public plant-food memory.

## Out Of Scope

- No complete diet reconstruction.
- No direct domestication proof from one assemblage.
- No final crop-processing or storage model.
- No market, migration, famine, ritual, or whole-economy claim.
- No botanical taxonomy handbook.

## Target Artifact

Promote archaeobotanical macroremain custody directly to Core with a checklist,
gate, refusal register, sibling-boundary register, and rubric v1.62 cap.
