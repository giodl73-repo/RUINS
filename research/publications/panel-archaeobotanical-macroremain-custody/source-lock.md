# Source Lock

## Locked Claims

- Archaeobotany studies preserved plant remains, including macroremains such as
  seeds, fruits, plant parts, wood, and charcoal.
- Macroremains can be charred, waterlogged, desiccated, or mineralized.
- Flotation and sampling methods affect recovered assemblages.
- Charred seed assemblages require taphonomic and crop-processing controls.
- Immature grain ratios and crop-processing models can inform but not close
  harvest or processing claims by themselves.
