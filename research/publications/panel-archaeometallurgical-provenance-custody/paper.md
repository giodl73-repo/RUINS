# Archaeometallurgy and Provenance Custody

Metallurgical evidence can make ancient production and movement newly legible.
Lead isotopes, trace elements, slag inclusions, microstructures, furnace debris,
and ore databases can constrain possible source fields, production stages, and
technology choices.

That constraint is not a direct route map. A lead-isotope match, slag chemistry
cluster, XRF result, or SEM-EDS phase can support bounded compatibility claims,
but it cannot by itself become an exact mine, direct trade route, certain
workshop, complete smelting recipe, production scale, political economy,
ethnic or craft identity, recycling history, technological superiority, or
solved metal circulation.

The controlling RUINS move is to separate sample, object/context, ore field,
analytical method, contamination and corrosion, recycling and mixing, isotope
overlap, reference database, chaîne opératoire, production stage, provenance
probability, exchange inference, and public memory. The same pressure appears
in lead-isotope reviews, overlap studies, slag-inclusion provenance work,
metal-composition papers, and slag-characterization studies.

This paper promotes `ruins-capability:archaeometallurgical-provenance-custody`
to Core.
