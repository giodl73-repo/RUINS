# Sibling Boundary

| Surface | Owner | RUINS boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Artefacts, samples, sites, assemblages, datasets, mines, and named workshops | CANON | RUINS gates evidence use but does not own entity identity. |
| Chemistry, isotope measurement, microscopy, mineralogy, corrosion, smelting technology, and database construction | CERES | RUINS records custody controls, not laboratory method implementation. |
| Ore fields, routes, and regional geography | PORTO | RUINS does not own route geography. |
| Exchange, political economy, production organization, and technological history | MAXIM/LUCIA | RUINS blocks overclaiming and hands synthesis away. |
| Ethnic or craft identity | Identity systems | RUINS refuses chemistry-as-identity claims. |
| Ancient-industry and trade-network narratives | Public-history systems | RUINS contains public memory. |
