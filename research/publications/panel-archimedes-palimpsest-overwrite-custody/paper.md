# Archimedes Palimpsest Overwrite Custody

**Status:** publication-ready
**Track:** Evidence and Absence

## Abstract

The Archimedes Palimpsest is not simply a lost book recovered by technology. It
is a prayer book made from scraped and reused parchment, carrying erased older
texts beneath later liturgical writing. Some pages are missing, some areas were
damaged by mold, some text was covered by later forged paintings, and modern
recovery required conservation, multispectral imaging, X-ray fluorescence,
post-processing, and scholarly reading. RUINS treats the case as overwrite
custody: recovery is a layered evidence chain, not an automatic restoration of
an original manuscript.

## Evidence Layers

## Source-Locked Synthesis

The Archimedes Palimpsest Project identifies the manuscript as a thirteenth
century prayer book containing erased earlier texts, including Archimedes
treatises found nowhere else, and records the 1998 auction, Walters deposit,
conservation, imaging, scholarship, and web publication of raw imaging data and
transcriptions. The project history records a tenth-century Constantinople
Archimedes manuscript, a 1229 Jerusalem prayer book, later movement, Heiberg's
1906 study, missing pages, severe mold, forged paintings, and the 1999 Walters
conservation/imaging campaign.

The imaging sources show why RUINS must gate recovery claims. Multispectral
imaging and X-ray fluorescence do not "make the original simply visible." XRF
maps element signals such as iron in ink, can help separate overlapping texts,
and was used to read through forged paintings, but it still requires
processing, comparison, and scholarly interpretation.

## What RUINS Refuses

| Overclaim | RUINS response |
|---|---|
| Imaging fully restores the original Archimedes manuscript. | It recovers signals and readings from damaged overwritten leaves. |
| The prayer book is just a disguise. | The euchologion is also the surviving object. |
| Every missing or mold-damaged text is recoverable. | Missing leaves and damaged areas remain separate loss layers. |
| An XRF image is a transcription. | It is an evidence surface for scholar reading. |
| Public web data equals complete scholarly closure. | Data release improves access; it does not settle every reading. |
| The case proves a simple "lost genius rediscovered" story. | The recovery is material, technical, scholarly, and cultural. |

## Method Result

Archimedes activates palimpsest overwrite custody. The reusable RUINS method is
to keep the overwritten artifact whole: undertext, overtext, damage,
conservation, imaging, processing, transcription, edition, and memory are all
necessary, and none can stand in for the others.
