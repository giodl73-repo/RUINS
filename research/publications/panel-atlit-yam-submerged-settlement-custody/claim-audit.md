# Claim Audit: Atlit-Yam

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Atlit-Yam is a submerged Neolithic settlement off the Carmel coast. | A | IAA and peer-reviewed sources support this. |
| The site preserves in-situ features including wells, buildings, graves, tools, and organic remains. | A | Source-locked feature categories support this. |
| Atlit-Yam provides an unlike test against Doggerland's basin-scale proxy case. | A | Evidence conditions differ clearly. |
| The settlement economy mixed farming, fishing, and other resource use. | B | Supported by synthesis, but exact quantification remains technical. |
| The site proves a complete social system or village plan. | Refused | Feature survival is not total recovery. |
| The site proves a single cause of abandonment. | Refused | Sea-level process and site history require separate modeling/source lock. |
| Human remains prove population identity, ancestry, or full disease history. | Refused | Requires medical and GENES custody. |
| The megalithic feature has certain ritual meaning. | Refused | RUINS records the interpretive pressure without making the ritual claim. |
