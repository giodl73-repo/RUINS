# Cultural Memory: Atlit-Yam

Atlit-Yam often appears in public as a spectacular submerged village. RUINS
keeps that spectacle separate from evidence.

## Memory Surfaces

| Memory surface | RUINS handling |
|---|---|
| Atlantis analogy | Refused as evidence and recorded as overclaim pressure. |
| Earliest-well or oldest-village phrasing | Allowed only when source-locked and scoped. |
| Disease-first storytelling | Refused unless medical source custody supports it. |
| Climate-change parable | Modern reception, not Neolithic motive or event evidence. |
| Underwater images | Display and documentation evidence, not total site recovery. |

## Rule

Atlit-Yam may illuminate why submerged archaeology matters now. That does not
make the site a complete narrative of climate loss, health, ritual, or social
identity.
