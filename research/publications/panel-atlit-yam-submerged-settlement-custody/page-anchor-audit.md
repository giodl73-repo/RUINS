# Page Anchor Audit: Atlit-Yam

## Anchored Claims

| Claim | Anchor posture | Current grade |
|---|---|---|
| Atlit-Yam is a submerged Neolithic site off the Carmel coast. | IAA report and peer-reviewed site reports. | Strong. |
| The site preserves in-situ settlement features including wells, buildings, and graves. | IAA and Galili reports. | Strong. |
| Organic, faunal, botanical, and tool evidence support mixed economy claims. | IAA and peer-reviewed synthesis. | Strong but method-bound. |
| The site informs sea-level change and submerged coastal settlement study. | IAA and peer-reviewed sources. | Strong. |
| Complete social history, ritual meaning, abandonment cause, and disease story are current-scope refusals. | Refusal register. | Strong refusal. |

## Anchor Limits

- Feature lists do not equal complete settlement plans.
- Human remains do not equal population identity or biography.
- Disease references require medical/osteological source custody before
  synthesis.
- Sea-level relevance does not create a single disaster narrative.
