# Plan: Atlit-Yam Submerged Settlement Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS score a submerged site when the evidence is not only proxy
mapping but direct underwater excavation of in-situ settlement remains?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Atlit-Yam as a submerged Neolithic settlement off the Carmel coast.
- Excavated evidence categories: wells, buildings, graves, megalithic features,
  tools, animal remains, fish, plant remains, and sea-level context.
- The contrast between Doggerland's basin-scale proxy evidence and Atlit-Yam's
  in-situ underwater settlement evidence.

Refused:
- Complete village plan or social system.
- Named culture identity beyond source wording.
- Certain cause of abandonment.
- Disease, ritual, or demographic conclusions beyond source lock.
- Atlantis, lost-civilization, or simple climate-parable framing.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Submerged-settlement custody gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.
9. Cross-case checklist.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:submerged-landscape-proxy-custody` to Core by pairing
Doggerland's proxy landscape with Atlit-Yam's in-situ submerged settlement and
adding a reusable checklist plus rubric v1.16 cap.
