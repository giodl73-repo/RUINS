# Source Lock: Atlit-Yam

## Locked For Current Claims

| Source | Locked claim |
|---|---|
| Israel Antiquities Authority, 2013 Atlit-Yam report | Atlit-Yam was underwater-excavated; it is a submerged Pre-Pottery Neolithic C site off the Carmel coast with wells, graves, buildings, megalithic features, tools, animal remains, fish, plant remains, and sea-level context. |
| Galili et al., Journal of Field Archaeology, 1993 | Atlit-Yam is a deeply submerged prehistoric settlement with strong preservation of architecture, material culture, animal/botanical remains, human burials, and evidence relevant to maritime activity, wells, and sea-level change. |
| Galili et al., Springer Coastal Research Library, 2017 | Atlit-Yam is a well-preserved submerged prehistoric settlement with material culture and organic remains associated with a mixed farming and fishing economy. |
| Harvard White-Levy project page | Regional underwater archaeology has identified multiple submerged prehistoric sites, with Atlit-Yam as the older, deeper offshore site in the Carmel-coast sequence. |
