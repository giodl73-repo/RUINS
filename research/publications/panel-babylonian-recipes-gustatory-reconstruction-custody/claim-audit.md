# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Babylonian recipes are a gustatory-reconstruction custody case. | Strong | Yale and Harvard anchor tablets, translation, reconstruction, and public recipes. |
| Public Yale recipes are exact ancient dishes. | Refused | Modern formatting, substitutions, quantities, and safety choices are access layers. |
| The tablets preserve recipe witnesses. | Strong | Public sources anchor clay-tablet recipe preservation. |
| The tablets provide complete cooking instructions. | Refused | Missing measures, timing, heat, and context remain separate. |
| Experimental dishes are direct Babylonian taste. | Refused | Modern ingredients, kitchen, techniques, and palate intervene. |
| "Oldest recipe" language is useful public memory. | Moderate | Acceptable only when kept separate from exact taste and safety claims. |
