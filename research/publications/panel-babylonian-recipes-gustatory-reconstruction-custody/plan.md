# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS grade an ancient dish reconstructed from a clay-tablet recipe
when modern public recipes and cooking videos make it seem directly recoverable?

## Evidence Surfaces

- Yale Babylonian recipe tablets.
- Akkadian text and translation.
- Terse recipe instructions.
- Missing measures, quantities, temperatures, and timing.
- Ingredient substitution and modern equivalents.
- Experimental cooking.
- Public recipe and video access.
- Oldest-recipe memory.

## Exclusions

- Exact Akkadian edition.
- Full Mesopotamian culinary history.
- Modern cooking instructions or food safety advice.
- Final ingredient identifications.
- Exact taste, texture, or serving context.

## Promotion Target

Use Babylonian recipes as the unlike second test for
`ruins-capability:gustatory-reconstruction-custody`, then promote the capability
to Core with a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.30 cap.
