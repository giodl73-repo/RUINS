# Chewed Birch Pitch and Masticated Resin Custody

The Syltholm birch-pitch paper is a clean RUINS trigger because a small chewed
object carries several evidence classes at once. The 2019 Nature Communications
study reports a complete ancient human genome and oral microbiome from a
5,700-year-old piece of chewed birch pitch from Denmark, along with microbial,
viral, plant, and animal DNA signals.

The case is powerful because it does not require bone or teeth to recover human
biomolecular evidence. It is also risky because the public story turns easily
into "ancient chewing gum" and then into a full person, face, last meal, illness,
medical treatment, adhesive technology, and Stone Age lifestyle.

RUINS promotes masticated resin biomolecular custody to Core here. A chewed
resin object can support bounded molecular, oral-microbiome, diet-exposure, and
technology-context claims, but it cannot become complete personhood, exact last
meal, disease diagnosis, direct medical treatment, whole diet, complete
toolmaking system, population history, or lifestyle synthesis without separate
custody.
