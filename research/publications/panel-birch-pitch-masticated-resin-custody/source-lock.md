# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- The Nature Communications paper reports a complete ancient human genome and
  oral microbiome from a 5,700-year-old chewed birch-pitch object from Denmark.
- The paper identifies microbial, viral, plant, and animal DNA, including
  signals that may derive from a recent meal.
- The DTU record reports average human-genome coverage of 2.3x and summarizes
  phenotype, oral microbiome, Epstein-Barr virus, animal DNA, and plant DNA
  findings.
- The later Huseby Klev paper treats prehistoric chewed pitch as a source of
  ancient DNA from humans and microbiomes and frames oral-health inference as
  metagenomic analysis.

Future-locked:

- Exact last meal.
- Complete individual biography or personhood.
- Disease diagnosis from microbial or viral signal alone.
- Direct medical treatment from pitch chewing.
- Complete toolmaking system or lifestyle.
- Population-history synthesis from one object.
