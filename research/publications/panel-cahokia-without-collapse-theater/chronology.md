# Chronology: Cahokia Without Collapse Theater

**Owner:** ABSENCE HISTORIAN
**Status:** working chronology

This chronology separates occupation, urban growth, abandonment, environmental
interpretation, and modern memory.

| Date / period | Layer | RUINS note |
|---|---|---|
| before 1050 CE | Pre-urban and regional context | Earlier occupation and regional networks require source-lock before detailed claims. |
| ca. 1050 CE | Urban transformation / "Big Bang" target | Cahokia's rapid growth is a major archaeological question, not a simple origin story. |
| ca. 1100-1200 CE | Peak urban and monumental phase | Population, mound count, and territorial influence vary by source and method. |
| 12th-13th centuries CE | Social and environmental stress hypotheses | Flooding, drought, resource use, political change, violence, and migration are separate claim surfaces. |
| ca. 1300-1350 CE | Abandonment / transformation | RUINS treats abandonment as population movement and institutional change, not disappearance. |
| 19th-20th centuries | Damage, excavation, preservation | Agriculture, urban development, excavation, and park formation alter what survives. |
| 20th-21st centuries | Public heritage and memory | Cahokia becomes a World Heritage/site-interpretation object and public counterexample to "no cities north of Mexico" assumptions. |

## Process Model

RUINS tracks:

1. earthwork and settlement survival,
2. excavation and preservation bias,
3. demographic reconstruction,
4. environmental proxy interpretation,
5. abandonment and movement,
6. public collapse/mystery storytelling,
7. Indigenous continuity and heritage context.
