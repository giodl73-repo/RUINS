# Custody Register: Cahokia Without Collapse Theater

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST
**Status:** first custody register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

## Evidence Surfaces

| Surface | Custody state | Paper use | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surviving mounds and earthworks | Publicly interpreted through UNESCO and Illinois site materials; direct landscape remains survive under state/heritage custody. | Establish material survival and public heritage status. | Does not explain abandonment by itself. |
| Monks Mound / Woodhenge / palisade features | Public-site descriptions and interpretive features. | Use as named examples of visible/public interpretation layers. | Detailed construction, dating, or ritual claims need feature-level literature. |
| Excavated artifacts, burials, household traces | Not locked at feature level in this pass. | Mention only as evidence classes. | No specific burial, object, or household claim without page audit and sensitivity review. |
| Flood and sediment proxies | Locked through Munoz et al. for flood-frequency hypothesis and Rankin et al. for local flooding/ecocide critique. | Discuss as environmental proxies and model inputs. | Do not treat as direct testimony or single-cause proof. |
| Demographic / fecal-stanol reconstruction | Locked through White et al. for Horseshoe Lake watershed post-Mississippian reconstruction. | Challenge disappearance language and show process after Cahokia. | Watershed model does not map all communities or descendant identities. |
| Public memory and heritage interpretation | Locked through UNESCO, Illinois, Berkeley, WashU, and media-facing source language. | Track "lost city," "first city," "collapse," and "vanishing" frames. | Public memory is evidence of afterlife, not of Cahokian self-description. |

## Custody Risks

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Using public-site values as if they were excavation data | Label values as UNESCO/Illinois public-site values. |
| Treating one proxy record as the city | Keep proxy records in environmental/model layer. |
| Repeating "vanished civilization" language | Route through refusal register and cultural-memory register. |
| Overusing Mound 72 or burial examples | Keep feature-level evidence out of current paper unless separately audited. |
| Flattening Indigenous continuity into a single line | Use continuity/repopulation wording only where source-locked and scoped. |

## Custody Decision

This pass is cleared for a stable working paper about evidence boundaries and
collapse-language refusal. It is not cleared for a detailed excavation history,
object inventory, burial interpretation, descendant-community mapping, or
general Mississippian synthesis.
