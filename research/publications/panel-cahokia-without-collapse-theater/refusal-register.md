# Refusal Register: Cahokia Without Collapse Theater

**Owner:** ERRATA / Safety Editor
**Status:** first register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

## Refused Claims

| Claim | Refusal | Allowed substitute |
|---|---|---|
| Cahokia collapsed because of one cause. | Single-cause collapse exceeds the locked source set. | Cahokia's abandonment/transformation is debated across environmental, demographic, political, and social models. |
| Flooding caused the collapse. | Flood-frequency correlation is a hypothesis input, not direct causal closure. | Flood-frequency shifts are one source-locked environmental hypothesis. |
| Wood overuse caused flooding and collapse. | Stratigraphic research challenges this narrative. | Wood-overuse/flooding is a contested model with scant supporting evidence in the locked source set. |
| The people vanished. | Disappearance language erases continuity, movement, and later regional presence. | Cahokia's urban center was abandoned/transformed; post-Mississippian population history must be scoped by source. |
| Mound 72 explains the whole society. | A feature-level burial context cannot stand for a whole urban system. | Mound 72 requires separate feature-level audit and sensitivity review. |
| Public-site numbers are final archaeological values. | Public-site values are useful but source-specific. | Label UNESCO/Illinois values and do not combine them into unsourced precision. |
| Heritage recognition proves original political structure. | World Heritage status is modern public custody. | Use heritage status for cultural memory and preservation history. |

## Phrase Controls

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Cahokia mysteriously vanished" | "Cahokia's urban occupation ended, while later population history is source-specific" |
| "Cahokia collapsed from flooding" | "flood-frequency changes are one environmental hypothesis" |
| "deforestation destroyed the city" | "wood-overuse collapse narratives are contested" |
| "the first American city proves..." | "public heritage framing often presents Cahokia as a major Indigenous urban center" |
