# Source Lock: Cahokia Without Collapse Theater

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST
**Status:** page-audited source lock
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This source lock defines what RUINS may say about Cahokia in this paper. It
separates public-site custody, research-paper hypotheses, and cultural-memory
framing. A source is locked for bounded use only; it is not a license to make a
single-cause collapse claim.

## Paper Use Rules

1. Use population, date, area, and mound values only with source labels.
2. Say "abandonment," "transformation," "depopulation," or "repopulation"
   when the source supports process language; do not default to collapse.
3. Treat flood, drought, resource stress, and wood-overuse claims as hypotheses
   or contested models unless a source supports a narrower statement.
4. Do not convert public-site summaries into detailed archaeological evidence.
5. Do not say Cahokia's people vanished.
6. Treat "America's first city" and "lost civilization" as public-memory
   language, not as direct evidence of Cahokian self-description.
7. Use [`page-audit.md`](page-audit.md) for current source-locked anchors.
