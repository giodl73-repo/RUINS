# Claim Audit: Cairo Genizah

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Cairo Genizah was a sacred storeroom connected with Fustat community manuscript storage. | A | Cambridge Digital Library source lock supports this. |
| Taylor-Schechter is the largest and most important single modern collection of medieval Jewish manuscripts. | A | Cambridge University Library source lock supports this institutional claim. |
| Digital projects make images, catalogue data, identifications, and transcriptions available. | A | Friedberg and Princeton sources support this access/custody claim. |
| The Genizah gives unusually rich evidence for medieval Jewish and Mediterranean life. | B | Broadly true but exact synthesis belongs to MAXIM/LUCIA. |
| The Genizah is a complete community archive. | Refused | Long-use storage is not complete representativeness. |
| Digital aggregation reunites the Genizah completely. | Refused | Digital access is not physical or interpretive reunion. |
| Any fragment can be quoted, joined, translated, or interpreted here. | Refused | Exact text work belongs to FONTES/MUNDUS. |
