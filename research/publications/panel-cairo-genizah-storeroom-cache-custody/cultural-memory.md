# Cultural Memory: Cairo Genizah

The Cairo Genizah often appears as treasure, sacred trash, or a recovered lost
world of medieval Mediterranean life. RUINS keeps those phrases as afterlife.

## Memory Surfaces

| Memory surface | RUINS handling |
|---|---|
| Sacred trash | Reception phrase; not a complete original category. |
| Treasure trove | Public discovery frame; may obscure fragment and custody limits. |
| Mediterranean society | Powerful scholarly synthesis; not a current RUINS claim. |
| Digital reunion | Access metaphor, not physical or interpretive completion. |
| Crowdsourced decipherment | Research process, not final edition. |

## Rule

Public and scholarly memory may explain why the Genizah matters. It cannot fill
original order, representativeness, exact readings, or complete social history.
