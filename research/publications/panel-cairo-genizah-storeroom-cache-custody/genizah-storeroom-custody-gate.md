# Genizah Storeroom Custody Gate

## Gate Rule

A genizah is a long-use storage and retirement practice, not automatically a
complete archive. Each claim must identify whether it rests on religious
storage practice, a fragment, a modern collection, a catalogue record, a digital
image, a transcription, or later public memory.

## Custody Layers

| Layer | Can support | Cannot support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Religious storeroom practice | Storage/retirement of texts by a community. | Complete communal archive. |
| Long accumulation | Broad temporal survival. | Even representation across centuries. |
| Fragment | Witness to a text/document surface. | Complete document or corpus. |
| Modern removal/collection | Modern custody and holding history. | Neutral provenance or original order. |
| Shelfmark/catalogue | Item identification and access. | Original arrangement. |
| Digital image | Access and comparison. | Scholarly edition or final reading. |
| Identification/transcription | Research aid or provisional reading where scoped. | Complete interpretation without edition custody. |
| Public memory | Reception and modern meaning. | Original function or representativeness. |

## Publication Test

Before publication, each Cairo Genizah claim must answer:

1. Is the claim about storeroom practice, a fragment, a document, a collection,
   a catalogue record, or a digital surrogate?
2. Does the claim require exact reading, join, translation, or social-history
   synthesis?
3. Does the claim confuse modern collection structure with original context?
4. Is the exact claim being made, refused, or demoted to P3?
