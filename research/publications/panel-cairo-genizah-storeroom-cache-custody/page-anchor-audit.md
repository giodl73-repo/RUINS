# Page Anchor Audit: Cairo Genizah

## Anchored Claims

| Claim | Anchor posture | Current grade |
|---|---|---|
| Cairo Genizah was a sacred storeroom connected with Fustat community practice. | Cambridge Digital Library. | Strong. |
| Taylor-Schechter is the largest and most important single modern collection of medieval Jewish manuscripts. | Cambridge University Library. | Strong as institutional claim. |
| Genizah fragments are now mediated by collection, shelfmark, catalogue, digital image, and identification systems. | Cambridge/Friedberg/Princeton sources. | Strong. |
| Digital projects aggregate images, metadata, identifications, and transcriptions. | Friedberg and Princeton sources. | Strong but access-bound. |
| Whole-community archive, complete social history, and digital reunion claims are current-scope refusals. | Refusal register. | Strong refusal. |

## Anchor Limits

- Collection scale does not prove original representativeness.
- Digital images and metadata do not equal final editions or translations.
- A shelfmark or catalogue record does not restore original order.
- Public "sacred trash" language is reception, not original classification.
