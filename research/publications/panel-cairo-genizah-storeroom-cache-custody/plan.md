# Plan: Cairo Genizah Storeroom Cache Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a long-use religious storeroom whose fragmentary
contents are abundant enough to tempt whole-community, whole-archive, and
complete social-history claims?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Cairo Genizah as a sacred storeroom connected with the Ben Ezra Synagogue in
  Fustat/Old Cairo.
- Cambridge Taylor-Schechter Genizah Collection as the largest single modern
  collection of medieval Jewish manuscripts.
- Fragment status, dispersal, cataloguing, digitization, and crowdsourced
  transcription as modern custody layers.

Refused:
- Complete archive of medieval Jewish life.
- Complete Mediterranean social history.
- Representative sample of every community, genre, language, or century.
- Exact readings, joins, translations, or legal-text interpretation.
- Digital reunification as physical or interpretive reunion.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Genizah-storeroom custody gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.
9. Cross-case sealed-cache checklist.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:sealed-cache-representativeness-custody` to Core by
pairing Dunhuang's sealed chamber with Cairo Genizah's long-use storeroom and
adding rubric v1.17 scoring controls.
