# Canopy LiDAR and Settlement Custody

Airborne LiDAR can make forested or vegetated archaeological landscapes newly
visible. It can reveal terrain patterns, platforms, roads, causeways,
reservoirs, canals, terraces, and field systems that ground survey did not see
at regional scale.

That visibility is not excavation. A point cloud, filtered ground return, or
hillshade is a mediated landscape record. It can support bounded feature
candidates and revised survey priorities, but it cannot by itself become a
complete settlement inventory, exact population estimate, direct political
organization, certain urban plan, complete road network, final chronology,
excavation-equivalent confirmation, environmental determinism, rediscovered
civilization, conservation-complete map, or solved collapse story.

The controlling RUINS move is to separate the sensor flight, point cloud, return
classification, canopy filtering, terrain model, visualization, feature
candidate, ground-truth sample, chronology, function, population model,
landscape boundary, publication image, and public memory. Maya lowland, Angkor,
Amazonian, and wetland-field cases show the same pressure: LiDAR can expand the
visible evidence field while making public discovery claims too easy.

This paper promotes `ruins-capability:canopy-lidar-settlement-custody` to Core.
