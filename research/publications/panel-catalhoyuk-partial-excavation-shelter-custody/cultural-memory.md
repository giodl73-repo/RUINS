# Cultural Memory: Catalhoyuk

Catalhoyuk's public memory is shaped by early city, egalitarian settlement,
Neolithic urbanism, wall paintings, crowded houses, roof access, and origins of
settled life. These frames are useful for explaining why the site matters. They
are also overclaim pressure.

RUINS may discuss that memory as reception. It may not use it to prove:

- complete settlement exposure,
- final settlement model,
- solved hierarchy,
- finished conservation,
- original-object custody from replicas,
- or a complete origin story for cities.

The important memory lesson is that visitor interpretation can be excellent and
still partial. A readable public route is not the whole archaeological record.
