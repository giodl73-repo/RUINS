# Plan: Catalhoyuk Partial Excavation Shelter Custody

## Scope

Use Catalhoyuk as the second case for partial excavation shelter custody after
Gobekli Tepe. The paper tests whether RUINS can promote the capability to Core
across monumental stone exposure and mudbrick settlement exposure.

## Current Claims

- Catalhoyuk is a Neolithic settlement with excavated remains preserved in situ.
- Two main excavation areas are protected by shelters.
- Visitor interpretation separates North Area neighbourhood views, South Area
  long-occupation overview, visitor centre exhibits, replica objects, and
  off-site original objects.
- The long Hodder project ended in 2018, records remain available, the physical
  archive is held off-site, and current excavation continues under new
  direction.
- Shelters reduce climate danger but do not end conservation work.

## Refused Claims

- The visitor route exposes the whole settlement.
- Shelters mean conservation is finished.
- Replica objects are original object custody.
- Current interpretation is the final settlement model.
- Catalhoyuk is a solved first city or complete origin story for urbanism.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:partial-excavation-shelter-custody` from Active to
Core by adding a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.21 cap.
