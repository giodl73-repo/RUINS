# Chaco Sun Dagger and Digital Restoration Custody

The Chaco Sun Dagger is unlike Newgrange because the key light-shadow relation
depends on slabs and petroglyphs whose condition and positions changed after
modern rediscovery. Public project records describe an interactive computer
model based on laser scanning that reproduces astronomical functioning in a
recreated setting, while chapter summaries note that visitor activity and
erosion caused slab shifts that changed light markings.

That makes the Sun Dagger a strong RUINS case for digitally restored
astronomical alignment. The model can preserve and test relationships between
slabs, spirals, sun, moon, light, and shadow. It cannot by itself become direct
ancient observation, exact calendar system, complete social function, or final
ritual meaning.

The cross-case rule is now stable: observed and modelled astronomical effects
must keep fabric, change history, sky model, marking, calendar inference,
meaning, and memory in separate custody.
