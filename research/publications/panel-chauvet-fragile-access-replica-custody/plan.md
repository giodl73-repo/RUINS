# Plan: Chauvet Fragile Access Replica Custody

## Scope

Use Chauvet as the second fragile-access case after Lascaux. The paper tests
whether RUINS needs a core method for fragile originals whose public knowledge
comes through restricted access, preventive conservation, replicas, and digital
or institutional mediation.

## Current Claims

- Chauvet was rediscovered in 1994 after long sealing and is treated by UNESCO
  as an exceptionally preserved decorated cave.
- The French state chose not to open the original cave to the public.
- Research and preservation access use strict routes, short visits, small
  numbers, and limited sampling.
- Grotte Chauvet 2 is a public replica, not direct access to the original.
- The replica is selective/condensed and depends on digital survey and
  production choices.

## Refused Claims

- Public visitors directly experience the original Chauvet cave.
- The replica is object-equivalent to the original.
- Conservation status is complete or risk-free.
- The image chronology and meaning are final.
- Chauvet alone explains Aurignacian culture or the origin of art.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:fragile-site-access-replica-custody` from Active to
Core by adding a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.19 cap.
