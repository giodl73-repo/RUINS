# Source Lock: Chauvet

## Locked For Current Scope

| Source | Locked claim |
|---|---|
| UNESCO World Heritage, Decorated Cave of Pont d'Arc | The cave contains early figurative drawings, more than one thousand inventoried images, and was sealed until rediscovery in 1994. |
| UNESCO World Heritage, Decorated Cave of Pont d'Arc | Integrity and authenticity depend on access restrictions, monitoring, and limited future expert/research visits. |
| French Ministry of Culture, Original preservation | The state decided not to open the cave to the public and organized preservation through legal protection and controlled movement. |
| French Ministry of Culture, Original preservation | Research access is preservation-constrained: compulsory routes, short visits, few people, no digging, limited probes and sampling. |
| French Ministry of Culture, A Grand Project | The replica was designed to share heritage through a large decorated-cave replica and to recreate an underground atmosphere. |
| French Ministry of Culture, A Grand Project | The replica could not reproduce the whole original cave; selected elements were built from digital survey, photos, and technical descriptions. |
| Grotte Chauvet 2 official site | The public visitor site presents itself as an astounding replica and the world's largest decorated-cave replica. |
