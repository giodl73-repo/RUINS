# Authenticity Report Audit: Dead Sea Scrolls Fragment Custody

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST / ERRATA
**Status:** current-scope authenticity-report audit
**Date:** 2026-07-05

This audit locks direct authenticity-report custody for the Museum of the Bible
Dead Sea Scroll fragment set. It authorizes claims about the MOTB collection
and the report's methods and conclusions. It does not authorize claims about
the whole Dead Sea Scrolls corpus, other post-2002 market fragments, or the
authenticity of any IAA / Leon Levy fragment.

## Report Target

| Report | Custody use | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| *Museum of the Bible Dead Sea Scroll Collection Scientific Research and Analysis*, final report, November 2019, Art Fraud Insights / Museum of the Bible. | Direct report custody for the MOTB 16-fragment authenticity finding, methods, and fragment-set boundary. | Applies only to the tested MOTB collection and its report. |

| Material / manipulation rationale | Report text extraction lines 40-42 discuss leather rather than typical DSS parchment, coatings identified as protein likely animal skin glue, and ink over preexisting deposits/cracks/delamination. | Use as high-level explanation of why the report reached a forgery conclusion. | Do not make fragment-specific laboratory claims without the report page/fragment section. |

## Current Decision

Direct authenticity-report custody is now complete for the MOTB 16-fragment set.
RUINS may say that the MOTB report concluded its tested DSS textual fragments
were not authentic and showed deliberate-forgery characteristics. RUINS must
continue to refuse whole-corpus forgery claims and must keep IAA / Leon Levy
image/metadata claims separate from MOTB authenticity findings.
