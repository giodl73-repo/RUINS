# Claim Audit: Dead Sea Scrolls Fragment Custody

**Owner:** ERRATA
**Status:** source-locked working audit

Use [`source-lock.md`](source-lock.md) for current claim boundaries and the
fragment-custody ladder.

## Claim Grades

| Claim | Max grade now | Audit note |
|---|---|---|
| Scroll fragments survive materially. | Observed / source-specific | IAA public framing supports thousands/tens of thousands of fragments; exact fragment claims need page audit. |
| A digital image can support a visual claim. | Source-specific | IAA/Leon Levy image custody supports mediated visual claims; source/image metadata must be named. |
| An edition can support a transcription claim. | Source-specific | Edition, page, line, and editorial conventions matter. |
| A translation is the ancient wording. | Refuse | Translation is interpretation. |
| A reconstructed gap is surviving text. | Refuse | Supplied text must be marked as reconstruction. |
| A fragment join is certain because an edition prints it. | Plausible at most | Join confidence needs source and evidence. |
| A market-fragment forgery result applies to all scrolls. | Refuse | Authenticity claims are fragment-set specific. |

## Source-Locked Claim Updates

| Claim | Source-locked grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| The Leon Levy Digital Library is official image/metadata custody. | Corroborated as official digital custody | IAA public pages describe the project and its imaging goals. |
| The corpus includes over 900 texts/manuscripts and thousands/tens of thousands of fragments. | Source-specific public value | IAA pages give public values; do not harmonize into an exact universal count. |
| Manuscript labels can reflect modern grouping, errors, or disagreements. | Corroborated as public classification caution | IAA content page states this explicitly. |
| Language identification can be impossible for very fragmentary manuscripts. | Corroborated as public classification caution | IAA language/script page states this explicitly. |
| MOTB authenticity results apply to all Dead Sea Scrolls. | Refuse | MOTB describes analysis of its 16 fragments; RUINS keeps this fragment-set-specific. |

## Phrases To Challenge

| Phrase | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "the scroll says" | "this edition/transcription/translation reads" |
| "the missing text says" | "the editor reconstructs" |
| "the fragment proves" | "the fragment supports, under this custody" |
| "the Dead Sea Scrolls were forged" | "this fragment or set has an authenticity finding" |
| "secret scrolls reveal" | "publication/access history and public secrecy memory differ" |
