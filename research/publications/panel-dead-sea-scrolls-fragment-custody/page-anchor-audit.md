# Page Anchor Audit: Dead Sea Scrolls Fragment Custody

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST / ERRATA
**Status:** current-scope page audit
**Date:** 2026-07-05

This audit locks page and line anchors for the current publication-ready
custody-ladder claims. It supports general corpus, imaging, classification,
language/script, publication-history, and modern-market authenticity-boundary
claims. It does not close edition-page custody for exact readings, joins,
lacunae, translations, or named fragments.

## Anchored Claims

| Claim surface | Anchor | Current RUINS use | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Public digital custody | IAA / Leon Levy home lines 77-79 state that the Digital Library uses advanced imaging, preserves thousands of scroll fragments, makes them publicly accessible, and offers high-resolution spectral images plus PAM scans. | Use as official public digital-library custody for mediated image access. | Do not treat digital image custody as physical fragment custody or edition custody. |
| Whole-collection imaging project | IAA / Leon Levy "The Digital Library" lines 89-97 describe documenting the collection online, imaging about 930 manuscripts comprising thousands of fragments, eventual metadata/transcriptions/translations/bibliography, multi-spectrum imaging, preservation monitoring, and recovery of faded writing. | Use for digital imaging process, preservation monitoring, and layer separation between image, metadata, transcription, translation, and bibliography. | Do not claim every fragment already has complete metadata, transcription, translation, or bibliography. |
| Fragmentary corpus condition | IAA "Introduction" lines 90-99 state that most of the archive consists of thousands of parchment and papyrus fragments, with scroll dates/materials/languages summarized. | Use for the core RUINS premise that survival is abundant but granular. | Do not infer exact fragment counts or exact fragment identity from this public overview. |
| Canon and textual variation caution | IAA "Introduction" lines 101-108 describe about 230 biblical Scroll manuscripts, later canon formation, and textual variation against later standard forms. | Use only for broad caution that scriptural importance does not remove textual/history complexity. | Do not make theological or textual-critical conclusions without RITE/MAXIM or edition custody. |
| Sectarian attribution caution | IAA "Introduction" lines 114-122 state that community attribution is debated and earlier Essene consensus has been challenged or modified. | Use to block a single settled community-origin claim. | Do not turn the paper into a sectarian-identity history. |
| Discovery and acquisition narrative | IAA "Discovery and Publication" lines 98-105 and 124-131 describe the discovery story, early photographs, acquisition, smuggling, sale, and purchase of the first scrolls. | Use as public discovery/publication-history context and custody caution. | Do not use public narrative as provenance proof for individual fragments. |
| Corpus scale and publication delay | IAA "Discovery and Publication" lines 149-163 describe tens of thousands of fragments, over 900 texts/manuscripts, Cave 4 scale, publication delay, IAA/Emanuel Tov publication expansion, and conservation-lab context. | Use for source-specific scale, publication-history, and conservation context. | Do not harmonize all count terms into one exact universal number. |
| Classification uncertainty | IAA "Scrolls Content" lines 90-114 describe manuscript categories, multiple-composition labels, modern errors/disagreements, and navigation-oriented categories. | Use to support identifier and classification caution. | Do not treat website navigation categories as settled scholarly taxonomy. |
| Language and dating caution | IAA "Languages and Scripts" lines 91-94 describe Hebrew, Aramaic, Greek, other languages, paleography, and carbon-14 dating. | Use for broad language/script and dating-method context. | Do not make detailed paleographic or radiocarbon claims without source-specific custody. |
| Fragmentary language uncertainty | IAA "Languages and Scripts" line 114 says some very fragmentary refuge-cave manuscripts cannot be assigned Hebrew or Aramaic. | Use to support refusal of overconfident language assignment for damaged witnesses. | Do not generalize this uncertainty to all manuscripts. |
| Cave 7 identification boundary | IAA "Languages and Scripts" lines 128-129 describe 27 Greek Qumran manuscripts and failed New Testament identification attempts for Cave 7 fragments. | Use to block sensational identification claims as a general RUINS caution. | Do not adjudicate Cave 7 identifications beyond the public IAA boundary. |
| Modern-market authenticity boundary | Museum of the Bible "A Journey for the Truth" lines 85-88 describe forged-fragment attempts, post-2002 market fragments, and independent imaging/materials analysis of 16 MOTB fragments. | Use to keep authenticity results fragment-set-specific and modern-market-specific. | Do not generalize MOTB findings to the entire Dead Sea Scrolls corpus. |

## Current Decision

The paper can stand as publication-ready for its current custody-ladder scope.
It has page anchors for the public official claims that support the
fragment/image/metadata/authenticity boundaries and the main refusals. Direct
edition-page custody remains required before future exact textual claims.
