# Plan: Dead Sea Scrolls Fragment Custody

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Evidence and Absence
**Status:** publication-ready
**Position in chain:** next-tranche textual-fragment case after Indus and House of Wisdom

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a fragmentary textual corpus when physical fragments,
photographs, editions, reconstructions, translations, provenance, and modern
authenticity claims all have different evidence status?

## Core Argument

The Dead Sea Scrolls are a RUINS case because they make fragment custody visible.
The fragments survive materially, but no single surface equals "the text." A
fragment, a plate photograph, a DJD edition, a reconstructed column, a
translation, a museum label, and a post-2002 market fragment each carry different
claim permissions. RUINS should build a ladder from fragment to edition to
translation to public memory and refuse any claim that skips rungs.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not use a translation as if it were the fragment.
2. Do not treat reconstructed lacunae as surviving text.
3. Do not merge cave provenance, collection history, and market provenance.
4. Do not turn authenticity disputes around modern fragments into claims about
   the whole corpus.
5. Do not use scriptural or sectarian importance to raise evidence grade.
