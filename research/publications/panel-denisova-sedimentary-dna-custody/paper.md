# Denisova Sedimentary DNA and Occupation-History Custody

The Denisova Cave sedimentary-DNA papers are a strong RUINS trigger because
they recover hominin and faunal evidence from cave deposits rather than from
bones alone. The 2021 Nature study reports DNA analysis of 728 sediment samples
from exposed Pleistocene deposits in all three chambers of Denisova Cave,
building on earlier work that showed cave sediments can preserve Neanderthal and
Denisovan DNA even where hominin remains are absent or scarce.

The evidence is powerful but not self-explaining. A sediment DNA signal is not a
person standing in a cave. It depends on sample location, layer integrity,
deposition, preservation, extraction, authentication, taxonomic assignment,
microstratigraphic hotspots, and association with artefacts, fauna, dates, and
hominin remains.

RUINS promotes sedimentary ancient DNA custody to Core here. Sedimentary DNA can
support bounded presence, turnover, and ecological claims, but it cannot become
exact individual presence, continuous occupation, direct species encounter,
artefact-maker identity, route, migration event, site function, complete
population history, or absence proof without separate custody.
