# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- The 2021 Nature Denisova study reports analysis of 728 sediment samples from
  exposed Pleistocene deposits in all three chambers.
- The paper builds on pilot work that detected ancient hominin mtDNA in 12 of 52
  sediment samples.
- Max Planck summary reports detection of Neanderthal and Denisovan DNA and
  frames the work as a high-resolution occupation-history study over about
  300,000 years.
- The 2017 Science paper shows Pleistocene cave sediments can contain ancient
  mammalian and hominin DNA, including where hominin remains are absent.
- Microstratigraphic work reports DNA concentrated in hotspots, including
  microscopic particles of bone or feces.

Future-locked:

- Exact individual presence.
- Continuous occupation.
- Direct species encounter.
- Artefact-maker identity.
- Migration route or population history.
- Absence proof from missing DNA.
