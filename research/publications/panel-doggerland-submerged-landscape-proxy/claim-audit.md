# Claim Audit: Doggerland

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Doggerland was a now-submerged landscape in the southern North Sea. | A | Multiple project and research sources support the broad condition. |
| Researchers reconstruct aspects of Doggerland through proxies. | A | The method is the case's central evidence condition. |
| Human occupation or use occurred in parts of the broader landscape. | B | Supported generally, but exact context varies by find and source. |
| Doggerland can be mapped as a complete settlement system. | Refused | Proxy and context limits block this. |
| Inundation can be narrated as one disaster event. | Refused | Current scope treats submergence as process and model-dependent event sequence. |
| Doggerland proves a named culture, migration route, or ancestry story. | Refused | These require CANON, PORTO, GENES, and direct evidence not in scope. |
| "Lost world" language proves original social reality. | Refused | This is cultural memory and public framing. |
