# Page Anchor Audit: Doggerland

## Anchored Claims

| Claim | Anchor posture | Current grade |
|---|---|---|
| Doggerland is a drowned/submerged southern North Sea landscape. | Project and edited-volume level. | Strong. |
| Research reconstructs landscape conditions through geophysical and environmental proxies. | Project and technical-source level. | Strong. |
| Ancient environmental DNA can inform palaeoenvironmental reconstruction. | Peer-reviewed paper level. | Strong but sample-specific. |
| Human use of parts of the landscape is plausible and evidenced unevenly. | Requires artefact/context source distinction. | Moderate. |
| Complete settlement maps or migration routes are not current-scope claims. | Refusal register. | Strong refusal. |

## Anchor Limits

- Project summaries can anchor research scope, not every technical conclusion.
- Edited volumes can anchor method and synthesis, not site-specific certainty.
- Ancient DNA anchors sample ecology, not named people.
- Trawled artefacts require find-context caution before social interpretation.
