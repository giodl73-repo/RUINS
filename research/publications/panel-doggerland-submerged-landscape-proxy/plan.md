# Plan: Doggerland Submerged Landscape Proxy

## Research Question

How should RUINS treat a vanished landscape when most evidence survives as
proxies rather than excavated in-situ settlement?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Doggerland as a submerged southern North Sea landscape.
- Broad postglacial inundation and changing habitability.
- Proxy categories: seabed mapping, seismic interpretation, sediment cores,
  trawled finds, faunal remains, ancient environmental DNA, and models.

Refused:
- A complete settlement map.
- A named cultural territory.
- A single disaster account.
- A precise migration route.
- Atlantis or lost-civilization framing.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Submerged-landscape proxy gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.

## Promotion Target

Fast-promote `ruins-capability:submerged-landscape-proxy-custody` from
Candidate to Active if the proxy gate blocks settlement, route, culture, and
lost-world overclaims without handing RUINS into geography or environmental
science.
