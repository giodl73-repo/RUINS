# Submerged Landscape Proxy Gate

## Gate Rule

A submerged landscape claim must name the evidence layer doing the work. No
single proxy may stand in for complete habitation, route, culture, event, or
identity.

## Proxy Layers

| Layer | Can support | Cannot support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Bathymetry/topography | Former surface form and seabed morphology. | Settlement layout or human route. |
| Seismic horizons | Buried landscape features and stratigraphic interpretation. | Occupation without independent context. |
| Sediment cores | Local environment, sequence, and sample context. | Whole-landscape social history. |
| Pollen/faunal evidence | Environmental conditions and species presence. | Named culture or population identity. |
| Ancient environmental DNA | Sample-specific ecological signal. | Ancestry, ethnicity, or migration route. |
| Trawled artefacts | Human presence in a broad region. | In-situ site plan or precise activity area. |
| Inundation models | Possible timing/process of submergence. | Lived disaster narrative. |
| Public reconstructions | Later visualization and memory. | Original-state proof. |

## Publication Test

Before publication, each sentence about Doggerland must answer:

1. Which proxy anchors the claim?
2. Is the find in-situ, displaced, modeled, or public-image evidence?
3. Does the claim require a sibling system?
4. Is the claim being made, refused, or demoted to P3?
