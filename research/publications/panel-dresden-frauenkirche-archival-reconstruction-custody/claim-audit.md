# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Dresden is an archival-reconstruction custody case. | Strong | Official sources anchor rubble, plans, photogrammetry, modeling, reused fabric, new material, and memory. |
| The rebuilt church is complete original fabric. | Refused | New stone, structural correction, reconstruction work, and mediation remain distinct. |
| The reconstruction was historically faithful in aim. | Moderate | Foundation source supports the aim, but exact fidelity needs item-level technical records. |
| The reconstruction recovered exact historical interiors. | Refused | Interior work is noted, but exact historical recovery is not source-locked here. |
| Peace and reconciliation are part of the afterlife. | Strong | Foundation and support society pages explicitly frame the campaign this way. |
| Dresden proves the same reconstruction policy as Warsaw. | Refused | UNESCO explicitly distinguishes Dresden's selective policy from Warsaw. |
