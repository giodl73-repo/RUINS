# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS grade a monument rebuilt after wartime destruction when the
reconstruction uses rubble, mapped finds, historical plans, photogrammetry,
computer modeling, reused stone, new stone, and public reconciliation memory?

## Evidence Surfaces

- Postwar rubble and ruin-memorial status.
- Mapped, recovered, and catalogued finds.
- Historical building fabric.
- Historical plans, floor plans, and reconstruction documentation.
- Photogrammetric recording and computer-aided modeling.
- Modern structural engineering and corrected load paths.
- New sandstone and modern mortar specifications.
- Interior artistic reconstruction.
- Peace, reconciliation, donation, and European-unity memory.

## Exclusions

- Full Dresden wartime history.
- Bombing-campaign judgment.
- Exact stone-by-stone inventory.
- Full theology or worship programming.
- Conservation engineering specification beyond source-locked claims.
- World Heritage policy argument.

## Promotion Target

Use Dresden as the unlike second test for
`ruins-capability:archival-reconstruction-custody`, then promote the capability
to Core with a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.27 cap.
