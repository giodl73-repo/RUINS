# Role Review: Dresden Frauenkirche Archival Reconstruction Custody

**Paper:** panel-dresden-frauenkirche-archival-reconstruction-custody
**Rubric:** v1.27
**Review date:** 2026-07-06

## Fragment Custodian

The paper separates rubble, historical fabric, mapped finds, reused fabric, new
sandstone, plans, photogrammetry, and computer model mediation. Exact reused
stone counts and item-level catalog claims are correctly future-locked.

## Absence Historian

Loss is layered: wartime destruction, rubble memorial, ruin removal,
reconstruction, structural correction, and memory afterlife are distinct. The
paper does not make Dresden a simple before/after restoration story.

## Reconstruction Skeptic

The strongest overclaims are refused: complete original fabric, exact interior,
model-as-direct-access, unchanged Baroque engineering, and total recovery.
Dresden is explicitly separated from Warsaw's reconstruction policy.

## Cultural Memory Reader

Peace, reconciliation, donation, European unity, and memorial-ruin framing are
handled as afterlife. They strengthen public-memory analysis without proving
original-state fidelity.

## Sibling Boundary Editor

Boundaries are clear: PORTO gets place, CANON gets named identities, CERES gets
materials and engineering, MAXIM/LUCIA get war history, RITE gets worship, and
policy/archive systems get doctrine and records.

## Paper Editor

The package has a plan, paper, source map, source lock, page-anchor audit, gate,
claim audit, refusal register, memory register, boundary register, and scorecard.

## Safety Editor

No looting, access, or conservation instruction risk. The paper avoids bombing
judgment and real-time conservation advice.

## Decision

Promote `ruins-capability:archival-reconstruction-custody` to Core. Add
`docs/archival-reconstruction-checklist.md` and rubric v1.27 cap.
