# Source Lock

Current publication claims are locked to official Frauenkirche Foundation pages
and UNESCO documentation.

## Locked Claims

- The Frauenkirche survived the Second World War as a mountain of rubble.
- Reconstruction began in 1994 and was completed in 2005.
- The 1993 work included removal of ruins plus mapping, recovery, and
  cataloguing of finds.
- Reconstruction used historical building fabric and original historical plans.
- Historical plans, floor plans, and documentation of earlier reconstruction
  measures served as sources.
- The ruins were photogrammetrically recorded in 1993.
- A computer-aided three-dimensional initial model mediated reconstruction and
  structural decisions.
- New regional sandstone and modern structural engineering were part of the
  rebuilt monument.
- The public campaign framed reconstruction through reconciliation, peace, and
  European unity.
- UNESCO describes Dresden's postwar work as restoration and reconstruction,
  and distinguishes Dresden's selective policy from Warsaw's.
