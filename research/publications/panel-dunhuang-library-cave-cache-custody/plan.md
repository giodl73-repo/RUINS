# Plan: Dunhuang Library Cave Cache Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a sealed manuscript cache that survives in bulk but does
not preserve a complete original library, order, institution, or corpus?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Cave 17 as a sealed chamber containing over 50,000 manuscripts, paintings,
  printed documents, textiles, and other objects dating from the 4th to 11th
  centuries, per IDP guidance.
- Modern discovery, Wang Yuanlu's custody, Stein/Pelliot-era dispersal, and
  later cataloguing/digitization as evidence layers.
- Peer-reviewed caution that original deposition purpose and original order are
  not settled.

Refused:
- Complete library function.
- Known sealing motive.
- Original cave order.
- Complete institutional archive or community archive.
- Whole Silk Road history.
- Digital reunification as physical or interpretive reunification.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Sealed-cache custody gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.

## Promotion Target

Fast-promote `ruins-capability:sealed-cache-representativeness-custody` from
Candidate to Active if the package blocks cache-as-whole-library and
digitization-as-reunification overclaims.
