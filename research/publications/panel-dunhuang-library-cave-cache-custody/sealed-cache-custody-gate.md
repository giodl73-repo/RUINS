# Sealed Cache Custody Gate

## Gate Rule

A sealed cache is not automatically representative of a whole library,
institution, community, tradition, or corpus. The paper must identify which
cache layer anchors each claim.

## Cache Layers

| Layer | Can support | Cannot support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Sealed survival | Material existed together in a hidden chamber. | Complete library function or institutional inventory. |
| Deposition purpose | A hypothesis when source-locked. | Certainty without explicit evidence. |
| Original order | Local grouping only when documented. | Full chamber order. |
| First discovery | Modern rediscovery sequence. | Original use or meaning. |
| Custodian/removal history | Modern access, sale, and dispersal custody. | Neutral provenance or ethical closure. |
| Expedition documentation | Site marks, bundle notes, and modern reconstruction aids. | Original arrangement unless specifically supported. |
| Catalogue record | Item identity and holding. | Corpus completeness or textual edition. |
| Digitized image | Access, preservation, and comparison. | Physical reunification or final reading. |
| Public lost-library memory | Reception and cultural afterlife. | Original function. |

## Publication Test

Before publication, each Cave 17 claim must answer:

1. Is the claim about the cache, a bundle, an item, a text, a collection, or a
   later digital record?
2. Does the claim depend on original order, modern site marks, or catalogue
   metadata?
3. Does the claim require FONTES/MUNDUS, CANON, MAXIM/LUCIA, or legal/policy
   handoff?
4. Is the exact claim being made, refused, or demoted to P3?
