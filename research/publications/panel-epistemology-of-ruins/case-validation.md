# Case Validation Matrix: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Owner:** ASHLAR / ERRATA
**Status:** current validation matrix

This matrix shows how the foundation vocabulary survived unlike case studies.

| Case | Evidence condition tested | Loss/process tested | Reconstruction pressure | Refusal pressure | Method result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Indus script | Direct material inscriptions, corpus catalogs, absent accepted reading. | Non-decipherment without a single loss event. | Sign lists and statistical models can be mistaken for readings. | Refuse live decipherment claims. | Method separates corpus survival from language closure. |
| Antikythera mechanism | Fragmentary artifact, inscriptions, imaging evidence. | Shipwreck, corrosion, recovery, modern imaging. | Functional models complete missing gear trains. | Refuse treating one model as the ancient whole. | Method separates artifact constraint from operational reconstruction. |
| Library of Alexandria | Reported institutions, copied/later textual memory, weak direct survival. | Multiple institutions and loss episodes compressed by memory. | Modern single-fire narrative over-completes the evidence. | Refuse one decisive destruction story. | Method keeps famous loss from becoming false event certainty. |
| Cahokia | Earthworks, settlement archaeology, proxies, demographic models. | Urban transformation and abandonment. | Collapse models and population values can outrun evidence. | Refuse single-cause collapse theater. | Method treats abandonment as layered change. |
| Roman concrete | Surviving material samples, textual traces, modern materials science. | Partial technical transmission. | Experiments can be mistaken for complete ancient recipe recovery. | Refuse secret-ingredient and total-loss claims. | Method separates material survival from workshop practice. |
| Knossos | Excavated palace remains plus major modern restoration. | Excavation, restoration, tourism, public image. | Restoration becomes visible object. | Refuse treating restored appearance as Bronze Age certainty. | Method treats restoration as part of the evidence history. |
| Great Zimbabwe | Monumental architecture, trade objects, heritage records, living memory. | Colonial denial and damaged custody. | Public myths and imported goods can distort authorship. | Refuse non-African origin theories and mystery-city framing. | Method handles interpretive violence as a ruin condition. |

## Vocabulary Adjustments After Validation

| Adjustment | Reason |
|---|---|
| Add damaged custody as a first-class layer. | Great Zimbabwe, Antikythera, Knossos, and Alexandria all show that recovery and interpretation can damage evidence. |
| Treat cultural memory as afterlife evidence. | Alexandria, Indus, Great Zimbabwe, Knossos, and Cahokia need memory without evidentiary inflation. |
| Require refusal registers for mature case papers. | The strongest case papers improved when refusals became operational, not rhetorical. |
| Require sibling-boundary registers for mature case papers. | The case set repeatedly wanted to drift into source custody, geography, economy, ritual, identity, or general history. |
