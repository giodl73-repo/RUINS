# Chronology: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Owner:** STRATA
**Status:** first-pass work surface

This file keeps RUINS from flattening long loss processes into one dramatic
event.

## Loss-Process Categories

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Sudden destruction | Fire, flood, battle, earthquake, demolition, or other bounded event. |
| Repeated damage | Multiple destructive episodes over time. |
| Dispersal | Object, archive, population, or tradition spread across locations or owners. |
| Abandonment | Use declines or stops without a single clear destruction event. |
| Reuse | Materials, places, or texts are repurposed, overwriting original context. |
| Censorship or erasure | Deliberate suppression, destruction, renaming, or removal. |
| Ordinary decay | Loss through environment, neglect, material fragility, or time. |
| Excavation loss | Modern recovery, documentation, or restoration damages or recontextualizes evidence. |
| Restoration transformation | Restoration becomes part of the object and changes later interpretation. |
