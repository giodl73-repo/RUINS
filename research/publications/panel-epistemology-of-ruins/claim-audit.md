# Claim Audit: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Owner:** ERRATA
**Status:** validated method audit

This file tracks claim-grade discipline before the paper becomes publication
candidate material.

## Audit Rules

| Rule | Check |
|---|---|
| Reconstruction downgrade | Claims depending on reconstruction stay at `plausible` or lower unless independently corroborated. |
| Copy/report downgrade | Later copies or reports cannot create `observed` claims about the original. |
| Visual-confidence control | A persuasive image, model, or restoration cannot raise evidence confidence by itself. |
| Memory separation | Cultural memory can be important without becoming direct evidence. |
| Refusal specificity | Every case must name what it cannot responsibly claim. |

## Seed Claim Audit

| Case | Claim to test | Maximum grade before source review | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Indus script | The surviving signs represent a readable language. | Speculative | Sign recurrence and artifact context do not by themselves prove decipherment or language identity. |
| Antikythera mechanism | The mechanism performed a specific modeled function. | Plausible | Surviving gear and inscription evidence may constrain functions, but missing parts require model completion. |
| Library of Alexandria | The library was destroyed in one decisive event. | Legendary / speculative | Public memory often compresses multiple institutions and episodes. |
| Cahokia | Cahokia collapsed because of one named cause. | Speculative | Settlement change is likely multi-layered unless evidence rules alternatives out. |
| Roman concrete | Roman concrete technology was simply lost. | Plausible at most | Surviving structures, textual traces, local continuity, and experimental recovery complicate "lost." |

## Failure Tests

RUINS should halt schema/repo-runtime promotion if:

- the vocabulary cannot classify multiple seed-case evidence surfaces,
- role reviewers cannot agree on grade boundaries,
- reconstruction claims repeatedly outrun evidence conditions,
- refusal sections become boilerplate,
- source needs require custody work that has not been done.

## Phrases To Challenge

| Phrase | Preferred discipline |
|---|---|
| "clearly shows" | Name the evidence and grade the claim. |
| "must have" | Use only when alternatives are ruled out. |
| "lost civilization" | Specify site, institution, practice, or corpus. |
| "mystery" | Replace with the exact unknown. |
| "forgotten technology" | Distinguish lost, partial, continuous, regional, or experimentally recovered technique. |
