## Method Use Rules

1. A RUINS paper must separate what survives from what is inferred.
2. A RUINS paper must distinguish loss event, loss process, damaged custody,
   later memory, and modern reconstruction when more than one is present.
3. A RUINS paper must grade claims conservatively and explain upgrades.
4. A RUINS paper must treat cultural memory as afterlife evidence, not as direct
   original-state evidence.
5. A RUINS paper must include refusals specific enough to block common
   overclaims.
6. A RUINS paper must name sibling handoffs rather than expanding into general
   encyclopedia work.

## Boundary

This method lock supports the foundation paper as a RUINS methodology note. It
does not claim that the method is exhaustive for all archaeology, heritage,
philology, conservation, or historical reconstruction.
