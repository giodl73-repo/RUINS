# Plan: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Track A - Evidence and Absence
**Venue:** Journal of Archaeological Method and Theory, or a portfolio research note before venue targeting
**Position in chain:** Track A, Paper 1 - independent; feeds all later RUINS case papers

## Research Question

Can lost cities, destroyed libraries, fragmentary texts, abandoned
infrastructure, vanished techniques, and contested reconstructions be handled
with one disciplined evidence vocabulary?

## Core Argument

RUINS should begin as an epistemic method, not as a catalog. The method separates
what survives from what is inferred, what is reconstructed from what is
remembered, and what must remain unknown from what can be responsibly claimed.
The first paper defines evidence conditions, claim grades, and refusal
discipline, then tests them against five unlike seed cases: Indus script,
Antikythera mechanism, Library of Alexandria, Cahokia, and Roman concrete.

## Primary Output

A reusable RUINS case-paper template with:

- evidence condition,
- loss event or loss process condition,
- source map,
- chronology table,
- claim-grade table,
- reconstruction-status table,
- cultural-memory afterlife table,
- claim audit,
- refusal section,
- sibling Knowledge Systems handoff.

## Methodology

1. Define the minimum vocabulary needed to compare partial-survival cases.
2. Build claim grades that avoid false numeric precision.
3. Build a metadata-only source map before grading claims.
4. Build a chronology that separates sudden events from cumulative processes.
5. Test the vocabulary against five unlike seed cases.
6. Audit reconstruction-dependent claims before they enter the paper.
7. Record where the vocabulary fails or needs subdivision.
8. Produce a paper template that future RUINS case studies must use.

## Claim Downgrade Rules

1. If a claim depends on a reconstruction, it cannot be graded above
   `plausible` unless independent evidence corroborates it.
2. If a claim depends on a later copy or report, it cannot be graded
   `observed`; use `corroborated`, `plausible`, `speculative`, or `legendary`
   according to support.
3. If visual restoration supplies missing structure, mark it as `modern
   projection` unless the missing structure is directly constrained.
4. If a source tradition preserves cultural memory but not direct evidence,
   grade it as memory evidence, not original-state evidence.
5. If role reviewers disagree about a claim grade, preserve the lower grade
   until the disagreement is resolved.

## Null Fallback

If the vocabulary cannot handle the five seed cases without forcing them into
unnatural boxes, RUINS should stay a loose essay series and defer any shared
schema, corpus, or child repo.

Concrete failure conditions:

- two or more seed cases require repeated unclassified evidence conditions,
- role reviewers repeatedly disagree about claim grade boundaries,
- reconstruction status exceeds evidence status in the paper argument,
- refusal sections become generic rather than case-specific,
- sibling handoffs dominate the paper, leaving RUINS with no distinct partial
  survival question.
