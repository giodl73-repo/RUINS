# Refusal Register: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Owner:** Reconstruction Skeptic / Safety Editor
**Status:** method-level refusal register

RUINS refuses the following method failures.

| Refused move | Why it is refused | Required replacement |
|---|---|---|
| Treating every ruin as a mystery. | "Mystery" hides the exact missing evidence. | Name the missing bridge: source, context, mechanism, chronology, or identity. |
| Treating reconstruction as recovery. | A model, restoration, or replica can be useful without restoring the original. | Label reconstruction status and evidence constraint. |
| Treating famous memory as direct evidence. | Later memory can preserve reception while distorting origin claims. | Classify memory as afterlife evidence. |
| Treating absence as permission to complete a story. | Gaps increase responsibility; they do not lower evidence standards. | Lower the claim grade or refuse closure. |
| Treating imported objects as authorship. | Contact evidence does not prove builders, users, or political control. | Grade as contact/trade unless stronger evidence exists. |
| Treating abandonment as collapse by default. | Settlement transformation may be layered, gradual, or regionally uneven. | Separate abandonment, migration, reuse, transformation, and memory. |
| Treating modern restoration as ancient appearance. | Restored surfaces can become the object people know. | Separate direct material from modern projection. |
| Turning RUINS into a general encyclopedia. | RUINS owns partial survival and reconstruction discipline, not every topic detail. | Hand off general history, geography, identity, ritual, source custody, and economy. |
