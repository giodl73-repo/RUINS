# Sibling Boundary Register: The Epistemology of Ruins

**Owner:** Sibling Boundary Editor
**Status:** method-level boundary register

RUINS owns partial survival, evidence condition, reconstruction status, cultural
memory, and refusal discipline. It does not own every fact needed by a case.

| Boundary | Handoff | RUINS keeps |
|---|---|---|
| General reference summaries | MAXIM | The partial-survival question and evidence-grade discipline. |
| Source custody and protected-source handling | FONTES / MUNDUS | Metadata-only source maps and claim gates. |
| Geography, routes, terrain, and site setting | PORTO | Site-setting claims only when they affect evidence condition. |
| Named entities, artifacts, works, signs, features | CANON | Identity uncertainty and variant-name risks. |
| Ritual, sacred place, mourning, legitimacy | RITE | Memory/custody layer and refusal to flatten sacred claims. |
| Kinship, lineage, inheritance, descent | GENES | Boundary against unsupported continuity claims. |
| Production, craft, economy, materials | CERES | Lost-tech and partial-transmission evidence posture. |
| Chronological narrative and actors | LUCIA | Loss-event/process separation. |

## Method Rule

If a section stops asking "what survives, what is missing, what is inferred, and
what must be refused," it belongs in a sibling system or a companion paper.
