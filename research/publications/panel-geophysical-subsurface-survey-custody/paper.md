# Geophysical Survey and Subsurface Custody

Geophysical prospection can make buried archaeology visible without opening the
ground. Ground-penetrating radar, magnetometry, resistivity, electromagnetic
induction, and magnetic susceptibility can reveal anomaly patterns that guide
excavation, reinterpret urban layouts, and widen landscape archaeology.

That nondestructive visibility is not excavation. A radar time slice,
magnetometry plot, resistivity map, or integrated anomaly image is a mediated
signal. It can support bounded feature candidates and survey priorities, but it
cannot by itself become a confirmed buried building, complete street grid, exact
wall line, certain tomb or ritual feature, complete urban plan, excavation-
equivalent proof, final chronology, preservation condition, sacred-landscape
design, solved site function, or whole city.

The controlling RUINS move is to separate the instrument, grid, sampling
interval, processing, georeferencing, depth model, soil contrast, anomaly class,
modern or natural interference, feature candidate, ground truth, chronology,
function, plan synthesis, and public memory. Falerii Novi and Stonehenge-style
survey programs show the same pressure: geophysics is powerful because it is
non-invasive, but that same power makes "revealed without digging" claims too
easy.

This paper promotes `ruins-capability:geophysical-subsurface-survey-custody` to
Core.
