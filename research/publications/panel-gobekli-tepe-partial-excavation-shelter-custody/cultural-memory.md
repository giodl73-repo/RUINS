# Cultural Memory: Gobekli Tepe

Gobekli Tepe's public memory is dominated by origin language: oldest temple,
first monumentality, hunter-gatherer builders, birth of civilization, and
rewriting history. These phrases explain the site's modern force, but they are
also overclaim pressure.

RUINS may discuss that memory as reception. It may not use it to prove:

- complete exposure of the site,
- final ritual or funerary meaning,
- complete settlement model,
- solved social hierarchy,
- finished conservation,
- or a total origin story for civilization.

The public-memory lesson is that spectacular partial exposure can make an
unfinished research problem look complete. RUINS keeps that incompleteness
visible.
