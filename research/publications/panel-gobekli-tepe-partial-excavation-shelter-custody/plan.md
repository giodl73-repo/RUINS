# Plan: Gobekli Tepe Partial Excavation Shelter Custody

## Scope

Use Gobekli Tepe as a pressure discovery for partially excavated, sheltered
public knowledge. The paper asks how RUINS should handle sites where the visitor
view is an exposed, conserved, roofed subset of a larger excavation and research
problem.

## Current Claims

- Gobekli Tepe is a Pre-Pottery Neolithic site with monumental buildings,
  T-shaped pillars, and animal imagery.
- Excavations began in 1995 after 1994 rediscovery and continue.
- Interpretation has shifted from primarily ritual/mountain-shrine framing
  toward a settlement with pronounced ritual component.
- Shelters and visitor access improve protection and visibility but do not equal
  complete site exposure.
- Conservation measures may reinstate collapsed pillars, repoint walls, and add
  reversible protective layers while leaving exact interpretation future-locked.

## Refused Claims

- The visitor sees the whole site.
- The site is finally explained as the world's first temple.
- Ritual or funerary meaning is settled.
- Domestic evidence erases ritual evidence, or ritual evidence erases domestic
  evidence.
- Conservation work means the site is finished or risk-free.
- Gobekli Tepe alone explains the Neolithic transition.
