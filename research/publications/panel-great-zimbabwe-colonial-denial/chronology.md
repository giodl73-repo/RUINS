# Chronology: Great Zimbabwe Colonial Denial

**Owner:** Absence Historian
**Status:** working chronology

## Layers

| Layer | Approximate horizon | Evidence condition | RUINS note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Construction and occupation | roughly late first / early second millennium CE through 15th century public ranges | material survival and research chronology | Exact ranges require source lock. |
| Regional networks and trade | medieval southern/eastern African trade horizons | artifacts and interpretation | Imported goods prove contact, not foreign authorship. |
| Abandonment / transformation | late occupation and post-occupation horizons | settlement/process question | Avoid single-cause collapse claims. |
| Antiquarian and colonial intervention | late 19th / early 20th century | damaged custody, looting, racial interpretation | Breaks evidence and produces denial narratives. |
| Scientific archaeology and consensus | 20th century onward | scholarly record | African construction is the high-level consensus; details remain source-specific. |
| National and heritage afterlife | independence era to present | public memory / heritage custody | Great Zimbabwe becomes national symbol and UNESCO heritage site. |
| Local sacred landscape / community memory | ongoing | living memory | Must not be erased by archaeology-only framing. |

## Chronology Controls

1. Do not treat imported artifacts as proof of foreign builders.
2. Do not repeat colonial non-African origin theories as live hypotheses.
3. Do not reduce abandonment to one cause without source-specific support.
4. Do not let national symbolism replace archaeological or local evidence.
