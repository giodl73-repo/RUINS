# Plan: Great Zimbabwe Colonial Denial

## Question

How should RUINS handle a monumental site whose material survival is strong, but
whose interpretation was damaged by colonial denial, looting, racial fantasy,
and later nationalist and heritage memory?

## Deliverables

1. Separate surviving drystone architecture, excavated artifacts, trade goods,
   stratigraphic record, oral/local memory, and modern heritage framing.
2. Treat non-African origin theories as cultural-memory / colonial-denial
   artifacts, not live archaeological alternatives.
3. Distinguish archaeological consensus on African construction from detailed
   claims about identity, political structure, population, and abandonment.
4. Track how Great Zimbabwe became national symbolism without making national
   memory substitute for archaeological evidence.
5. Refuse both colonial erasure and overcorrection that flattens local,
   scholarly, and heritage claims into one story.

## Current Scope

This pass is a stable working paper. It source-locks UNESCO public values,
archaeological consensus on local African construction, colonial-denial
framing, and local/community-memory surfaces. It does not yet include
page-level anchors for publication-ready use of exact values or interpretations.

## Out Of Scope

- Travel guide or site itinerary.
- Excavation or conservation instructions.
- Complete Shona/Venda/Rozvi history.
- Full inventory of artifacts, birds, walls, or enclosures.
- Modern political history of Zimbabwe beyond Great Zimbabwe's memory role.
