# Source Lock: Great Zimbabwe Colonial Denial

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST
**Status:** first source lock
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This source lock defines what RUINS may say about Great Zimbabwe in this paper.
It separates material survival, public heritage values, archaeological
consensus, colonial denial, local memory, and national afterlife. A locked
source supports bounded claims only; it does not make every origin, identity,
population, or abandonment claim final.

## Paper Use Rules

1. Treat imported goods as evidence of trade/contact, not foreign builders.
2. Use UNESCO values with source labels and avoid harmonizing date, area,
   population, or abandonment claims into false precision.
3. State African/local construction as the high-level archaeological consensus,
   while keeping detailed identity and continuity claims source-specific.
4. Treat colonial non-African origin theories as modern denial artifacts.
5. Treat local memory and sacred landscape claims with sensitivity and source
   posture; do not flatten them into tourism or folklore.
6. Do not provide excavation, conservation, or site-management instructions.
