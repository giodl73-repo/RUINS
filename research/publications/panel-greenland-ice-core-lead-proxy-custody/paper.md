# Greenland Ice-Core Lead and Historical Proxy Custody

Greenland and Arctic ice-core lead papers are a strong RUINS trigger because
they reconstruct ancient mining and smelting activity from atmospheric pollution
preserved far from the source. The 2018 PNAS antiquity paper frames Greenland
lead pollution as a proxy for European emissions that track plagues, wars, and
imperial expansion, while later Arctic work extends the method to medieval
silver production and Roman-era public-health modelling.

The evidence is powerful but not self-explaining. An ice-core lead signal is not
a direct economic ledger, a witnessed war, a plague impact statement, or a
population health record. It depends on core chronology, annual-layer
resolution, contaminant chemistry, deposition process, transport modelling,
source apportionment, conversion from deposition to emissions, event alignment,
and uncertainty at every scale.

RUINS promotes ice-core pollution proxy custody to Core here. Ice-core lead can
support bounded pollution, source, and historical-correlation claims, but it
cannot become exact mining output, a precise economic index, single-event proof,
imperial expansion proof, plague impact certainty, war chronology, population
exposure, IQ loss, civilizational decline, or complete industrial history
without separate custody.
