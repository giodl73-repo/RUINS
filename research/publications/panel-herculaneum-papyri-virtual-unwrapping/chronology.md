# Chronology: Herculaneum Papyri Virtual Unwrapping

**Owner:** CALENDAR
**Status:** working chronology

| Layer | Event/process | RUINS significance |
|---|---|---|
| AD 79 | Vesuvius eruption buries Herculaneum and the Villa of the Papyri. | Destruction and preservation are the same event complex. |
| 1752-1754 | Villa/library discovery and excavation bring carbonized scrolls into modern custody. | Recovery begins a new damage and interpretation history. |
| Early opening attempts | Physical unrolling and mechanical opening recover some text but damage many scrolls. | Access can destroy evidence. |
| 2000s-2010s | CT and virtual unwrapping mature as noninvasive alternatives. | Scan custody becomes a new evidence layer. |
| 2023 | Vesuvius Challenge launches around open data, segmentation, ink detection, and prizes. | Public research organization becomes part of the evidence afterlife. |
| 2024-2026 | Progress moves from passages toward more complete scroll reading. | Milestone claims must be source-scoped and separated from final editions. |

## Chronology Rule

Do not collapse burial, survival, physical opening, scan acquisition, model
inference, and scholarly reading into one recovery event.
