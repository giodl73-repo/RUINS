# Plan: Herculaneum Papyri Virtual Unwrapping

**Status:** publication-ready
**Owner:** ASHLAR
**Date:** 2026-07-05

## Purpose

Use the Herculaneum papyri to test a RUINS capability for digitally mediated
survival. The case is not just fragmentary text. It is a chain from material
scroll, CT volume, surface segmentation, virtual unwrapping, ink detection,
scholarly reading, and public "AI recovered a library" memory.

## Questions

1. What survives materially when a scroll is carbonized and closed?
2. What does a CT volume preserve, and what does it not preserve?
3. Where does virtual unwrapping become reconstruction rather than observation?
4. How should RUINS grade machine-learning ink detections?
5. When does recovered text become a scholarly claim rather than a model output?
6. How should public "lost library restored by AI" narratives be classified?

## Deliverables

- Source map and first source lock.
- Chronology separating burial, physical opening, scanning, modeling, and
  reading.
- Claim audit for material, scan, model, text, and interpretation claims.
- Refusal register for AI-miracle and recovered-library overclaims.
- Role review and scorecard.

## Non-Goals

- No technical implementation.
- No download or processing of CT volumes.
- No claims about exact Greek readings without edition/papyrological custody.
- No conservation, handling, or acquisition advice.
