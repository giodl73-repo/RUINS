# Chronology: House of Wisdom Institution Memory

**Owner:** STRATA
**Status:** first-pass work surface

## Layered Chronology

| Period | Layer | Evidence condition | RUINS caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Abbasid period | Court, library, archive, and translation patronage develop around Baghdad. | textual / contextual | Do not collapse broad patronage into one institution. |
| 8th-10th centuries | Translation and scholarly activity intensify in Abbasid contexts. | textual transmission / secondary synthesis | Translation movement is not automatically Bayt al-Hikma. |
| Medieval source period | Bayt al-Hikma / Khizanat al-Hikma references circulate in bibliographic and biographical traditions. | copied / reported textual | Exact institutional meaning requires source custody. |
| 1258 | Mongol sack of Baghdad. | reported catastrophe / historiography | Major destruction event; exact collection claims need audit. |
| Later medieval and modern periods | House of Wisdom becomes a symbol of lost knowledge and golden-age memory. | cultural memory | Memory is historically important but not direct institutional evidence. |

## Process Categories

| Process | Use in this paper |
|---|---|
| Institutional ambiguity | The object of study may be a named office, collection, archive, court library, or later label. |
| Dispersal and destruction | Books, scholars, buildings, and source traditions may be lost in different ways. |
| Memory compression | Baghdad, translation, library loss, and 1258 catastrophe become one public image. |
| Category drift | Modern terms like university, academy, public library, and research center can import false precision. |
