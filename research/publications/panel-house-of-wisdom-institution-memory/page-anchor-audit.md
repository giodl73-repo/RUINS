# Page Anchor Audit: House of Wisdom Institution Memory

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST / ERRATA
**Status:** current-scope page audit
**Date:** 2026-07-05

This audit locks page or line anchors for the current stable-working-paper
claims. It does not close direct primary-source custody for al-Nadim, al-Qifti,
or other medieval witnesses, and it does not replace direct Brill custody for
the Encyclopaedia of Islam Three article.

## Anchored Claims

| Claim surface | Anchor | Current RUINS use | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Palace-library boundary | Gutas/van Bladel accessible EI3 copy lines 10-18 describe Bayt al-Hikma as the palace library of early Abbasid caliphs, mentioned in connection with al-Rashid and al-Ma'mun, and conclude the references are to a palace library. | Use "palace-library/source-tradition problem" as the strongest current institutional wording. | Do not call it a modern university, public academy, or research institute. |
| Term alternation | Gutas/van Bladel lines 11-14 note alternation between Bayt al-Hikma and Khizanat al-Hikma / khizana. | Use both terms when discussing named source traditions. | Do not harmonize the terms into one settled modern institution. |
| Function of the library | Gutas/van Bladel lines 13, 21-28 describe collecting, preserving, copying, binding, affiliations, Persian translation links, and caution that evidence does not show regular Bayt al-Hikma Greek-translation activity. | Treat Bayt al-Hikma as collection/preservation/court-library evidence with limited translation links. | Do not make it the sole or regular center of the Graeco-Arabic translation movement. |
| Academy / translation-bureau myth | Gutas/van Bladel lines 29-38 trace the modern full-fledged academy/institute myth and state that no evidence supports those assertions. | Refuse modern academy, university, and central Greek-translation-bureau claims as current RUINS conclusions. | May discuss those claims as twentieth-century scholarship or public memory. |
| Teaching-summary dispute framing | University of Delhi PDF page 0 lines 1-24 says House of Wisdom may refer to public academy/intellectual center or private library, states active dispute and lack of physical evidence, and says it was unlikely to be the sole center of translation work. | Use as public teaching summary that corroborates dispute, lack of physical evidence, and non-sole-center boundary. | Teaching summary cannot replace direct source custody. |
| Khizanat al-Hikma teaching summary | University of Delhi PDF page 1 lines 34-36 and page 2 lines 53-55 connect Khizanat al-Hikma / Bayt al-Hikma to preservation of rare books and poetry. | Use as secondary/public support for storage/preservation language. | Exact medieval term use still needs primary-source audit. |
| 1258 memory caution | Biran 2019 abstract lines 28-30 and 98-100 state that destruction of Baghdadi libraries is a powerful 1258 image often tied to civilizational decline, but the article challenges that claim by reconstructing Ilkhanid Baghdad's libraries and intellectual community. | Treat 1258 as destruction context and catastrophe memory, not total intellectual closure. | Article page audit is still needed before stronger detailed claims. |
| Biran bibliographic custody | Biran page lines 31-41 and 93-95 identify article type, journal, pages 464-502, DOI, and publication year. | Use for bibliographic/source-custody target. | Abstract/metadata lock only until article pages are audited. |
| Public-memory expansive image | 1001 Inventions lines 76-87, 111-125, and 130-131 present translation-house, intellectual-powerhouse, global-scholar, and Mongol-destruction imagery. | Use as modern public-memory contrast. | Do not use as medieval institutional proof. |
| Public image artifact | 1001 Inventions lines 93-104 list related books and identify an imaginary drawing of the House of Wisdom library. | Use as evidence that the House has a modern visual/public afterlife. | Image/public afterlife is not original-state evidence. |

## Current Decision

The paper can stand as a stable working paper. It has enough anchors to block
the most common overclaims and to classify the House of Wisdom as institutional
memory under partial survival. It should not be publication-ready until direct
primary/near-primary source references and direct Brill/page custody are added.
