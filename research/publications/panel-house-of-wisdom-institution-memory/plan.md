# Plan: House of Wisdom Institution Memory

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Evidence and Absence
**Status:** publication-ready
**Position in chain:** next-tranche case after the first RUINS method set

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle the Baghdad House of Wisdom when the public image of a
single destroyed academy is stronger than the surviving institutional evidence?

## Core Argument

The House of Wisdom is a RUINS case because the ruin is not only a destroyed
library. It is a contested institutional memory. Baghdad's Abbasid translation
and scholarly world was real, but the named institution, its functions, its
centrality, and its destruction are not equally recoverable. RUINS must separate
Baghdad's broader intellectual ecology from the narrower Bayt al-Hikma evidence,
and both from later civilizational-loss memory.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not treat the House of Wisdom as a modern university unless source custody
   supports that institutional form.
2. Do not treat the entire translation movement as located inside one building.
3. Do not use the 1258 sack of Baghdad as proof of every popular library-loss
   image.
4. Do not repeat river-of-ink or total-world-memory claims as facts without
   source audit.
5. Treat modern golden-age/catastrophe narratives as cultural memory, not direct
   institutional evidence.

## Next Work

1. STRATA separates Abbasid patronage, translation activity, Baghdad's fall, and
   later memory.
2. ERRATA audits whether the paper may say academy, library, archive, bureau,
   collection, or metaphor.
3. PALIMPSEST adds direct primary/Brill/article custody before any future exact
   quotation, source-page, or destruction-detail claim.
