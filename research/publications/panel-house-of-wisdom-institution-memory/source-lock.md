# Source Lock: House of Wisdom Institution Memory

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST
**Status:** first source lock
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This source lock defines what RUINS may say about the Baghdad House of Wisdom in
this paper. It separates the named Bayt al-Hikma / Khizanat al-Hikma evidence,
the broader Abbasid translation movement, the 1258 destruction context, and
later golden-age/catastrophe memory.

## Paper Use Rules

1. Use "House of Wisdom" as a modern English label, not as a settled medieval
   institutional category.
2. Prefer Bayt al-Hikma / Khizanat al-Hikma when discussing the named source
   tradition.
3. Do not call the institution a university, academy, research institute, public
   library, or translation bureau without source-specific framing.
4. Treat the broader translation movement as real but not identical with one
   building or office.
5. Treat 1258 as a destruction context, not a complete ledger of every lost
   book, manuscript, or institution.
6. Treat golden-age and river-of-ink imagery as cultural memory until direct
   source custody supports a stronger claim.
