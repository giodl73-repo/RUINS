# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| H6 is a notated-performance reconstruction custody case. | Strong | Sources anchor tablet, text, notation, interval-number ambiguity, rival interpretations, and modern notation mediation. |
| The tablet preserves exact ancient sound. | Refused | Source lock supports notation and instruction, not direct sound. |
| The interval-number text can be confidently played as a modern score. | Refused | Museum and recent scholarship both stress uncertainty and rival interpretations. |
| Tablaturization is a useful custody method. | Moderate | Recent scholarship supports it, but RUINS treats it as method, not final performance. |
| Modern recordings are evidence of the ancient performance. | Refused | Recordings are modern interpretations or public afterlife. |
| H6 can be called an "oldest song" case. | Moderate | Useful public shorthand, but must not collapse object, notation, interpretation, and performance. |
