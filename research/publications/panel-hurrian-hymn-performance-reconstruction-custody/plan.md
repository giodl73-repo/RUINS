# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS grade a damaged and ambiguous ancient musical notation when
modern readers want a playable song?

## Evidence Surfaces

- Terracotta tablet from Ras Shamra, ancient Ugarit.
- Hymn text and musical instructions separated on the tablet.
- Akkadian interval names and number signs.
- Tuning term and instrument reference.
- Damaged signs and uncertain readings.
- Transliteration and translation.
- Modern Western staff notation.
- Tablaturization and string-number presentation.
- Modern performance and recordings.
- "Oldest song" public memory.

## Exclusions

- Full Hurrian or Ugaritic history.
- Complete translation of the hymn.
- Final musicological theory.
- Exact instrument reconstruction.
- Recording review or performance recommendation.
- Ritual interpretation beyond evidence-layer handling.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:notated-performance-reconstruction-custody` from
Candidate to Active with a concrete gate and refusal register.
