# Source Lock

Current publication claims are locked to the National Museum of Damascus virtual
museum page and a 2026 Early Music article.

## Locked Claims

- The tablet is identified as Hurrian Hymn no.6 and held by the National Museum
  of Damascus.
- The object is terracotta, from Ras Shamra / ancient Ugarit, and dated to about
  1400 B.C.
- The tablet includes hymn text and musical notation/instructions.
- The musical instruction layer includes interval names followed by numbers.
- The interpretation of those numbers and their transfer to modern staff
  notation is uncertain.
- The tablet has produced multiple rival interpretations.
- H6 is treated in recent scholarship as one of the oldest and most complete
  examples of instructional musical notation in cuneiform.
- Modern Western notation can over-close ancient musical ambiguity.
- Tablaturization is proposed as a presentation method that preserves more of
  the original instruction structure.
