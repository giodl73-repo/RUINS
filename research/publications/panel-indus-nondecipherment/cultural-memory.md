# Cultural Memory Register: Indus Script Non-Decipherment

**Owner:** RELIQUARY
**Status:** first example register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This register records how the undeciphered condition circulates outside the
strict evidence chain. These entries are not evidence for translation. They are
evidence for how absence becomes public meaning.

## Examples

| Memory form | Example surface | What it does | RUINS use | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ancient-mystery framing | Popular articles describe the script as a long-standing mystery because it lacks accepted reading, long texts, known language, and a bilingual anchor. | Turns a precise evidence gap into a general puzzle narrative. | Shows why RUINS must name the missing bridge rather than repeat "mystery" as explanation. | Not authority for corpus values or decipherment status. |
| AI-breakthrough framing | Technology coverage of computational work often presents pattern detection as movement toward decipherment. | Converts statistical structure into a progress story. | Useful for auditing the boundary between pattern and reading. | Pattern detection is not translation. |
| Prize/challenge framing | Public coverage of Tamil Nadu's 2025 prize offer frames decipherment as a civilizational challenge with monetary and identity stakes. | Makes non-decipherment a public competition and cultural prestige object. | Shows how unresolved evidence can attract closure incentives. | The prize does not change the evidence grade of any reading. |
| Language-family identity framing | Public summaries and debate histories connect proposed readings to Dravidian, Indo-Aryan, Munda, or other language-family claims. | Turns decipherment into a continuity or ownership claim. | Supports a refusal rule: RUINS records these as proposals unless independently anchored. | Do not infer modern identity from undeciphered signs. |
| Scholarly controversy framing | Linguistic and non-linguistic interpretations are debated through papers, reviews, and public summaries. | Keeps the object in contested scholarly circulation rather than simple ignorance. | Lets RUINS distinguish live debate from solved reading. | A debate position is not final closure. |

## Paper Rules

1. Cultural-memory examples may explain why claims become attractive.
2. They may not upgrade a decipherment, language-family claim, or corpus count.
3. They should preserve the difference between "undeciphered" and
   "unknowable."
4. When public coverage uses breakthrough language, RUINS should restate the
   underlying evidence claim in claim-grade terms.
