# Plan: Indus Script Non-Decipherment

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Track A - Evidence and Absence
**Position in chain:** Track A, Paper 3 - depends on `panel-epistemology-of-ruins`
**Status:** working plan

## Research Question

What does responsible non-decipherment look like when a large sign corpus
survives, but the language, reading, bilingual anchor, and long-text context do
not?

## Core Argument

The Indus script is a RUINS case because the ruin is not only a lost city or
broken object. It is a partial-survival condition for meaning. Seals, tablets,
pottery marks, and sign sequences survive; a stable, generally accepted reading
does not. RUINS should not decide whether the system is linguistic,
proto-writing, administrative marking, or something else. It should define what
can be responsibly said while no decipherment is accepted.

## Primary Output

A refusal model for undeciphered textual systems:

- what survives,
- what is absent,
- what statistical structure can and cannot prove,
- how to discuss proposed decipherments without endorsing them,
- how to record cultural memory and public mystery without letting it grade the
  evidence.

## Method

1. Map the surviving evidence surfaces: artifact types, inscription length,
   sign inventories, location/context, and corpus catalogs.
2. Separate source classes: corpus catalog, archaeological context, statistical
   analysis, decipherment proposal, non-linguistic critique, and public mystery
   narrative.
3. Apply RUINS claim grades to a small set of representative claims.
4. Write refusals that preserve what is interesting without making the absence
   feel solved.

## Working Claims To Test

| Claim | Initial grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| The signs form a structured sign system. | Plausible | Multiple statistical studies report order, but structure is not meaning. |
| The signs encode a natural language. | Speculative | The corpus is short and lacks a bilingual anchor or known language. |
| The signs are non-linguistic symbols. | Speculative | This is a serious hypothesis, but it is also an interpretation of absence and distribution. |
| A complete decipherment currently exists. | Refuse | No generally accepted decipherment exists. |

## Null Fallback

If the paper cannot distinguish structure, language, meaning, and decipherment,
it fails as a RUINS paper. In that case, keep Indus script as a source-map
entry only and do not use it as the first case study.

## Reviewer Notes

Reviewers should block:

- treating sign frequency as translation,
- treating undeciphered as unknowable in every respect,
- endorsing a proposed decipherment,
- dismissing public fascination instead of classifying it as cultural memory,
- using "mystery" where a precise unknown can be named.
