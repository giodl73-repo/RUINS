# Refusal Register: Indus Script Non-Decipherment

**Owner:** ERRATA / Safety Editor
**Status:** publication-ready refusal register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This register turns the paper's refusals into operational rules. A refusal is
not ignorance. It is a named boundary where evidence is insufficient for a
stronger claim.

## Refusal Rules

| Tempting claim | RUINS response | Reason | Allowed substitute |
|---|---|---|---|
| "This sign means..." | Refuse | No accepted decipherment anchors sign-to-meaning. | "A proposed reading claims..." |
| "The script encodes language X." | Refuse | Language-family claims lack the required bridge. | "This is a language-family proposal." |
| "Statistical order proves natural language." | Refuse | Ordered structure can exist without translation. | "The system shows structure under this model." |
| "The system is definitely non-linguistic." | Refuse as closure | The critique is serious, but not final proof. | "A non-linguistic interpretation is a live critique." |
| "The missing bilingual means nothing can be known." | Refuse | Non-decipherment is not total ignorance. | "Translation is weak; material and structural claims remain possible." |
| "Public prizes, AI coverage, or identity stakes strengthen a reading." | Refuse | These are cultural-memory evidence, not translation evidence. | "These show closure incentives around the undeciphered condition." |
| "CISI or M77 custody permits object-level claims." | Refuse without page audit | Volume custody is not object-entry audit. | "The volume is a custody target; this specific entry needs audit." |

## Escalation Rule

Any draft sentence that crosses from trace, source posture, or model behavior
into semantic reading must be downgraded, sourced as a proposal, or removed.

## Publication Check

The current paper makes no live translation, language-family selection,
object-level identification, or solved-decipherment claim.
