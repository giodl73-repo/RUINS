# Sibling Boundary Register: Indus Script Non-Decipherment

**Owner:** Sibling Boundary Editor
**Status:** publication-ready boundary register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

RUINS owns the partial-survival and non-decipherment condition. It does not own
the whole Indus civilization, every source asset, sign identity canon, or place
history.

## Boundary Map

| Surface | RUINS role | Handoff | Do not do here |
|---|---|---|---|
| General Indus Valley civilization context | Name only where it explains the evidence condition. | MAXIM | Build a civilization overview or narrative history. |
| Corpus volumes, PDFs, source files, and access routes | Record bounded custody and claim permissions. | MUNDUS / FONTES | Become a source repository or quote bank. |
| Sign-list identities, variants, and labels | Treat as catalog decisions that affect evidence grade. | CANON | Normalize signs or maintain a canonical sign registry. |
| Sites such as Harappa and Mohenjo-daro | Mention only as source/corpus context. | PORTO | Build site geography, maps, or travel/place guides. |
| Ritual, ownership, economy, or administration | Treat as hypotheses when relevant to reconstruction status. | RITE / CERES | Explain the whole social system. |
| Public mystery, prizes, identity claims, AI coverage | Treat as afterlife and closure incentives. | RELIQUARY within RUINS; broader reception can hand off later. | Treat modern reception as ancient evidence. |

## Expansion Rule

If a section starts requiring maps, object inventories, sign-normalization
tables, long historical background, or quoted source custody, it should become a
handoff note rather than remain inside this paper.

## Current Scope Check

The current paper remains a RUINS case study: it explains what survives, what is
missing, what can be responsibly modeled, what must be refused, and how the
absence circulates later.
