# Jerusalem Archaeomagnetism and Destruction-Layer Custody

Jerusalem and Levant archaeomagnetic papers are a strong RUINS trigger because
they use burned archaeological materials to reconstruct ancient geomagnetic
field direction and intensity, then compare those signals with destruction
layers and historical narratives. The 2020 Jerusalem study anchors the magnetic
field around the Babylonian destruction, while the 2022 Levant campaign paper
uses multiple destruction layers to test regional campaign correlations.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. A magnetic signal is not a witnessed
campaign, exact date, or textual confirmation. It depends on whether a feature
was heated enough, cooled in place, remained undisturbed, was sampled with known
orientation, preserved stable magnetic minerals, and was compared against the
right regional secular variation or archaeointensity record.

RUINS promotes archaeomagnetic destruction-layer custody to Core here.
Archaeomagnetism can support bounded dating and correlation claims, but it
cannot become exact destruction date, certain campaign attribution, direct
textual confirmation, complete site chronology, conquest map, radiocarbon
replacement, synchronized regional destruction, or proof of scripture/history
without separate custody.
