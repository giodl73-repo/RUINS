# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- The 2020 PLOS ONE Jerusalem study reconstructs geomagnetic field direction and
  intensity from burned floor segments associated with the Babylonian
  destruction.
- The 2022 PNAS/PMC study compares archaeomagnetic signals from multiple Levant
  destruction layers with historically known military campaigns.
- Archaeomagnetic dating depends on burned materials that cooled in place,
  preserved thermoremanent magnetization, and can be compared with regional
  calibration records.
- Public coverage frames archaeomagnetic work as a way to date biblical events,
  creating proof-story and radiocarbon-replacement pressure.

Future-locked:

- Exact destruction date from magnetism alone.
- Certain campaign attribution.
- Direct textual confirmation.
- Complete site chronology or conquest map.
- Replacement of radiocarbon/history.
- Proof of scripture or national history.
