# Cultural Memory: Joya de Ceren

Joya de Ceren's public afterlife often centers on the phrase "Pompeii of the
Americas" and the idea of ordinary Maya life frozen in place.

## Memory Surfaces

| Memory surface | RUINS handling |
|---|---|
| Pompeii of the Americas | Public comparison; not evidence equivalence. |
| Frozen Maya village | Reception phrase; not complete daily life. |
| Ordinary people preserved | Valuable interpretive frame; not whole society. |
| Last meal or last action | Refused unless source-locked at microsequence level. |
| UNESCO uniqueness | Heritage value, not total recovery. |

## Rule

Public memory may explain why Joya de Ceren matters. It may not fill missing
household, crop, activity, escape, or whole-society evidence.
