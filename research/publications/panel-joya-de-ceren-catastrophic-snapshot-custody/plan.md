# Plan: Joya de Ceren Catastrophic Snapshot Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a disaster-preserved farming village when household and
agricultural preservation tempts whole-daily-life and final-moment claims?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Joya de Ceren as a Classic-period village in El Salvador buried by volcanic
  ash from Loma Caldera.
- UNESCO framing for settlement foundation, destruction, discovery, and
  outstanding preservation.
- Preserved architecture, household contexts, agricultural/plant evidence, and
  "Pompeii of the Americas" public comparison as evidence layers.

Refused:
- Complete daily life of ordinary Maya people.
- Exact final moment or activity sequence.
- Whole household inventory or whole village plan.
- Certain escape story from absent bodies alone.
- Complete crop economy or diet.
- Civilizational or Maya-history synthesis.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Catastrophic-village snapshot gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.
9. Cross-case catastrophic-snapshot checklist.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:catastrophic-snapshot-preservation-custody` to Core
by pairing Akrotiri's urban ash-preservation with Joya de Ceren's household and
agricultural catastrophic preservation, then adding rubric v1.18 controls.
