# Kennewick / Ancient One and Ancient Genomics Custody

Kennewick Man, also known by many tribal communities as The Ancient One, is the
unlike second test for ancient genomics reconstruction custody. Cheddar Man
stressed phenotype prediction and facial reconstruction. Kennewick stresses
affiliation, governance, legal custody, morphology conflict, repatriation, and
living-community relation.

The Nature paper sequenced the genome to approximately 1x coverage and compared
it to worldwide genomic data. It found that Kennewick was closer to modern
Native Americans than to any other extant population, and that among available
groups several appeared descended from a population closely related to him,
including the Colville. Public and institutional records also document the
long legal dispute and the 2017 return of the remains to tribes who claimed him
as an ancestor.

RUINS promotes ancient genomics custody to Core because this case proves the
method must handle more than ancestry graphs. Genome evidence can challenge a
prior morphology claim and inform affiliation, but it cannot alone become living
community authority, cultural continuity, legal policy, complete identity, or
the final story of early North America.
