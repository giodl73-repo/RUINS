# Chronology: Knossos Restoration Projection

**Owner:** Absence Historian
**Status:** working chronology

## Layers

| Layer | Approximate horizon | Evidence condition | RUINS note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bronze Age Knossos / Minoan palatial phases | second millennium BCE | excavated material survival | Not directly visible without excavation/conservation mediation. |
| Later ruin and burial of the site | post-Bronze Age to modern discovery | survival and loss process | Determines what fabric reached excavators. |
| Early excavation and naming | late 19th / early 20th century | excavation record and interpretation | Evans and predecessors shape the modern evidence surface. |
| Evans reconstruction / reconstitution | 1900-1930s | modern concrete, paint, columns, display | A modern artifact layered onto ancient remains. |
| Later critique and conservation | mid-20th century to present | scholarship and site management | Restorations become both problem and protected heritage surface. |
| UNESCO / serial heritage framing | 2025 inscription context | modern institutional recognition | Confirms heritage significance, not every reconstruction. |
| Popular afterlife | contemporary tourism/media | cultural memory | Restored imagery becomes the public "Minoan palace." |

## Chronology Controls

1. Do not let Evans-era reconstruction collapse into Bronze Age fabric.
2. Do not use modern heritage recognition as proof of specific room/fresco
   reconstructions.
3. Do not treat critique of reconstruction as proof that the site is fake.
4. Do not use mythic Minos/labyrinth language as archaeological evidence.
