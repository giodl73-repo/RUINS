## Memory Forms

| Memory form | Evidence surface | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Palace of Minos | Evans-era naming and mythic frame. | Cultural-memory and interpretation layer, not direct ancient self-description. |
| Labyrinth / Minotaur | Mythic afterlife repeatedly associated with Knossos. | Treat as reception unless source-locked to a specific ancient/later text. |
| Red columns and painted palace | Visitor-facing image shaped by restoration. | Use as modern visual afterlife. |
| "Fake Knossos" critique | Modern pushback against over-restoration. | Useful warning, but too broad as a claim grade. |
| Modernist Knossos | Gere's frame for reinforced-concrete reconstruction and twentieth-century projection. | Interpretive scholarship about modern reception. |
| UNESCO heritage status | Global heritage recognition. | Custody/significance evidence, not restoration proof. |
| Digital restoration / AR | Modern attempt to separate or reimagine layers. | New reconstruction surface, not ancient state. |

## Publication Rules

1. Put Minos, Labyrinth, and Minotaur language in cultural-memory context.
2. Treat red-column imagery as restored visual memory unless source-labeled.
3. Do not describe digital models as direct recovery of the Bronze Age palace.
4. Keep critique of Evans specific rather than replacing one myth with another.
