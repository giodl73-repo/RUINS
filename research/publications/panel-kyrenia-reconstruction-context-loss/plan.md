# Plan: Kyrenia Reconstruction Context Loss

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Shipwrecks and Context Loss
**Status:** activation-ready working paper
**Position in chain:** second shipwreck case after Uluburun activation

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a shipwreck whose survival is unusually strong but
whose meaning now depends on excavation, conservation, reconstruction, replica,
digital catalog, and revised dating layers?

## Core Argument

Kyrenia tests whether `ruins-capability:shipwreck-context-loss` works outside
Uluburun's spectacular Late Bronze Age cargo. The Kyrenia ship preserves hull,
cargo, and excavation records well enough to support strong questions about
ancient shipbuilding and maritime practice. It also forces a different RUINS
boundary: reconstructed hull, museum display, replica sailing, catalog
database, and revised dating work are modern knowledge layers, not the ship's
original operating context.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not treat reconstruction as the original ship.
2. Do not treat cargo origin as a complete route map.
3. Do not treat final report summaries as permission for exact object claims.
4. Do not treat radiocarbon/date revision as a complete voyage narrative.
5. Do not publish site-location, diving, recovery, or collecting guidance.
