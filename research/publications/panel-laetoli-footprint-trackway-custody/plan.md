# Plan: Laetoli Footprint Trackway Custody

**Owner:** ASHLAR
**Status:** publication-ready
**Date:** 2026-07-06

## Problem

The Laetoli footprints are direct traces, but directness can be misleading.
Footprints preserved in volcanic ash support bipedalism and movement claims at
a trace level. They do not automatically settle individual identity, exact
species, social relation, intention, or full locomotor reconstruction.

## RUINS Claim

Laetoli tests cast-void identity custody beyond Pompeii. A footprint is a
negative impression rather than a body cast, yet the same discipline applies:
separate substrate, footprint, trackway, cast/scan, taxon, gait, group relation,
and public memory.

## Work Surface

1. Lock Smithsonian claims for Site G trail length, footprint count, age,
   preservation in wet volcanic ash, bipedal gait, and likely attribution.
2. Lock Nature claims for Site A rediscovery, 3D documentation, disputed
   bear/hominin attribution, locomotor diversity, and scan/cast limits.
3. Build a cast-void custody gate for trackways.
4. Refuse species certainty, whole-gait reconstruction, family/group stories,
   and "first human steps" closure beyond source scope.
5. Promote `ruins-capability:cast-void-identity-custody` to Core with a
   checklist and rubric cap.
