# Cultural Memory: Lalibela

Lalibela's public memory is shaped by New Jerusalem, living pilgrimage,
monolithic church images, holy-city language, and the visual drama of churches
cut down into rock. These frames explain why the site matters, but they can make
one image look like the whole complex.

RUINS may discuss this memory as reception. It may not use it to prove:

- complete site history from one church image,
- final religious meaning,
- finished conservation,
- complete drainage reconstruction,
- original iconography from damaged paintings or sculptures,
- or total Ethiopian Christian history.

The key memory lesson is that living use and iconic form are powerful evidence
layers, but they are not substitutes for drainage, condition, conservation, or
whole-site custody.
