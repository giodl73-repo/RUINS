# Plan: Lalibela Rock-Cut Church Shelter Custody

## Scope

Use Lalibela as the second case for rock-cut facade erosion custody after Petra.
The paper tests whether the capability works across a sandstone rock-cut city
and a living monolithic church complex.

## Current Claims

- Lalibela contains eleven medieval monolithic cave churches.
- The churches are hewn from living rock and linked by trenches, drainage
  ditches, ceremonial passages, and related features.
- The complex remains a pilgrimage and devotion site.
- Drainage disruption, water damage, structural risks, mural degradation,
  sculpture damage, and shelters affect integrity.
- Temporary shelters can protect some churches while also impacting visual
  integrity.

## Refused Claims

- One church image is the whole site.
- Shelter equals finished conservation.
- Living pilgrimage proves every original meaning.
- Drainage features are a complete site-engineering reconstruction.
- Lalibela alone explains Ethiopian Christianity or medieval Ethiopia.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:rock-cut-facade-erosion-custody` from Active to Core
by adding a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.22 cap.
