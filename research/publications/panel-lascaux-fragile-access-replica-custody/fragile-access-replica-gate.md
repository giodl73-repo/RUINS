# Fragile Access Replica Gate

## Gate Rule

When a fragile original is inaccessible, every claim must name whether it rests
on the original site, conservation record, replica, digital image, visitor
experience, or later cultural memory.

## Evidence Layers

| Layer | Can support | Cannot support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Original cave | Site existence, parietal art, rock context where source-locked. | Public access or current condition. |
| Conservation closure | Access restriction and protection rationale. | Complete conservation status. |
| Environmental monitoring | Condition claims when source-locked. | Meaning, dating, or visitor experience. |
| Replica/facsimile | Public access, display, and interpretation. | Object equivalence. |
| Digital/virtual visit | Visual access and pedagogy. | Material measurement or final reading. |
| Animal imagery | Motif and image description. | Ritual certainty or whole-culture interpretation. |
| Dating claims | Chronology where specialist source-locked. | Final authorship or cultural identity. |
| Public memory | Reception and modern value. | Original function. |

## Publication Test

Before publication, each Lascaux claim must answer:

1. Is this original, conservation, replica, digital, image, chronology, or
   memory evidence?
2. Is the viewer accessing the original or a mediated surrogate?
3. Does the claim require CERES, RITE, PORTO, CANON, MAXIM/LUCIA, or digital
   experience handoff?
4. Is the exact claim being made, refused, or demoted to P3?
