# Page Anchor Audit: Lascaux

## Anchored Claims

| Claim | Anchor posture | Current grade |
|---|---|---|
| Lascaux was discovered in 1940 and is central to prehistoric-art history. | French Ministry and UNESCO. | Strong. |
| The original works are inaccessible to ordinary public access and mediated through replicas/virtual visits. | French Ministry. | Strong. |
| Lascaux IV is publicly framed as a full and exact replica. | Lascaux IV official source. | Strong as replica-access claim. |
| UNESCO highlights about 100 animal figures for detail, colour, and vivid rendering. | UNESCO. | Strong. |
| Final dating, conservation status, ritual meaning, and whole-culture claims are refused in current scope. | Refusal register. | Strong refusal. |

## Anchor Limits

- A replica source anchors visitor experience, not original cave material status.
- UNESCO value anchors significance, not final interpretation.
- Ministry access framing anchors mediation, not complete conservation science.
- Virtual tours anchor public visual access, not direct measurement.
