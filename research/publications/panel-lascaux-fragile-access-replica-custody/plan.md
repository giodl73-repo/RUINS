# Plan: Lascaux Fragile Access Replica Custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a fragile original site when public access and most
public knowledge are mediated by replicas, virtual visits, and institutional
interpretation?

## Scope

Allowed:
- Lascaux as a decorated Palaeolithic cave discovered in 1940 and central to
  the Vézère Valley World Heritage property.
- French Ministry framing that replicas and virtual visits allow discovery of
  inaccessible works.
- Lascaux IV as a complete replica of the original cave, per official visitor
  framing.
- UNESCO framing of Lascaux's animal figures and prehistoric-art importance.

Refused:
- Public replica access as original cave access.
- Replica fidelity as object equivalence.
- Complete conservation status.
- Final dating or final interpretation.
- Ritual certainty from animal imagery.
- Whole prehistoric culture from cave art alone.

## Required Artifacts

1. Source map and source lock.
2. Page-anchor audit.
3. Fragile-access replica gate.
4. Claim audit.
5. Refusal register.
6. Cultural-memory register.
7. Sibling-boundary register.
8. Role review and scorecard.

## Promotion Target

Fast-promote `ruins-capability:fragile-site-access-replica-custody` from
Candidate to Active if the package blocks original/replica/digital collapse
without weakening legitimate conservation-access claims.
