# Plan: Library of Alexandria Loss and Memory

## Question

How should RUINS handle a famous loss whose public memory often compresses
several weakly connected historical processes into one dramatic burning?

## Deliverables

1. Separate the Library, Mouseion, Serapeum, book warehouses, and later memory.
2. Treat Caesar's Alexandrian fire as one source tradition, not the complete
   explanation of the Library's fate.
3. Track the Serapeum and late antique evidence without turning it into a simple
   replacement for the Ptolemaic Library.
4. Refuse the Arab-conquest destruction story unless treated as later legend and
   source history.
5. Explain why the Library became a cultural-memory symbol for lost knowledge.

## First Pass Scope

This pass is a working paper. It may use ancient witnesses and bibliographic
secondary targets, but it should not claim to solve the Library's final fate.

## Out Of Scope

- Full history of Hellenistic scholarship.
- Object-level reconstruction of the Library's holdings.
- Map of ancient Alexandria.
- Complete bibliography of all modern Alexandria scholarship.
