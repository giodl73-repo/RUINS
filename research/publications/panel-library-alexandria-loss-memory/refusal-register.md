# Refusal Register: Library of Alexandria Loss and Memory

**Owner:** ERRATA / Safety Editor
**Status:** publication-scope refusal register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This register turns the paper's refusals into operational rules. A refusal is a
named evidence boundary, not a denial that loss occurred.

## Refusal Rules

| Tempting claim | RUINS response | Reason | Allowed substitute |
|---|---|---|---|
| "The Library burned in one event." | Refuse as closure | The source layers do not support one final destruction event. | "One source tradition reports..." |
| "Caesar destroyed the whole Library." | Refuse as total claim | Plutarch and Dio support fire/book-loss traditions, not complete final fate. | "Plutarch reports a Caesar-fire tradition." |
| "The Serapeum was simply the Great Library." | Refuse without identity audit | Institution identity is unresolved in this paper. | "The Serapeum is a related but separate source problem." |
| "The Arab conquest explains the Library's fate." | Refuse as direct evidence | The story is late and not source-locked here. | "Later medieval accounts attribute destruction to Umar." |
| "The Library contained all ancient knowledge." | Refuse literally | This is modern universal-library symbolism. | "The Library became a symbol of lost knowledge." |
| "Modern grief proves ancient holdings." | Refuse | Reception is afterlife evidence, not collection evidence. | "Modern memory shows the loss became culturally powerful." |

## Escalation Rule

Any sentence that assigns final blame, reconstructs holdings, or merges Library,
Museum, Serapeum, and warehouses must be downgraded, sourced as a tradition, or
removed.

## Current Scope Check

The current paper makes no holdings reconstruction, no final-destruction claim,
and no direct historical claim from the Arab-conquest story.
