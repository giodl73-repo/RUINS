# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| London Mithraeum is an urban-overbuild reconstruction case. | Strong | Official sources describe discovery, redevelopment, relocation/reconstruction, and current display. |
| The public display is mediated by reconstruction and experience design. | Strong | London Mithraeum and MOLA anchor reconstructed remains plus light, haze, and sound. |
| The earlier reconstruction did not preserve original position/orientation. | Strong | Historic England and MOLA both anchor relocation and reconstruction status. |
| The current display proves complete Roman ritual practice. | Refused | Ritual interpretation is not locked and immersive effects are visitor interpretation. |
| The reconstructed temple is object-equivalent to the original Roman context. | Refused | Original fabric, modern materials, relocation, and display context must remain separate. |
| Artefacts from the excavation reconstruct all Roman London activity. | Refused | Artefact counts support recovery scale, not total urban history. |
| Public queues prove archaeological completeness. | Refused | Public memory is afterlife evidence. |
