# Madjedbebe OSL and First-Arrival Custody

Madjedbebe OSL dating is a strong RUINS trigger because it dates sediment burial
around archaeological artefacts at one of the most important early occupation
sites in Sahul. The 2017 Nature paper reports new excavations and argues for
human occupation of northern Australia around 65,000 years ago, using
stratigraphic integrity, artefact refits, optical dating, and other sediment
analyses to support the association.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. OSL dates sediment burial, not a person
arriving, an artefact being made, or a migration route being travelled. It
depends on sample location, grain population, light exposure before burial,
partial bleaching, equivalent-dose distribution, dose-rate environment,
stratigraphy, disturbance checks, and the independent association between dated
sediment and artefacts.

RUINS promotes OSL sediment-age custody to Core here. OSL can support bounded
occupation-timing claims, but it cannot become exact human arrival date, exact
artefact age, certain in-situ association, complete site integrity, migration
route, megafauna-extinction cause, genetic history, continuous occupation, or
complete peopling model without separate custody.
