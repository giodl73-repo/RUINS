# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- The 2017 Nature Madjedbebe paper reports new excavations and optical dating
  supporting human occupation of northern Australia by about 65,000 years ago.
- The paper frames artefacts as concentrated in stratigraphic bands and uses
  refits, optical dating, and sediment analyses to support stratigraphic
  integrity.
- OSL dates time since sediment grains were last exposed to light and buried,
  not directly the time an artefact was made or used.
- Public summaries connect the result to first-arrival, sea-crossing, and
  megafauna-extinction debates, creating memory pressure.

Future-locked:

- Exact human arrival date.
- Exact artefact age.
- Certain in-situ association without stratigraphic custody.
- Migration route or genetic history.
- Megafauna extinction cause.
- Complete peopling model.
