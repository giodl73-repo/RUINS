# Source Lock

Current publication claims are locked to the University of Hawaii news page, the
Nottingham Trent repository metadata/abstract, and the Tell Timai project
publication page.

## Locked Claims

- The Tell Timai Project excavates ancient Thmuis in the Nile Delta.
- Thmuis is described as home to famous Mendesian and Metopian perfumes.
- Archaeological evidence includes third-century BCE kilns and perfume bottles.
- A liquid-production area was uncovered near the kilns.
- Residue analysis of amphora contents was being conducted.
- Researchers recreated Mendesian and Metopian perfumes based on formulas in
  ancient Greek texts.
- The published article used Classics, Egyptology, and experimental archaeology.
- Classical sources and paleobotany were important for identifying ingredients
  such as camphor and balanos.
- The Tell Timai perfumery contributed to reconstructing perfume manufacture.
- The recreated Mendesian was exhibited and publicly experienced as a scent.
