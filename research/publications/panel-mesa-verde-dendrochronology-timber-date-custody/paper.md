# Mesa Verde Tree Rings and Timber-Date Custody

Dendrochronology is one of the strongest chronological tools in archaeology
because tree rings can be crossdated to annual sequences. Mesa Verde and the
broader US Southwest show why that strength matters: beams, posts, repair
timbers, and cutting clusters can refine construction and remodeling histories
that broad typology or radiocarbon ranges cannot resolve alone.

The evidence remains bounded. A tree-ring date is not automatically a building
completion date, occupation span, abandonment date, drought-causation proof,
community census, timber-trade map, or household biography. The claim depends
on sample context, tree species, ring-sequence quality, whether the terminal
ring or bark edge survives, whether the beam was reused or curated, the
reference chronology, construction context, repair episodes, and how dated
timbers are distributed across rooms and phases.

RUINS promotes dendrochronological timber-date custody to Core here. Annual
tree-ring precision can support bounded felling, construction, repair, and
chronology claims, but it cannot become exact building completion, complete
occupation history, abandonment, drought collapse, community size, timber trade,
forest management, or settlement synthesis without separate custody.
