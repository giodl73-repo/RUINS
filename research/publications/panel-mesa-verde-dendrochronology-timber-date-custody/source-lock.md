# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Archaeological dendrochronology can assign calendar dates to tree-ring
  sequences when crossdating and reference chronologies support the match.
- Mesa Verde and Southwest tree-ring work can support construction and
  remodeling chronologies when sampled timbers and contexts are controlled.
- Terminal-ring condition, sapwood, bark edge, reuse, repair, and context affect
  whether a tree-ring date can be read as a felling, construction, or use date.
- Dendrochronological climate and provenance signals require separate custody
  before they become drought, migration, trade, or forest-management history.

Future-locked:

- Exact building completion date.
- Complete occupation span.
- Abandonment date from last dated timber.
- Drought-caused migration or collapse.
- Community size from beam counts.
- Complete timber-trade or forest-management system.
