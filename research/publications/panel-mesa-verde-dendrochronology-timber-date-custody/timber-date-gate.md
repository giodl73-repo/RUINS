# Timber-Date Gate

Before using a tree-ring date as archaeological chronology, lock:

1. Sample identity, site, room, feature, collection history, and publication.
2. Species, wood condition, ring-sequence length, and crossdating quality.
3. Terminal-ring status: bark edge, waney edge, sapwood, heartwood, or unknown.
4. Felling, transport, stockpiling, reuse, repair, and secondary placement.
5. Construction context, remodeling episode, and room/feature association.
6. Reference chronology, match uncertainty, and provenance inference.
7. Sibling handoffs for occupation, abandonment, drought, migration, trade,
   community size, forest management, and public exact-year stories.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to dated wood or bounded felling
evidence rather than building, occupation, or settlement history.
