# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| The Met sphinx is a lost-polychromy trace case. | Strong | The Met anchors pigment remains, methods, and reconstruction. |
| Scientific methods identified multiple pigments. | Strong | The Met names methods and pigment identifications. |
| Reconstructions are mediated research outputs. | Strong | The Met says they draw on analysis, imaging, and research. |
| The reconstruction is the original object restored. | Refused | Reconstruction is a hypothesis/display layer. |
| Pigment traces prove the complete color scheme. | Refused | Traces and samples are partial evidence. |
| Color evidence settles final funerary meaning. | Refused | Meaning belongs to RITE and requires separate custody. |
| The case defines all Greek aesthetics. | Refused | One object and exhibition frame cannot stand for a whole culture. |
