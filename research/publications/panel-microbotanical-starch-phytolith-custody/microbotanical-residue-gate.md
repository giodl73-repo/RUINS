# Microbotanical Residue Gate

Before using plant microremains as plant-use evidence, lock:

1. Sample identity, artefact/sediment/calculus context, collection, and lab record.
2. Microremain type: starch, phytolith, pollen, fiber, mixed residue, or unknown.
3. Preservation, degradation, burning, cooking, burial, and soil chemistry.
4. Contamination controls from field, handling, sediment, lab, and modern plants.
5. Extraction protocol, microscope method, images, counts, and observer process.
6. Reference collection, taxonomic criteria, morphotype uncertainty, and assignment grade.
7. Processing damage, use-wear, residue formation, activity inference, and quantification.
8. Sibling handoffs for diet, domestication, tool function, cooking, medicine,
   craft, storage, seasonality, and public ancient-meal stories.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to bounded microremain presence
rather than diet, domestication, function, recipe, or plant-use history.
