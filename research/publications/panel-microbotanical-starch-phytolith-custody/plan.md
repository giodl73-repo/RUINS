# Plan

## Question

Can RUINS use starch, phytolith, and plant-residue evidence without turning
microremains into complete diets, domestication events, or tool functions?

## Scope

- Use plant-residue and starch reviews as method pressure.
- Use phytolith/starch taphonomy papers as preservation and contamination pressure.
- Use dental calculus and lithic-residue studies as context-pressure examples.
- Separate sample, microremain, preservation, contamination, extraction,
  reference collection, assignment, processing, quantification, synthesis, and
  memory.

## Out Of Scope

- No exact species without taxonomic custody.
- No complete diet, direct meal, domestication proof, crop-adoption date,
  cooking recipe, medicine, craft process, storage system, or seasonal calendar.
