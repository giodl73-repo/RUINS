# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Starch, phytolith, pollen, and other plant microremains can survive in
  archaeological residues, dental calculus, sediments, and carbonized deposits.
- Plant microremain interpretation requires preservation, contamination,
  extraction, reference collection, and taxonomic uncertainty controls.
- Processing, cooking, grinding, charring, chewing, and burial can alter plant
  microremains and affect interpretation.
- Microbotanical evidence can support bounded plant-presence and residue claims,
  but diet, domestication, tool function, recipe, medicine, craft, and seasonal
  claims require separate custody.

Future-locked:

- Exact species.
- Complete diet or direct meal.
- Domestication proof or crop-adoption date.
- Complete tool function or cooking recipe.
- Medicine, craft process, storage system, or seasonal calendar.
