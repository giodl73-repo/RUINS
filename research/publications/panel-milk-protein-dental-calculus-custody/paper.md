# Milk Protein Dental Calculus and Dairying Reconstruction Custody

The 2014 Scientific Reports milk-protein paper is the unlike second test for
dental calculus custody. The Neanderthal case used shotgun DNA to infer diet,
microbiome, and health. This case uses protein tandem mass spectrometry and the
whey protein beta-lactoglobulin, preserved in human dental calculus, as direct
evidence of milk consumption from the Bronze Age to the present.

The evidence is strong but still bounded. Beta-lactoglobulin can support
individual milk-consumption and, in some cases, species-source claims for
cattle, sheep, and goat milk products. It does not by itself reconstruct exact
meals, cuisine, daily diet, herd management, lactase-persistence genotype,
complete dairying economy, or cultural adaptation.

RUINS promotes dental calculus reconstruction custody to Core here. Dental
calculus can preserve different evidence classes, but the method must keep
matrix, sampling, assay, protein assignment, contamination, consumption
inference, food-production history, and public memory separate.
