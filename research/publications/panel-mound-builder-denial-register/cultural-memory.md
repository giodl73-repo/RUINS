## Memory Forms

| Memory form | Evidence surface | RUINS treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Lost race / vanished civilization | Nineteenth-century denial literature and public myth | Interpretive violence; not live archaeology. |
| Giants | Popular pseudoarchaeological afterlife | Public myth; refuse as authorship. |
| Ancient astronauts or visitors | Modern pseudoarchaeology | Public afterlife; refuse as evidence. |
| Ten Lost Tribes / Biblical origins | Antiquarian and religious framing | Historiographic surface; not authorship proof. |
| "Mystery" tourism/media | Public compression | Replace with source-scoped evidence and unanswered questions. |
| Indigenous sacred relation | Living memory and heritage custody | Sensitive custody layer, not decorative folklore. |
| UNESCO / World Heritage recognition | Modern public heritage | Afterlife and protection layer, not total interpretation proof. |
| Professional correction | Smithsonian/Bureau and later archaeology | Archive layer showing denial was contested and corrected. |

## Publication Rules

1. Keep lost-race, giant, ancient-visitor, and Biblical-origin claims inside
   denial-history or public-memory sections.
2. Do not use "mystery" language when the mystery depends on denying Indigenous
   capability.
3. Separate Indigenous sacred relation from exact archaeological attribution.
4. Treat World Heritage status as public custody and recognition, not proof of
   every site detail.
