# Plan: Mound Builder Denial Register

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Monumental Sites and Contested Interpretation
**Status:** publication-ready
**Position in chain:** second colonial-denial-register case after Great Zimbabwe

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle North American mound and earthwork interpretation when
nineteenth-century "Mound Builder" stories denied Indigenous authorship by
inventing a vanished race, ancient visitors, giants, or other non-Native
builders?

## Core Argument

The Mound Builder myth is a RUINS case because the ruins were not merely
fragmentary. Their interpretation was damaged by denial. Earthworks, mounds,
burials, artifacts, geometry, and astronomical alignments survive as evidence
of Indigenous monumental practice. The "lost race" story is not an equal
explanation. It is a modern interpretive layer that converted Indigenous
achievement into ownerless mystery and, in some versions, into a rationale for
dispossession.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not treat "Mound Builders" as a lost people separate from Indigenous
   histories without source-specific custody.
2. Do not turn earthwork scale, geometry, or artifact richness into proof of
   non-Native authorship.
3. Do not flatten many mound-building traditions into one people, tribe, site,
   or date.
4. Do not use modern sacred memory as a shortcut to every archaeological
   identification.
5. Do not provide excavation, collecting, or site-location guidance.

## Promotion Decision

This paper, paired with Great Zimbabwe, promotes
`ruins-capability:colonial-denial-register` to Core. The reusable method is the
register pattern: separate material evidence, current source-scoped archaeology,
denial narrative, public mystery framing, and living/heritage memory.
