# Cultural Memory: Nasca

Nasca's public memory is dominated by giant images, aerial photographs, mystery
language, puzzle framing, and sensational explanations. Those frames explain why
the site travels so widely in public culture. They are not evidence that the
ancient builders expected aircraft, that one interpretive key explains every
line, or that public outlines are complete archaeological records.

RUINS may discuss mystery memory as afterlife. It may not use that memory to
prove:

- ancient aerial intended viewers,
- alien or secret-history explanations,
- one final ritual or astronomical function,
- a complete inventory,
- complete damage status,
- or a whole-culture explanation.

The important public-memory lesson is methodological: a site can become famous
through the scale that makes it visible, while the evidence itself remains a
ground surface requiring source custody.
