# Plan: Nasca Geoglyph Scale Visibility Custody

## Scope

Use Nasca as a pressure discovery for scale-mediated surface survival. The paper
asks how RUINS should handle a vast ground surface that becomes public knowledge
through aerial imagery, satellite/radar data, AI candidate discovery, field
confirmation, maps, and mystery memory.

## Current Claims

- Nasca and Palpa geoglyphs are large desert surface modifications and a UNESCO
  World Heritage property.
- The Peruvian Ministry of Culture presents the site as a large symbolic
  cultural landscape of lines, swept fields, and zoomorphic/anthropomorphic
  figures.
- Remote-sensing imagery can support protection by identifying fragile terrain
  and human disturbance.
- AI-assisted survey can identify likely geoglyph candidates that still require
  screening and field confirmation.
- Line-type and relief-type figures may differ in scale, motif, distribution,
  and viewing context.

## Refused Claims

- Aerial visibility proves ancient aerial intended viewers.
- AI detection equals field-confirmed discovery.
- Public outlines are complete archaeological records.
- Nasca has one final ritual, astronomical, or communicative function.
- A damage or monitoring image is a complete condition report.
- Nasca alone explains Paracas/Nasca society.
