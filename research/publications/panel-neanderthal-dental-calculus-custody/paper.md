# Neanderthal Dental Calculus and Residue-Life Reconstruction Custody

The 2017 Nature dental-calculus paper is a RUINS pressure case because it turns
calcified plaque into evidence for diet, oral microbiome, disease, ecology, and
possible plant use. The study shotgun-sequenced ancient DNA from five
Neanderthal dental-calculus specimens and reported regional differences between
Belgian and Spanish individuals, including dietary and microbial signals.

The same case is risky. A small number of specimens, a mixed matrix, taxonomic
assignment limits, contamination, and later cautionary work can all affect how
strongly diet or health claims should be stated. A poplar-associated signal may
be interesting; it is not automatically deliberate medicine. A pathogen or
microbiome signal is not a complete diagnosis. A few calculus specimens are not
a total Neanderthal lifestyle.

RUINS therefore activates dental calculus reconstruction custody. Calculus can
be a remarkable archive, but every claim must keep sample, method, taxon,
microbiome, diet, health, behaviour, and public memory separate.
