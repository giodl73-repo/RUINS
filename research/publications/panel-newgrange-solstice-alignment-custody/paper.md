# Newgrange and Winter-Solstice Alignment Custody

Newgrange is a strong RUINS case because the visible winter-solstice phenomenon
is real, famous, restored, documented, and culturally powerful. The National
Museum of Ireland describes the roof-box as channeling sunlight along the
passage to illuminate the chamber at sunrise on the winter solstice. Recent
World Heritage research documents the phenomenon with high-resolution
photography and videography, and uses blocked-daylight conditions to simulate a
probable Neolithic lighting condition.

Those anchors support a careful alignment claim: Newgrange has a winter
solstice light event mediated by roof-box architecture and modern observation.
They do not by themselves settle exact calendrical precision, full ritual
meaning, design certainty, or continuous local memory.

RUINS therefore activates astronomical alignment reconstruction custody. The
case must hold apart present fabric, reconstructed fabric, excavation history,
observation window, simulated conditions, sky geometry, calendrical inference,
ritual interpretation, and public solstice afterlife.
