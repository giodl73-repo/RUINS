# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Geochemical methods can assign many obsidian artefacts to geological sources. | Strong | Requires source library, calibration, and assignment grade. |
| pXRF can support non-destructive field or museum source work. | Strong | Method stays instrument-scoped. |
| Hydration rims can provide chronological pressure. | Bounded | Requires rate model, water content, and thermal history. |
| Source assignment proves a direct trade route. | Refused | Route and exchange mechanism are sibling synthesis. |
| Hydration rim gives exact artefact age. | Refused | Rim and rate uncertainty block exact dating. |
| Source clusters prove ethnicity, territoriality, or political alliance. | Refused | Identity and political claims require separate custody. |
