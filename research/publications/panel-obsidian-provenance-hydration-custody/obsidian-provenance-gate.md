# Obsidian Provenance Gate

Before using obsidian as provenance, dating, or exchange evidence, lock:

1. Artefact identity, context, collection history, and publication.
2. Geological source library, sub-source structure, and comparison set.
3. Instrument method, calibration, standards, elements, thresholds, and error.
4. Source assignment grade: match, likely match, ambiguous, nonmatch, or unknown.
5. Hydration surface, rim measurement, intrinsic water, diffusion rate, and
   thermal history.
6. Cortex, reduction stage, use/discard context, curation, and redeposition.
7. Sibling handoffs for routes, migration, ethnicity, markets, alliances,
   territoriality, and public network stories.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to bounded obsidian source or
hydration pressure rather than exchange, chronology, identity, or network proof.
