# Oplontis Villa A and Paradata Transparency Custody

Oplontis Villa A is the concrete project test that paradata transparency needed.
The project publishes a large open digital scholarly stack around Villa A:
open-access volumes, a project database, and a fully navigable 3D model. The
King's Visualisation Lab page describes a navigable three-dimensional model
documenting setting, architecture, and decor. Public project material says a
fully navigable model accompanies the digital publication and that the database
of excavated material is available for study.

That is exactly where RUINS needs paradata custody. A model can reconnect wall
painting fragments, show restored and actual states, and support lighting or
viewing experiments. But the evidential strength depends on whether source
evidence, metadata, decision history, uncertainty, actual/restored distinction,
and public communication remain inspectable.

The Oplontis case promotes paradata transparency to Core: a digital
reconstruction is not strong because it is immersive. It is strong only where
its reconstruction process is auditable.
