# Plan: Palmyra Replica Reconstruction

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Reconstruction and Modern Projection
**Status:** stable working paper
**Position in chain:** post-Maya case testing replica-status custody

## Research Question

How should RUINS grade Palmyra reconstruction claims when damaged monuments,
digital models, public replicas, emergency conservation, and formal restoration
governance all circulate as if they were the same recovery?

## Core Argument

Palmyra is a RUINS case because the damaged site, the documented site, the
replica arch, and authorized restoration work are different evidence layers.
Digital and physical replicas can resist erasure, educate publics, and preserve
measurement traces. They cannot by themselves restore the original monument or
authorize reconstruction at the World Heritage property.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not treat the Trafalgar Square arch replica as the original Arch of
   Triumph.
2. Do not treat photogrammetry or public 3D models as conservation approval.
3. Do not claim Palmyra has been rebuilt because one replica circulated.
4. Separate documentation, emergency stabilization, restoration, reconstruction,
   public art, and cultural-memory claims.
5. Treat UNESCO/World Heritage Committee governance as a claim boundary, not
   decoration.
