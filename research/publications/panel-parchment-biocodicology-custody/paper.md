# Parchment Biocodicology and Manuscript Material Custody

Parchment biocodicology is a strong RUINS trigger because it turns manuscript
surfaces into biological archives. Field guides and case studies describe
sampling parchment with conservation eraser crumbs, then using ZooMS peptide
mass fingerprinting, DNA, mtDNA, microbiome, and proteomic methods to identify
animal species and study manuscript production.

The evidence is valuable because it can recover material information without
reading the text itself. It is also risky because a folio-level species call can
be promoted too quickly into complete manuscript provenance, scriptorium
attribution, trade route, herd history, library reconstruction, conservation
status, or textual authority.

RUINS promotes parchment biocodicology custody to Core here. Parchment
biomolecular signals can support bounded material and production questions, but
they cannot become complete provenance, exact animal identity, scriptorium
attribution, trade route, herd history, library reconstruction, textual
authority, or conservation status without separate custody.
