# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Biocodicology uses biological analysis of parchment to study manuscripts as
  material and biological objects.
- Field-guide sources describe eraser crumbs as a sampling route for parchment
  protein, DNA, and microbiome analysis.
- ZooMS uses collagen peptide mass fingerprinting for taxonomic identification
  and is widely applied in archaeology and cultural heritage.
- Parchment case studies use non-invasive peptide fingerprinting to identify
  animal species such as calf, sheep, and goat.
- Library-scale biocodicology work collects eraser crumbs from manuscript
  leaves and applies MALDI-ToF and LC-MS/MS workflows.
