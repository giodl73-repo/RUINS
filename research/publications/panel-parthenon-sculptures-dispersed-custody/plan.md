# Plan: Parthenon Sculptures Dispersed Custody

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Reconstruction and Modern Projection
**Status:** publication-ready
**Position in chain:** second dispersed-object provenance custody case after Benin Bronzes

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle the Parthenon Sculptures when surviving fragments are
split across museums, original architectural context is damaged, title and
return claims are contested, and display strategies use originals, plaster
copies, line-of-sight, and world-museum framing?

## Core Argument

The Parthenon Sculptures are not simply present or absent. They survive as a
dispersed object group: some in the British Museum, some in Athens, some in
other museums, and much of the original decoration lost. RUINS treats this as
dispersed-object provenance custody. Current possession, claimed legal title,
loan possibility, plaster-copy display, architectural recontextualization, and
return/reunification claims are separate evidence layers.

## Required Boundaries

1. Separate object group, fragment, frieze/metope/pediment category, and
   institution.
2. Keep removal, legal acquisition, current holding, loan/request, display, and
   reunification claims apart.
3. Do not treat plaster copies, digital records, or gallery alignment as
   object-equivalent.
4. Do not decide legal title or diplomatic settlement in RUINS.
5. Hand Greek history, law, conservation, monument reconstruction, and object
   identity to sibling systems.
