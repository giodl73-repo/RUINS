# Cultural Memory: Petra

Petra's public memory is dominated by the Treasury, the Siq approach, lost-city
language, wonder lists, caravan-city romance, and film/tourism images. These
frames explain why Petra is globally legible. They are also overclaim pressure.

RUINS may discuss that memory as reception. It may not use it to prove:

- a single facade is the whole city,
- the water system is completely reconstructed,
- conservation is finished,
- tourism image is archaeological explanation,
- or Petra alone explains Nabataean history.

The key memory lesson is that iconic visibility can narrow a large archaeological
landscape into one image. RUINS keeps the image useful without letting it become
the evidence whole.
