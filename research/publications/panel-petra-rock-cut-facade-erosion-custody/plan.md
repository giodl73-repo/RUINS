# Plan: Petra Rock-Cut Facade Erosion Custody

## Scope

Use Petra as a pressure discovery for iconic rock-cut facade survival. The paper
asks how RUINS should handle a site where a famous facade image can stand in for
a much larger city, water system, erosion condition, conservation program, and
visitor landscape.

## Current Claims

- Petra is a half-built, half-carved Nabataean caravan city in a sandstone
  landscape.
- Its remains include facades, tombs, temples, religious high places, water
  channels, tunnels, diversion dams, cisterns, reservoirs, and public buildings.
- Monuments face ongoing erosion, flood risk, tourism pressure, and
  infrastructure pressure.
- Palace Tomb conservation addresses a specific facade/water-diversion problem.
- The deteriorated channel system no longer fully protects some facades from
  water erosion.

## Refused Claims

- The Treasury or any single facade is the whole city.
- Palace Tomb conservation proves Petra conservation is finished.
- Water channels prove a complete water-system reconstruction.
- Tourism image is equivalent to archaeological knowledge.
- Petra's public lost-city image explains total Nabataean history.

## Promotion Target

Fast-promote `ruins-capability:rock-cut-facade-erosion-custody` from Candidate
to Active if the scorecard confirms no current P2 findings.
