# Pompeii Cast Void Custody

**Status:** publication-ready
**Track:** Evidence and Absence
**Capability:** `ruins-capability:cast-void-identity-custody`

## Abstract

Pompeii's body casts are among the most powerful images of ancient disaster,
but their evidentiary form is easy to misread. They are not intact bodies and
not simple photographs of final moments. They are casts made from cavities left
after bodies decomposed in volcanic deposits, sometimes containing skeletal
material, later displayed, damaged, restored, scanned, copied, sampled, and
narrated. RUINS treats them as casted absences: evidence of posture and
depositional process at a source-locked scope, not automatic proof of identity,
kinship, emotion, death sequence, or population history.

## Evidence Layers

| Layer | Current claim | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Eruption process | Pumice/lapilli, collapse, pyroclastic flow, ash, and decomposition shaped the evidence. | Not a full volcanology paper. |
| Negative cavity | Body forms remained as voids after biological material decomposed. | A void is not the whole person. |
| Cast object | Fiorelli's method made a little over one hundred casts from 1863 onward. | A cast is a technical intervention. |
| Skeletal material | Some casts preserve skeletal remains that can be sampled. | Sampled individuals do not define all victims. |
| Display/restoration | Casts were displayed, placed, damaged in 1943, restored, scanned, and copied. | Display arrangement is not original social relation. |
| DNA interpretation | Recent DNA contradicts some identity and kinship assumptions. | DNA result is not a complete biography. |
| Public memory | Casts anchor tragedy, final-moment, family, and frozen-city narratives. | Memory is afterlife, not direct proof. |

## Source-Locked Synthesis

The Archaeological Park of Pompeii describes over one thousand victims found,
body positions preserved when pyroclastic flow covered them, and a little over
one hundred casts made since Fiorelli's method in 1863. It also records display,
wartime damage, partial restoration, scanning, 3D copies, and the distinction
between original casts and copies for loan.

Current Biology and the Max Planck summary add a second layer: genome-wide DNA
and strontium data from skeletal material in selected casts challenge older
interpretations based on appearance, jewelry, physical proximity, and display
story. That does not make DNA a total biography. It narrows some identity and
kinship claims while leaving many exact claims future-locked.

## What RUINS Refuses

| Overclaim | RUINS response |
|---|---|
| The casts are the bodies themselves. | They are technical casts from voids, sometimes with embedded skeletal remains. |
| Posture proves emotion or final thought. | Posture is evidence; emotion is not directly recoverable. |
| A display group proves a family. | Kinship requires separate biological and archaeological custody. |
| Jewelry proves gender. | The DNA study directly warns against this assumption. |
| DNA from a few casts describes all Pompeiians. | It is a sampled result, not a whole-city demographic ledger. |
| The casts freeze Pompeii exactly as life stopped. | They preserve a transformed trace after eruption, decay, casting, restoration, and display. |

## Method Result

Pompeii activates cast-void identity custody. The method is for cases where an
absence becomes visible through casting, impression, void filling, footprint,
trackway, scan, or display reconstruction. The rule is simple: the more human
the trace appears, the more carefully RUINS separates cavity, cast, sample,
display, scientific inference, and memory.
