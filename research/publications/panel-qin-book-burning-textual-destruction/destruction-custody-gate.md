# Destruction Custody Gate: Qin Book Burning

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST / ERRATA
**Status:** current-scope gate closed
**Date:** 2026-07-05

This gate prevents a deliberate destruction report from silently becoming a
destroyed inventory, total corpus history, exact quotation, or civilizational
closure claim.

## Current Permissions

| Claim type | Current permission |
|---|---|
| Li Si memorial as recorded by Sima Qian | Allowed as source-tradition / translation-mediated claim. |
| 213 BCE book-burning policy | Allowed as broad source-scoped deliberate textual destruction. |
| Target categories | Allowed at category level: Odes, Documents, hundred schools, non-Qin histories, and politically dangerous private texts. |
| Exemptions and survivals | Required whenever total-loss pressure appears. |
| Scholar-execution tradition | Allowed only as separate source/memory problem; not automatic fact closure. |
| Exact Chinese quotation or English wording | Refuse until source/translation custody is added. |
| Destroyed-title inventory or book counts | Refuse. |
| Total intellectual rupture or full transmission explanation | Refuse. |

## Future Exact-Claim Minimum

Before RUINS makes stronger claims, it must record:

1. exact Sima Qian passage, edition, chapter, and Chinese text;
2. translation comparison and quotation boundary;
3. modern scholarly source custody for Qin/Han textual transmission;
4. target category definitions in context;
5. separate treatment of book burning, scholar execution, archive fires, and
   later moral memory.
