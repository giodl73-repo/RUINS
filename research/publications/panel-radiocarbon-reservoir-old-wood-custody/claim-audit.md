# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Radiocarbon dates support bounded sample chronology after calibration. | Strong | Stays at sample and probability-range level. |
| Reservoir effects can offset marine and freshwater samples. | Strong | Requires local model and material custody. |
| Old wood can predate the archaeological event. | Strong | Blocks direct event inference from charcoal alone. |
| Bayesian models organize chronological uncertainty. | Strong | Priors and model structure must be explicit. |
| Calibrated range is exact event date. | Refused | Probability range is not an event. |
| SPD peak is population curve. | Refused | Demographic inference is sibling synthesis. |
