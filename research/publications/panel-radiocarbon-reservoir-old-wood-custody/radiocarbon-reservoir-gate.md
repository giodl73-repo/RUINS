# Radiocarbon Reservoir Gate

Before using a radiocarbon result as archaeological chronology, lock:

1. Sample identity, material, organism, context, collection, and lab record.
2. Pretreatment, collagen or material quality, contamination control, and error.
3. Carbon source: terrestrial, marine, freshwater, mixed diet, old wood, or bulk.
4. Calibration curve, calibration version, and reported probability range.
5. Reservoir model: marine, freshwater, hardwater, Delta-R, local offset, and
   temporal variability.
6. Bayesian model structure: priors, phases, boundaries, outlier models, SPDs,
   or wiggle matches.
7. Sibling handoffs for event, transition, population, migration, abandonment,
   collapse, diet, pottery-use, and public exact-date stories.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to bounded sample chronology rather
than event date, social process, or solved historical chronology.
