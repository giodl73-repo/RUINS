# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Radiocarbon dates require calibration and are probability distributions, not
  exact calendar events.
- Marine and freshwater reservoir effects can make samples appear older than
  comparable terrestrial samples, and offsets can vary by place and time.
- Old wood can make charcoal or timber dates predate the archaeological event.
- Bayesian chronological models, outlier models, SPDs, and wiggle matches
  structure uncertainty but require explicit priors and context custody.

Future-locked:

- Exact event date.
- Direct occupation or burial date.
- Universal reservoir correction.
- Population curve or migration proof.
- Collapse timing or solved chronology.
