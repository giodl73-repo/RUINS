# Rapa Nui Lake Cores and Collapse-Memory Custody

Rapa Nui lake-core pollen and charcoal debates are a strong RUINS trigger
because they reconstruct vegetation change, fire, erosion, and environmental
history from sedimentary proxies while carrying unusually heavy public collapse
memory. Palm-pollen decline, grass expansion, charcoal particles, diatoms,
geochemistry, and radiocarbon age models can support bounded environmental
history, but they do not directly narrate social collapse.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. A lake core is not a whole-island census,
an exact deforestation clock, a final tree witness, or a moral proof of ecocide.
It depends on catchment scale, proxy transport, preservation, erosion, age-model
uncertainty, taxon assignment, fire interpretation, and separate testing of
human activity, rats, drought, climate, agriculture, demography, and resilience.

RUINS promotes lake-core pollen charcoal custody to Core here. Lake-core proxies
can support bounded vegetation, fire, and erosion claims, but they cannot become
exact deforestation date, whole-island vegetation map, direct human overuse
proof, population collapse, warfare or famine proof, climate determinism,
last-tree narrative, moral parable, or complete Rapa Nui history without
separate custody.
