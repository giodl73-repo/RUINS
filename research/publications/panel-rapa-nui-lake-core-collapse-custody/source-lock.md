# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Rapa Nui lake and sediment studies use pollen, charcoal, sediment, and related
  proxies to reconstruct vegetation, fire, erosion, drought, and human-history
  pressure.
- Public and scholarly narratives have often tied palm decline and deforestation
  to ecocide or collapse stories.
- Multi-driver studies argue that human activity, climate, rats, erosion,
  agriculture, chronology, and resilience debates must not be collapsed into a
  single proxy explanation.
- Settlement and demographic chronology papers create pressure against simple
  last-tree or sudden-collapse timelines.

Future-locked:

- Exact deforestation date.
- Whole-island vegetation map.
- Direct human overuse proof.
- Population collapse, warfare, or famine proof.
- Climate determinism.
- Complete Rapa Nui history.
