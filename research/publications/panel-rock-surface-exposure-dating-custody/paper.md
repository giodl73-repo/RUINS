# Rock-Surface Exposure Dating Custody

Rock-surface dating methods can constrain exposure histories that archaeology
often needs but cannot see directly. Cosmogenic nuclides, lichenometry, rock
varnish, microerosion, and weathering rinds can all provide age or sequence
evidence for stone surfaces.

That evidence is not automatically the date of a human event. A 10Be age, lichen
diameter, varnish layer, microerosion rate, or weathering rind can support a
bounded surface-history claim, but it cannot by itself become exact monument
construction date, direct carving date, certain petroglyph age, final quarry
date, abandonment event, complete surface stability, path-use chronology,
landform-event proof, cultural phase date, or solved site chronology.

The controlling RUINS move is to separate sample surface, exposure event,
pre-exposure inheritance, shielding, erosion, calibration, species/substrate,
varnish formation, disturbance, date model, archaeological association, event
inference, and public age memory. Surface dating is valuable precisely because
it turns weathered rock into chronological evidence; RUINS keeps that evidence
from becoming a direct cultural timestamp without intermediate custody.

This paper promotes `ruins-capability:rock-surface-exposure-dating-custody` to
Core.
