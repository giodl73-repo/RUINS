# Claim Audit: Roman Concrete Partial Survival

**Owner:** ERRATA
**Status:** working audit

## Claim Grades

| Claim | Max grade now | Audit note |
|---|---|---|
| Some Roman concrete structures have survived for very long periods. | Observed / corroborated | Needs source-specific examples before publication use. |
| Roman concrete was made with lime, pozzolanic materials, water, and aggregates. | Corroborated | Broad formulation needs source lock and context labels. |
| Vitruvius preserves Roman concrete recipes. | Corroborated with limits | Textual witness, not total practice record. |
| Roman concrete had one lost secret ingredient. | Refuse | Evidence points to multiple materials, environments, and processes. |
| Marine Roman concrete can strengthen through seawater-driven mineral formation. | Plausible / source-specific | Needs Jackson source lock and marine-environment limit. |
| Lime clasts indicate hot mixing and self-healing potential. | Plausible / source-specific | Needs Masic 2023 source lock and mechanism boundary. |
| Pompeii construction-site evidence confirms all Roman concrete was hot-mixed. | Refuse as universalization | Pompeii can support a context-specific workflow claim. |
| Modern engineers can simply copy Roman concrete. | Refuse | Ancient materials, standards, loads, environments, and codes differ. |
| Roman concrete was always better than modern concrete. | Refuse | Category error across material systems and engineering requirements. |

## Phrases To Challenge

| Phrase | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "the secret of Roman concrete" | "one source-specific mechanism or material system" |
| "the recipe was lost" | "some practices were partially preserved textually and materially, then reinterpreted scientifically" |
| "Roman concrete heals itself" | "some sampled Roman concretes show mechanisms interpreted as self-healing under specific conditions" |
| "seawater makes Roman concrete stronger" | "marine Roman concrete may develop strengthening mineral phases through seawater interaction" |
| "we can rebuild it today" | "modern materials research can translate selected principles" |
