# Plan: Roman Concrete Partial Survival

## Question

When is Roman concrete a lost technique, a partially surviving recipe, a
surviving material system, or a modern reconstruction inspired by ancient
evidence?

## Deliverables

1. Separate ancient textual recipe, surviving structures, sampled mortar, lab
   characterization, and modern experimental reconstruction.
2. Treat "Roman concrete" as multiple material practices rather than one
   universal formula.
3. Distinguish marine concrete, wall mortar, repair mortar, and modern
   Roman-inspired concrete.
4. Refuse "secret ingredient found" and "modern concrete is inferior" claims
   unless source-specific.
5. Explain why media memory turns partial technical survival into a lost-secret
   story.

## First Pass Scope

This pass is a working paper. It names major source targets and claim
boundaries, but does not yet source-lock Vitruvius passages, mineral claims,
hot-mixing claims, or Pompeii construction-site claims.

## Out Of Scope

- Engineering instructions for making structural concrete.
- Recipe advice for construction, restoration, or conservation.
- Full Roman architectural history.
- Complete catalog of Roman concrete structures.
- Materials-safety or building-code guidance.
