# Refusal Register: Roman Concrete Partial Survival

**Owner:** ERRATA / Safety Editor
**Status:** first register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

## Refused Claims

| Claim | Refusal | Allowed substitute |
|---|---|---|
| Roman concrete had one secret ingredient. | The locked evidence involves texts, aggregates, lime, pozzolans, seawater, site workflow, and long-term reactions. | Roman concrete is a family of material practices with source-specific mechanisms. |
| Vitruvius is a complete concrete manual. | Vitruvius is a textual witness, not exhaustive evidence of all practice. | Vitruvius preserves important ancient material descriptions. |
| Marine concrete findings apply to all Roman concrete. | Marine mineral evolution depends on environment and samples. | Some marine Roman concrete shows source-specific seawater-mineral processes. |
| Hot mixing was universal across the Roman empire. | Current locked evidence is sample- and site-specific. | Some sampled mortars and the Pompeii context support hot-mixing claims. |
| Pompeii proves all Roman concrete production methods. | One in-situ worksite cannot close empire-wide practice variation. | Pompeii provides unusually strong workflow evidence for one 79 CE context. |
| Roman concrete is always superior to modern concrete. | Category error across time, material systems, codes, reinforcement, and environments. | Some Roman concretes endured exceptionally in specific contexts. |
| RUINS can publish a usable recipe. | This paper is informational and not construction guidance. | RUINS can classify evidence surfaces and source limits. |
| Modern engineers can simply copy Roman concrete. | Modern design constraints and materials differ. | Modern engineers may translate selected principles into new materials. |

## Phrase Controls

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "the Roman concrete recipe" | "a source-specific Roman concrete formulation or practice" |
| "the secret ingredient" | "one proposed mechanism or material component" |
| "Roman concrete heals itself" | "some sampled materials support a lime-clast self-healing mechanism" |
| "Vitruvius tells us how they did it" | "Vitruvius preserves one textual layer of the evidence" |
| "we can recreate it" | "modern materials research can translate selected principles" |
