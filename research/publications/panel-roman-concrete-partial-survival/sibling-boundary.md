# Sibling Boundary: Roman Concrete Partial Survival

**Owner:** Sibling Boundary Editor
**Status:** first boundary register
**Date:** 2026-07-04

## RUINS Scope

RUINS owns the evidence-boundary problem: how to distinguish partial technical
survival from lost-secret mythology, and how to grade claims made from text,
material survival, sampled mortar, laboratory model, and modern reconstruction.

## Handoffs

| Topic | Repo | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Roman architecture, urbanism, roads, aqueducts, and engineering history | MAXIM | RUINS may cite context; MAXIM owns broad historical synthesis. |
| Source PDFs, translations, page custody, and bibliography | FONTES / MUNDUS | RUINS records locked uses; archives own full source custody. |
| Named structures and sites such as Pantheon, Pompeii, Privernum, harbors | CANON | RUINS may name examples; CANON owns entity records. |
| Lime burning, pozzolan, aggregate, fuel, ceramics, labor, and supply chains | CERES | RUINS treats them as evidence surfaces, not full production history. |
| Pozzolan geography, quarry/source mapping, Bay of Naples, Pompeii | PORTO | RUINS may refer to location; PORTO owns spatial model. |
| Modern concrete engineering and building-code practice | Out of scope / future applied repo | RUINS does not provide engineering implementation. |

## Drift Controls

1. Do not turn this paper into a construction guide.
2. Do not inventory all Roman concrete structures.
3. Do not turn modern Roman-inspired concrete into ancient practice.
4. Do not flatten varied material systems into one formula.
