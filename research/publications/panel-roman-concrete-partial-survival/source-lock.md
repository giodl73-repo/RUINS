# Source Lock: Roman Concrete Partial Survival

**Owner:** PALIMPSEST
**Status:** page-audited source lock
**Date:** 2026-07-04

This source lock defines what RUINS may say about Roman concrete in this paper.
It separates ancient textual witness, surviving material, laboratory model,
archaeological workflow evidence, and modern public interpretation. A locked
source supports bounded claims only; it does not authorize construction advice.

## Locked Sources

| ID | Source | Type | Locked use | Limits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | Vitruvius, *De Architectura* / *Ten Books on Architecture* | ancient technical text, translation-mediated | Ancient textual vocabulary for lime, pozzolanic materials, wall construction, and underwater/marine work. | Not a complete field manual; not a universal recipe for all Roman concrete. |
| S2 | Jackson et al., "Phillipsite and Al-tobermorite mineral cements produced through low-temperature water-rock reactions in Roman marine concrete" (*American Mineralogist*, 2017) | marine concrete mineralogy paper | Marine concrete mineral evolution, seawater interaction, phillipsite, and Al-tobermorite claims. | Marine/environment-specific; do not universalize to all Roman concrete. |

| S4 | MIT News 2023 summary of Masic / Seymour et al. | institutional research summary | Public-language framing for lime clasts, hot mixing, and self-healing. | Secondary summary; use only after S3 for public-memory framing. |

## Paper Use Rules

1. Never present Roman concrete as one recipe.
2. Treat Vitruvius as textual evidence, not as a complete recipe manual.
3. Label concrete type and context: marine, wall, vault, repair, sample,
   experimental analog, or modern inspired.
4. Use "hot-mixing hypothesis," "sampled materials," or "Pompeii evidence"
   when discussing quicklime and lime clasts.
5. Treat self-healing as a bounded mechanism, not a magical property of all
   Roman concrete.
6. Do not provide structural, restoration, or construction instructions.
7. Treat modern green-concrete claims as afterlife / engineering translation.
8. Use [`page-audit.md`](page-audit.md) for current source-locked anchors.
