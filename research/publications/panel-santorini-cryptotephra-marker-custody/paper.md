# Santorini Cryptotephra and Chronology Custody

Santorini/Thera cryptotephra work is a strong RUINS trigger because it uses
microscopic volcanic glass and ash chemistry to connect archaeological and
environmental archives. The method is powerful precisely because tephra can act
as a marker horizon, while the Santorini eruption remains a high-pressure case
where radiocarbon, archaeological chronology, ice-core signals, and public
eruption stories compete for authority.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. A cryptotephra signal is not an exact
eruption date, a complete Bronze Age chronology, a direct destruction layer, or
a collapse cause. It depends on shard recovery, geochemical fingerprinting,
stratigraphic position, redeposition controls, archive-specific age models,
source attribution, and careful transfer of dates between archives.

RUINS promotes cryptotephra marker-horizon custody to Core here. Cryptotephra
can support bounded correlation and chronology claims, but it cannot become
exact eruption date, certain source attribution, direct destruction proof,
complete archaeological chronology, cultural phase synchronization, migration
cause, climate event, collapse explanation, or solved Santorini/Thera chronology
without separate custody.
