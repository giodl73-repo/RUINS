# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Cryptotephra is used in archaeology as a dating and correlation tool, with
  both potential and limitations across sedimentary contexts.
- Tephrochronology can provide marker horizons for synchronizing archaeological
  and palaeoenvironmental records when source, chemistry, and stratigraphy are
  controlled.
- Santorini/Thera eruption dating is a long-running chronology problem because
  radiocarbon, archaeological, and environmental archives have not collapsed
  into one uncontested date.
- Ice-core sulfate and volcanic glass correlations have been debated and cannot
  be treated as certain source attribution without geochemical custody.

Future-locked:

- Exact eruption date.
- Certain source attribution.
- Direct destruction layer.
- Complete Bronze Age chronology.
- Migration, climate, or collapse causation.
- Solved Santorini/Thera public chronology.
