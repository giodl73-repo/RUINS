# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Seikilos is a notated-performance custody case. | Strong | Museum source anchors stone, poem, notation, damaged line, sparse corpus, and transmission loss. |
| Seikilos preserves a relatively complete notated song. | Strong | Current scope can say text and notation survive together; exact performance remains separate. |
| The stele gives direct access to ancient sound. | Refused | It preserves notation, not voice, tempo, instrument, delivery, or context. |
| Modern staff transcriptions are equivalent to the ancient notation. | Refused | They are access layers and editorial presentations. |
| Recordings recover the ancient performance. | Refused | Recordings are modern interpretations. |
| The case defines ancient Greek music practice as a whole. | Refused | The comparative corpus is sparse and mostly non-stone. |
