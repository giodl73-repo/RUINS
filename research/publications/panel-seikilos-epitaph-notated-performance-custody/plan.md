# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS grade an unusually complete ancient notated song when modern
listeners can encounter it through transcriptions and recordings?

## Evidence Surfaces

- Marble/stone grave monument.
- Dedication and poem.
- Letter and symbol notation between lines.
- Damaged lower line and Euterpe reference.
- Sparse comparative corpus of ancient music notation.
- Professional notation-use context.
- Loss of continuous notation tradition into the Middle Ages.
- Modern staff transcription.
- Modern performance and recording.
- "Oldest complete song" public memory.

## Exclusions

- Full Greek music theory.
- Exact translation and philology.
- Final rhythm and tempo theory.
- Complete funerary interpretation.
- Instrument reconstruction.
- Recording or arrangement review.
