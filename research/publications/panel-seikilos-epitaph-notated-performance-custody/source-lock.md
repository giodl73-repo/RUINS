# Source Lock

Current publication claims are locked to the National Museum of Denmark page and
an accessible scholarly essay by Armand D'Angour.

## Locked Claims

- The stele is a Greek grave monument from the first century CE.
- The inscription names Seikilos and includes a hymn/poem with musical notation.
- Note signs appear between the lines as letters and symbols.
- The lower line is damaged and mentions Euterpe.
- The inscription does not tell who or what Seikilos was.
- About fifty ancient music examples are known from classical to Roman time, and
  only three are in stone.
- The Greek notation system probably developed in the third or fourth century
  BCE.
- The notation system was probably used by professional composers and chorus
  leaders, while others learned melodies by listening.
- There is no evidence that the Greek notation system survived into the Middle
  Ages.
