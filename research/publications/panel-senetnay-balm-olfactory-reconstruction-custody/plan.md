# Plan

## Question

How should RUINS grade a reconstructed ancient scent when the evidence comes
from biomolecular residue analysis of canopic jars and museum scent design?

## Evidence Surfaces

- Canopic jar residues.
- Tomb, excavation, and collection custody.
- Mummified-organ context.
- Biomolecular characterization.
- Beeswax, plant oil, fats, bitumen, Pinaceae resins, balsamic substance, and
  dammar-or-Pistacia ambiguity.
- Ingredient origin and trade-route inference.
- Modern perfumer/sensory museology reconstruction.
- Exhibition scent and public access.
- Afterlife and "scent of eternity" memory.

## Exclusions

- Exact embalming recipe.
- Chemical protocol or safety guidance.
- Full Senetnay biography.
- Full Egyptian trade-route history.
- Complete mummification ritual interpretation.
- Exhibition review.

## Promotion Target

Use Senetnay as the unlike second test for
`ruins-capability:olfactory-reconstruction-custody`, then promote the capability
to Core with a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.29 cap.
