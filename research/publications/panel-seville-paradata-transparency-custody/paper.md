# Seville, London, and Paradata Transparency Custody

The Seville Principles and London Charter are not ruins in the ordinary sense,
but they are crucial to RUINS because they define how virtual archaeology should
document the reconstruction process. The Seville Principles state that metadata
and paradata are crucial for scientific transparency, while London Charter
materials emphasize that computer-based visualization processes and outcomes
must be understandable and evaluable.

RUINS treats this as a method paper. A virtual reconstruction cannot be scored
only by visual plausibility. The paper must expose source evidence, metadata,
paradata, modelling decisions, uncertainty, visualization outcome, reuse,
preservation, and public communication.

This activates paradata transparency custody. It will become Core only after a
second concrete reconstruction project shows that the gate improves scoring in
practice.
