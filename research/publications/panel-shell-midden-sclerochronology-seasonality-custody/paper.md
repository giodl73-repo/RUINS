# Shell Midden Seasonality and Sclerochronology Custody

Shell midden sclerochronology is a strong RUINS trigger because it can recover
seasonal information from shell growth increments and isotope profiles. Oxygen
isotope sequences, shell-edge samples, and modern water baselines can support
bounded claims about shell death season, harvest season, and nearshore
conditions.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. A shell is not a complete annual
occupation calendar. It depends on specimen identity, growth preservation,
species ecology, habitat temperature and salinity, isotope calibration,
diagenesis, cooking or burning alteration, edge preservation, and whether the
assemblage sample represents a broader occupation pattern.

RUINS promotes shell sclerochronology seasonality custody to Core here. Shell
seasonality can support bounded harvest-season claims, but it cannot become an
exact harvest day, complete occupation calendar, permanent settlement proof,
whole diet, complete climate reconstruction, resource depletion proof, mobility
route, social schedule, or complete coastal lifeway without separate custody.
