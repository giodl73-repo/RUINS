# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- Shell midden sclerochronology can study climate, environmental change, human
  subsistence, resource pressure, and habitat interaction from accretionary shell
  remains.
- Oxygen isotope profiles in mollusc shells can support seasonality and season
  of collection claims when species, habitat, water baseline, and preservation
  are controlled.
- Cooking, burning, boiling, and post-depositional alteration can affect shell
  carbonate signals and require separate custody.
- Public and interpretive uses can overread seasonal shell data into occupation
  calendars, permanent settlement, diet, mobility, or whole coastal lifeways.

Future-locked:

- Exact harvest day.
- Complete annual occupation calendar.
- Permanent settlement proof.
- Whole diet or mobility route.
- Complete climate reconstruction.
- Resource depletion proof.
