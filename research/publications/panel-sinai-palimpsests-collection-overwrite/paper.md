# Sinai Palimpsests Collection Overwrite

**Status:** publication-ready
**Track:** Evidence and Absence
**Capability:** `ruins-capability:palimpsest-overwrite-custody`

## Abstract

The Sinai Palimpsests Project makes erased manuscript layers newly accessible,
but RUINS treats that access as a custody chain rather than a recovery miracle.
The project works at collection scale: St. Catherine's Monastery preserves more
than 160 known palimpsests, while the project spectrally imaged 74 palimpsests,
6,800 pages, and identified 305 erased texts. These results are powerful, but
they do not make every undertext an edition, every processed image a reading,
or every catalogue record a settled date, author, translation, or textual
history.

## Source-Locked Synthesis

The UCLA project page states that spectral imaging is used to recover erased
texts from St. Catherine's palimpsests and that the monastery holds more than
160 palimpsests with erased layers preserving unstudied texts from the 4th to
12th century. EMEL gives the completed project scale: 74 palimpsests, 6,800
pages, 305 erased texts, and an online library.

The technology page makes the custody chain explicit. Multispectral imaging
uses reflectance, fluorescence, transmission, and raking illumination. Raw
captured images are combined into processed images; automated processing may
reduce overtext contrast and enhance undertext, while manual methods may be
needed for difficult folios. Scholars then identify and describe erased texts
and catalogues make results searchable.

## What RUINS Refuses

| Overclaim | RUINS response |
|---|---|
| Spectral imaging recovers the texts completely. | Imaging creates evidence surfaces for processing and reading. |
| A processed image is an edition. | Editions require separate textual custody. |
| A catalogue record settles authorship, date, language, and text history. | Metadata is an access and description layer. |
| Discovery counts equal fully studied corpus. | Counts indicate identified erased texts at project scope. |
| Digital access makes the monastery collection fully solved. | Access opens work; it does not close it. |
| Sinai proves one simple history of hidden knowledge. | It shows layered manuscript reuse, preservation, scholarship, and reception. |

## Method Result

Sinai promotes palimpsest overwrite custody to Core. Archimedes showed a single
famous codex with unique undertexts, damage, forgeries, conservation, and XRF.
Sinai shows collection-scale recovery with fragile handling, spectral capture,
processing, language identification, cataloguing, and preliminary discovery.
Together they justify a reusable RUINS cap: erased text, images, processed
images, catalogue records, and digital libraries must not be treated as complete
recovery or final edition.
