# Micromorphology Surface Gate

Before using micromorphology as activity-area evidence, lock:

1. Block sample identity, thin section, room, surface, layer, collection, and lab record.
2. Context: floor, fill, hearth, pit, wall, collapse, trample, mixed, or unknown.
3. Microfacies, laminae, inclusions, voids, fabrics, and feature boundaries.
4. Sediment and residue sources: ash, dung, plaster, plant, charcoal, sediment,
   anthropogenic input, or natural input.
5. Depositional pathway: in-use accumulation, dumping, sweep-out, renewal,
   construction, abandonment, or redeposition.
6. Post-depositional alteration: bioturbation, compaction, dissolution, burning,
   water movement, and later disturbance.
7. Spatial sampling, controls, experimental analogues, and multiproxy support.
8. Sibling handoffs for room function, household behavior, occupation intensity,
   abandonment, cleaning, craft, storage, penning, ritual, and public domestic scenes.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to bounded microstratigraphic or
residue evidence rather than room function, daily life, or site-use proof.
