# Claim Audit

| Claim | Grade | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Use-wear/residue analysis is a RUINS custody case. | Strong | It reconstructs function from partial surface traces. |
| Edge traces can support broad worked-material inference. | Moderate | Requires raw-material, taphonomic, and experimental controls. |
| Residue morphology identifies a single worked species. | Refused | Residues can be ambiguous and transferred. |
| Polish alone gives exact tool function. | Refused | Polish must be contextualized with edge, action, and experiment. |
| Experimental replicas are direct ancient task sequences. | Refused | Analogues are bounded comparisons, not event records. |
| Blood or collagen residue proves a hunting event. | Refused | Residue, action, and event are separate layers. |
| Use-wear maps site activity or skill biography. | Refused | Assemblage and social synthesis require sibling custody. |
