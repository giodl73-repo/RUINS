# Stone Tool Use-Wear and Residue Custody

Lithic use-wear and residue analysis is a strong RUINS trigger because it turns
tiny surface changes into functional interpretation. Edge polish, striations,
rounding, micro-scarring, residues, FTIR spectra, microscopy images, and
surface-texture measurements can support worked-material and action claims when
they are compared with experimental replicas and reference collections.

The evidence is useful but fragile. Residues can be ambiguous, surfaces are
heterogeneous, cleaning can remove or move evidence, taphonomy can mimic use,
and experimental analogues are not direct ancient events.

RUINS promotes lithic use-wear residue custody to Core here. Use-wear and
residue traces can support bounded functional inference, but they cannot become
exact tool function, single worked species, complete task sequence, certain
hafting system, hunting event, full diet, craft specialization, skill biography,
or site activity map without separate custody.
