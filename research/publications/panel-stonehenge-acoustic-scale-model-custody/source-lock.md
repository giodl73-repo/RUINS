Current publication claims are locked to Salford and Journal of Archaeological

## Locked Claims

- Salford built and tested a 1:12 acoustic scale model of Stonehenge.
- The model represented all 157 stones in a reconstructed 2200 BC layout.
- The model was used to determine how sound would have been altered.
- The scholarly paper quantifies Late Neolithic and early Bronze Age acoustics.
- Reported effects include a mid-frequency reverberation time around 0.64
  seconds and speech amplification around 4.3 dB.
- The results support inference about speech and musical sounds.
