# Source Lock

## Current Lock

Current publication claims are locked to British Museum and National Trust pages.
The paper may say:

- Sutton Hoo preserved the imprint of a decayed ship and a central chamber of
  treasures.
- The ship was about 27 m / 90 ft long.
- The oak ship no longer exists and rotted away in acidic soil.
- The body did not survive, but phosphate evidence indicates a body once lay
  there.
- The identity of the person buried there cannot be named for certain.
- The Royal Burial Ground has eroded and robbed mounds, while the Great Ship
  Burial escaped major robbing.
- The treasure is displayed at the British Museum.

## Locked Out

- Complete original ship reconstruction.
- Named identity of the buried person.
- Complete biography or ritual script.
- Exact rivet counts or plank plan.
- Complete object catalogue.
- Beowulf or royal-memory comparison as direct evidence.
