# Takarkori Pottery Lipids and Foodway Custody

The Takarkori/Libyan Sahara lipid-residue papers are a strong RUINS trigger
because they reconstruct early dairy and food-processing practices from
absorbed residues in pottery rather than from complete meals, recipes, or
direct diet records. The core evidence is chemical: fatty acids, lipid
biomarkers, and stable carbon isotope values extracted from ceramic sherds.

The evidence is powerful but bounded. A pottery lipid signal is not an exact
meal, a complete recipe, a whole herd economy, or direct proof of an individual's
diet. It depends on vessel context, residue location, preservation, extraction,
contamination control, biomarker assignment, isotopic reference ranges, vessel
reuse, and comparison with animal, plant, ceramic, and landscape evidence.

RUINS promotes absorbed ceramic lipid residue custody to Core here. Ceramic
lipids can support bounded commodity and vessel-use claims, but they cannot
become exact meals, complete recipes, direct individual diet, domestication
proof, ethnic identity, trade route, climate proxy, vessel biography, or
complete foodway without separate custody.
