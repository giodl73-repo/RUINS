# Source Lock

Locked claims:

- The Nature Takarkori paper reports lipid-residue evidence for dairying in
  pottery from the Libyan Sahara.
- The public framing treats the work as direct evidence for early dairying in
  Saharan Africa, creating first-dairying memory pressure.
- Archaeological lipid-residue method literature treats biomarkers and isotope
  values as interpreted chemical evidence requiring preservation, contamination,
  reference, and context controls.
- Comparative vessel-lipid work shows dairy-fat assignments can be made from
  pottery while still needing vessel-use and diet-scale custody.

Future-locked:

- Exact meal or complete recipe.
- Direct individual diet.
- Whole herd economy.
- Domestication proof from pottery alone.
- Ethnic identity or trade route.
- Climate proxy or complete foodway history.
