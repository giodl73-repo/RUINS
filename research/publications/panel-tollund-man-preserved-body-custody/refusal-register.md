# Refusal Register: Tollund Man Preserved Body Custody

**Owner:** ERRATA
**Status:** publication-ready register

| Refused claim | Why refused | Safer wording |
|---|---|---|
| Tollund Man's face tells us who he was. | The preserved face is evidence of survival and display, not complete identity. | Preserved facial appearance. |
| Hanging means sacrifice. | Forensic cause and ritual motive are separate. | Hanged; motive remains gated. |
| The meal was a ritual meal. | The evidence allows possible relation, not certainty. | Last meal with possible ritual relevance. |
| The displayed remains equal the original find. | Excavation, autopsy, conservation, and head preservation changed the evidence surface. | Conserved display state. |
| Tollund Man explains all bog bodies. | Bog bodies vary by context, preservation, and interpretation. | Tollund as one bog-body case. |
| The museum encounter is direct access to the person. | It is mediated by preservation, conservation, display, and memory. | Face-to-face public memory. |
