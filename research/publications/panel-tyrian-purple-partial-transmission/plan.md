# Plan: Tyrian Purple Partial Transmission

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Lost Techniques and Broken Transmission
**Status:** publication-ready
**Position in chain:** second partial-technical-transmission case after Roman concrete

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle an ancient technique where shell heaps, pigment
residues, dyed textiles, ancient descriptions, chemical analysis, and modern
museum summaries survive, but no complete operational recipe or production
system survives?

## Core Argument

Tyrian or murex purple is not a simple lost-recipe case. It is a partial
technical transmission case with several surviving surfaces: mollusk shells,
pigment residues, dye molecules on textiles, workshop deposits, ancient textual
descriptions, public royal-purple memory, and modern analytical experiments.
Those surfaces can prove technique fragments and overclaim pressures. They
cannot by themselves reconstruct an exact ancient recipe, universal production
method, industrial scale, social rule, or modern production guide.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not turn Pliny/museum process summaries into a complete recipe.
2. Do not let shell heaps alone prove dye production without context and
   analysis.
3. Do not let HPLC pigment identification prove workshop process sequence.
4. Do not let royal/imperial memory prove all purple textiles had the same
   status or source.
5. Do not provide modern production, harvesting, or dye-manufacturing guidance.
