# Cultural Memory: Uffington

Uffington's public life is shaped by White Horse Hill, Dragon Hill, St George
tradition, scouring, local care, national-icon language, and the striking
long-distance image of the horse. These memory layers matter because they help
explain why the figure has been maintained and restored.

RUINS may discuss that afterlife. It may not use it to prove:

- the complete original outline,
- all ancient intended viewpoints,
- final exact date,
- maker identity,
- ancient symbolic meaning,
- or the meaning of British hill figures as a group.

The key cultural-memory lesson is that care can preserve a surface mark while
also changing the evidence. Scouring and restoration are not noise; they are
part of the custody chain that makes the image visible today.
