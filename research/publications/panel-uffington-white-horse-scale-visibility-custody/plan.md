# Plan: Uffington White Horse Scale Visibility Custody

## Scope

Use Uffington as the second case for scale-mediated surface visibility after
Nasca. The paper tests whether RUINS can promote the capability to Core across a
desert geoglyph field and a chalk hill figure maintained by scouring and modern
restoration.

## Current Claims

- Uffington White Horse is a large chalk-cut hill figure on Whitehorse Hill.
- The figure is visible from a distance and sits within a wider ancient
  landscape.
- Historic England records changes to the figure, 1936 consolidation, and
  wartime covering.
- English Heritage and National Trust source the recent drone survey,
  restoration, shrinkage, and OSL dating frame.
- Maintenance and restoration are part of the survival chain, not transparent
  access to complete original form.

## Refused Claims

- Current outline is the complete original shape.
- Restoration recovers the whole ancient figure without remainder.
- Distance visibility proves all ancient intended viewing.
- OSL range is a final exact date.
- Dragon Hill or St George tradition explains the ancient figure.
- Uffington alone explains British prehistoric hill figures.

## Promotion Target

Promote `ruins-capability:scale-mediated-surface-visibility-custody` from Active
to Core by adding a cross-case checklist and rubric v1.20 cap.
