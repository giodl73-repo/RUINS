# Source Lock: Uffington

## Locked For Current Scope

| Source | Locked claim |
|---|---|
| Historic England scheduled monument record | Uffington White Horse is a chalk-cut hill figure on a west-facing slope, visible from several miles, about 111 metres long and 40 metres high. |
| Historic England scheduled monument record | Geophysical survey indicates alteration in the head feature; the figure was consolidated in 1936 and covered during the Second World War. |
| National Trust White Horse Hill page | The site is presented as an internationally renowned Bronze Age chalk figure within a wider ancient landscape. |
| English Heritage White Horse Project | Drone survey and comparison with earlier drawings and aerial photographs showed shrinkage in the chalk lines. |
| English Heritage White Horse Project | Earlier OSL samples indicated an origin between 1380 and 550 BC, with new sampling planned to refine dating. |
| National Trust Our Work page | Recent work restored narrowed areas where grass and topsoil had encroached, especially at the head and neck. |
