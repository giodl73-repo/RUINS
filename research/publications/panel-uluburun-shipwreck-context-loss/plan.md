# Plan: Uluburun Shipwreck Context Loss

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Shipwrecks and Context Loss
**Status:** activation-ready working paper
**Position in chain:** first shipwreck context-loss case after capability roadmap

## Research Question

How should RUINS handle a shipwreck where cargo, hull traces, excavation
records, conservation, museum display, source analyses, and reconstructed trade
networks survive unevenly?

## Core Argument

Uluburun is a RUINS case because the wreck preserves an extraordinary cargo
assemblage while damaging the operating context that made that cargo meaningful:
route, destination, owners, crew identity, exchange purpose, and political
setting. The wreck is not a Bronze Age trade map by itself. RUINS should
separate object survival, cargo assemblage, wreck-site excavation, conservation,
scientific analysis, route inference, exchange-network interpretation, and
public treasure/globalization memory.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not turn cargo diversity into a complete route map.
2. Do not treat the wreck as proof of one settled destination or owner.
3. Do not treat cargo objects as representative of all Late Bronze Age trade.
4. Do not treat museum display or replica/reconstruction as the ship's original
   operating context.
5. Do not publish site-location, diving, recovery, or collecting guidance.
