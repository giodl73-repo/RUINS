# Plan: Vinland Saga Site Crosscheck

**Module:** RUINS
**Track:** Evidence and Absence
**Status:** stable working paper
**Position in chain:** post-Palmyra case testing saga-memory crosscheck

## Research Question

How should RUINS relate the Norse sagas of Vinland to the archaeological site at
L'Anse aux Meadows without collapsing literary memory, place names, route
claims, and site evidence into one proof?

## Core Argument

L'Anse aux Meadows gives direct archaeological evidence for Norse presence in
North America. The Vinland sagas give literary memory of westward voyages,
written down later. These two surfaces correspond in important ways, but they
are not the same evidence layer. RUINS should let archaeology confirm a Norse
site and let saga tradition remain a meaningful memory source without turning
the site into every saga place, every named voyage, or a full route map.

## Required Boundaries

1. Do not identify L'Anse aux Meadows as Vinland without source-specific
   qualification.
2. Do not treat saga narrative as an archaeological site report.
3. Do not use the AD 1021 date as proof of all saga events.
4. Do not turn one confirmed site into a complete Norse North America map.
5. Do not use Norse evidence to erase Indigenous presence or histories.
