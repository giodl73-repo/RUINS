# VR Firelight and Paleolithic Art Reconstruction

Wisher's 2023 Journal of Archaeological Science: Reports paper presents VR
modelling as a way to simulate dynamic firelight for Paleolithic art analysis,
interpretation, and presentation. The method uses Unity and digital models
captured through whitelight scanning and photogrammetry, applied to Las Monedas
parietal art and Montastruc portable plaquettes.

For RUINS this is the unlike test for light-mediated custody. The first case
used live experimental torches, lamps, and fireplaces. This one uses digital
firelight, scanned surfaces, modelled viewing positions, and presentation
interfaces. Both cases need the same refusal: reconstructed light is not direct
ancient perception.

The VR work is valuable because it can test sensory hypotheses without damaging
fragile sites or objects. Its claims remain strongest when framed as modelled
visibility under declared assumptions, not as recovery of original viewer
experience or final image meaning.
