# Source Lock

## Current Lock

Current publication claims are locked to UNESCO and Warsaw Heritage
Interpretation Centre pages. The paper may say:

- Warsaw's Old Town was deliberately and extensively destroyed during World War
  II.
- Reconstruction was a coherent project devised by the Warsaw Reconstruction
  Office in 1945-1951.
- The project used surviving structures, street networks, archival documents,
  iconography, inventories, and expert knowledge.
- The target appearance was late 18th century where documentation supported it.
- Some buildings were deliberately not rebuilt.
- Interior layouts and flats were adapted to contemporary standards.
- Public-use interiors were sometimes recreated.
- The BOS Archive records destruction and reconstruction and was recognized by
  UNESCO Memory of the World.

## Locked Out

- Complete original fabric.
- Exact historical interiors for every building.
- Unbroken urban continuity.
- Total national recovery.
- Final conservation doctrine.
- Building-level reconstruction accuracy claims.
