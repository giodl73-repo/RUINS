# Bone-Modification Gate

Before using bone modification as behavior evidence, lock:

1. Specimen identity, taxon, skeletal element, context, collection, and publication.
2. Surface preservation, weathering, abrasion, excavation damage, and visibility.
3. Mark type, morphology, location, orientation, depth, cross-section, and count.
4. Actor and process alternatives: human, carnivore, trampling, fire, sediment,
   knapping, handling, or unknown.
5. Timing: peri-mortem, post-depositional, excavation, curation, or modern.
6. Assemblage controls: recovery, screen size, fragmentation, NISP/MNI, element
   frequency, age-at-death, and mortality profile.
7. Experimental, ethnoarchaeological, microscopic, or 3D comparison.
8. Sibling handoffs for diet, hunting, scavenging, ritual, site function, social
   organization, human evolution, and public meat-eating stories.

If any layer is missing, downgrade the claim to bounded taphonomic signal
rather than behavior, subsistence, ritual, or site-function proof.
